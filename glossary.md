# Glossary

Canonical terms, abbreviations and naming conventions for the BEBA monograph.

## Core Theory Terms

| Term | Canonical use | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| BEBA | Brand-Enabled Behaviour Architecture | Multi-layer architecture linking signals, brand equity, belief formation, intention and resource behaviour. |
| Nonprofit Brand Equity | Nonprofit Brand Equity | Brand equity in nonprofit decision contexts, treated as signal architecture rather than only marketing asset. |
| Epistemic Uncertainty | Epistemic uncertainty | Uncertainty about organisational quality, integrity, impact and future behaviour. |
| Information Asymmetry | Information asymmetry | Situation where donors cannot fully observe what organisations know or do. |
| Credence Goods | Credence goods | Goods or services whose quality remains hard to evaluate even after use; key analogy for charitable giving. |
| Signalling Theory | Signalling Theory | Theoretical basis for how observable cues communicate unobservable qualities. |
| Donation Enactment | Donation enactment | Translation of donation intention into actual giving behaviour. |
| Brand Equity Maturity | Brand Equity Maturity | Stage or maturity logic of brand-equity components, potentially Kano-inspired. |

## Behavioural Models

| Abbreviation | Meaning | Role |
|---|---|---|
| TPB | Theory of Planned Behaviour | Explains intention formation via attitude, norms and perceived behavioural control. |
| IBM | Integrated Behavioural Model | Extends behavioural explanation through knowledge, salience, habit and constraints. |
