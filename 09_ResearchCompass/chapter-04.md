# Research Compass - Chapter 4

## Chapter

Towards BEBA

## Scientific Question

How can Signalling Theory, Brand Equity, TPB and IBM be integrated into a theory of charitable behaviour under uncertainty?

## Purpose

This chapter develops BEBA as the central theoretical architecture of the dissertation.

## Theoretical Landscape

- Signalling Theory.
- Brand equity.
- Theory of Planned Behaviour.
- Integrated Behavioural Model.
- Charitable Triad.
- Institutional legitimacy.
- Intention-behaviour gap.

## Core Argument

Brand equity is not the endpoint of nonprofit branding. It is a signal layer that shapes beliefs, intentions and behavioural enactment. BEBA therefore moves from brand measurement to behavioural explanation.

## Proposed BEBA Layers

1. Institutional context.
2. Signal layer.
3. Brand equity layer.
4. Belief formation layer.
5. Enactment layer.
6. Resource layer.

## Literature Review Tasks

1. Review Signalling Theory in marketing and organisational contexts.
2. Review TPB applications in donation and volunteering.
3. Review IBM and behaviour enactment conditions.
4. Review intention-behaviour gap in prosocial and donation contexts.
5. Identify theories that challenge BEBA: habit, moral identity, social identity, institutional isomorphism, affective giving.

## Falsifiability and Boundary Conditions

- BEBA would be weakened if brand equity has no effect beyond general trust or social norms.
- BEBA would be weakened if intention fully explains behaviour without enactment conditions.
- BEBA may vary by donor type, organisation type and resource type.

## Output Expected

A theory chapter that formulates BEBA as an architecture, not as a single SEM specification.
