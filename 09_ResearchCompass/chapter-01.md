# Research Compass - Chapter 1

## Chapter

Introduction and Research Programme

## Scientific Question

Why should nonprofit brand equity be understood not merely as a marketing construct, but as a mechanism that reduces uncertainty in philanthropic decision-making?

## Purpose of the Chapter

This chapter establishes the dissertation as a coherent research programme. It introduces charitable giving as a decision under uncertainty, positions nonprofit brands as signals, and motivates the development of BEBA as a behavioural architecture.

## Theoretical Landscape

- Decision-making under uncertainty.
- Information asymmetry.
- Credence goods.
- Signalling Theory.
- Nonprofit trust and legitimacy.
- Brand equity and donor behaviour.
- Behavioural theories of intention and enactment.

## Core Argument

The dissertation does not primarily ask whether brands influence donations. It asks through which mechanisms brands become behaviourally relevant when donors cannot fully observe organisational impact, integrity or effectiveness.

## Falsifiability and Boundary Conditions

- Some giving is habitual or normatively driven rather than brand-driven.
- Some organisations may benefit from sectoral trust without strong brand equity.
- Strong awareness can become harmful when negative information is salient.
- Donors may rely on social ties rather than organisational brand signals.

## Output Expected

A chapter opening that begins with the problem of philanthropic decisions under uncertainty and only then introduces brand equity as a theoretical solution.
