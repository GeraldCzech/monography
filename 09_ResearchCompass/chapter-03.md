# Research Compass - Chapter 3

## Chapter

The Evolution of Brand Equity

## Scientific Question

How did brand equity evolve from a commercial marketing concept into a possible signal and trust mechanism for nonprofit organisations?

## Purpose

This chapter reconstructs the conceptual history of brand equity and prepares the transition from existing measurement architectures to BEBA.

## Theoretical Landscape

- Brand equity as asset.
- Customer-based brand equity.
- Brand knowledge.
- Brand value chain.
- Relationship marketing.
- Brand credibility.
- Nonprofit brand equity.

## Existing Nonprofit Models

- Faircloth: awareness, image, personality and resource-provider support.
- Boenigk and Becker: trust and commitment as relational nonprofit brand equity.
- Rios Romero et al.: differentiated NGO donor-based brand equity dimensions.

## Core Argument

Existing nonprofit brand-equity models are not merely alternative scales. They represent competing theoretical answers to the question of what nonprofit brands actually do: create awareness, structure perceptions, generate trust, build relationships or enable behaviour.

## Literature Review Tasks

1. Review Aaker and Keller traditions.
2. Review brand value chain and brand knowledge.
3. Review relationship marketing and brand relationships.
4. Review nonprofit brand equity.
5. Compare Faircloth, Boenigk and Becker, and Rios Romero et al. as theoretical architectures.

## Boundary Conditions

- Commercial brand equity cannot be transferred to nonprofit settings without adaptation.
- Nonprofit brands may signal trust and legitimacy more strongly than differentiation.
- Highly known organisations may still lack donor commitment.

## Link to Next Chapter

The chapter ends by showing that measurement architectures alone are insufficient; a behavioural architecture is needed.
