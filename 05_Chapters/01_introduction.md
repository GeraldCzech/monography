# Chapter 1 - Introduction and Research Programme

## Scientific Question

Why should nonprofit brand equity be understood not merely as a marketing construct, but as a mechanism for reducing uncertainty in philanthropic decision-making?

## Working Opening Logic

1. Charitable giving differs from ordinary consumption.
2. Donors often cannot observe the actual quality or impact of nonprofit work.
3. This creates epistemic uncertainty and information asymmetry.
4. Under such conditions, signals become behaviourally relevant.
5. Brands are institutionalised signal systems.
6. Nonprofit brand equity captures the strength and structure of these signals.
7. BEBA explains how these signals become intentions, behaviour and resources.

## Draft Thesis

This dissertation does not ask only whether nonprofit brands influence donations. It asks how brands become behaviourally relevant when donors must act under uncertainty about organisational effectiveness, integrity and impact.
