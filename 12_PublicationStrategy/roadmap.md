# Publication Roadmap

## Purpose

The monograph is written as a coherent dissertation, but several chapters are structured so that they can be converted into journal articles.

## Planned and Possible Spin-Offs

| ID | Working Title | Type | Monograph Anchor | Status | Core Contribution |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Triple Replication of Nonprofit Brand Equity Models | Empirical replication | Chapter 8 | Working paper basis | Compares Faircloth, Boenigk and Becker, and Rios Romero et al. in the Austrian NPO context. |
| P2 | Evidence-Based Charity Brand Dashboards | Applied / empirical | Chapter 9 | Submitted / article basis | Translates psychometric and SEM evidence into actionable dashboard constructs. |
| P3 | BEBA: A Brand-Enabled Behaviour Architecture for Charitable Giving | Theory paper | Chapter 4 | Planned | Develops the behavioural architecture linking signals, brand equity, TPB and IBM. |
| P4 | Competing Structural Models of Nonprofit Brand Equity | Methods / SEM paper | Chapters 7 and 10 | Planned | Uses competing SEM architectures as theory comparison rather than simple model testing. |
| P5 | The Intention-Behaviour Gap in Charitable Giving | Empirical BEBA paper | Chapter 10 | Planned | Tests how brand equity and enactment conditions translate intention into behaviour. |
| P6 | Brand Equity Maturity: A Kano-Inspired Stage Model | Conceptual paper | Chapter 11 | Planned | Develops a staged view of awareness, image, trust, commitment, habit and advocacy. |
| P7 | Evidence-Based Nonprofit Branding | Synthesis paper | Chapter 12 | Optional | Integrates replication, dashboard evidence and BEBA into a managerial research agenda. |

## Publication Logic

The dissertation remains the source text. Papers may condense, specialise or translate parts of the monograph, but they should not introduce inconsistent construct definitions without a DecisionLog or ADR entry.

## Consistency Rules

- Each paper must reference the construct versions used.
- Each paper must identify which pipeline outputs support its empirical claims.
- Any deviation from monograph terminology must be listed in `glossary.md`.
- Empirical coefficients, sample sizes and fit statistics must come from checked Quarto or pipeline outputs.
