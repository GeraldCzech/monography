# BEBA Relationship Model v1

Status: canonical working architecture

## Purpose

Scientific knowledge emerges from relationships between objects. This document defines the controlled relationship vocabulary used in the BEBA research system.

Relationships should be directional, explicit, and semantically meaningful.

## Hierarchical Relationships

- contains
- belongs_to
- part_of
- extends
- specialises
- generalises

## Scientific Relationships

- explains
- supports
- contradicts
- refines
- competes_with
- depends_on
- is_required_for
- is_independent_of

## BEBA-Specific Relationships

- belongs_to_layer
- connects_layer
- crosses_layer

- reduces_uncertainty_of
- translates_into_behaviour
- enables_resource_mobilisation

## Constraints

1. Every relationship should have a direction.
2. Relationships should connect defined objects, not vague prose.
3. Relationships should be supported by evidence, theory, or explicit reasoning.
4. Competing relationships may coexist if status and confidence are documented.
5. Relationships generated by AI are candidates until reviewed.
