# Epistemic Uncertainty

## Definition

Epistemic uncertainty refers to the donor's limited ability to know whether a nonprofit organisation is competent, trustworthy, effective and likely to create the intended impact.

## Role in BEBA

Epistemic uncertainty is the foundational problem that makes signals, trust, reputation and brands theoretically relevant. It motivates the entire BEBA architecture.

## Core Claims

### EU-001

Claim: Donors cannot fully observe organisational quality, integrity or impact before donating.

Status: active

Confidence: medium

BEBA relevance: foundational problem.

### EU-002

Claim: Epistemic uncertainty creates a need for observable signals.

Status: active

Confidence: medium

BEBA relevance: links Epistemic Layer to Signal Layer.

## Boundary Conditions

- Some donors do not seek to reduce uncertainty cognitively.
- Emotional, moral or habitual giving may bypass detailed evaluation.
- Institutional trust may reduce perceived uncertainty.

## Operationalisation

Currently conceptual. Potential links include perceived transparency, perceived effectiveness, trust, perceived impact, donor confidence, and perceived accountability.

## Relationship Statements

- Epistemic Uncertainty motivates Signal Search.
- Epistemic Uncertainty is reduced by Trust.
- Epistemic Uncertainty is reduced by credible Brand Signals.
- Epistemic Uncertainty forms the foundation for BEBA.
