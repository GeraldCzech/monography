**Brand familiarity moderates the customer satisfaction formation process and its subsequent effect on behavioral intentions.**

*Strength: moderate*

*Concepts: Brand Familiarity, Customer Satisfaction, Behavioral Intentions, Brand-Enabled Behaviour Architecture*

**Limitations:** The evidence is based on a longitudinal survey and structural equation modeling in a restaurant setting, establishing correlational rather than causal evidence.

**Notes:** This finding is directly relevant to BEBA because it suggests that familiarity changes the behavioural architecture linking experience evaluation to future behaviour.
