**A nonprofit retaining long-term decision-making authority over beneficiaries, substituting its judgment for theirs, is an act of paternalism.**

*Strength: moderate*

*Concepts: Paternalism, Power, Justice*

**Limitations:** Most relevant in long-term, dependent relationships between funders and beneficiaries.

**Notes:** This defines a key risk for nonprofit brands, where effectiveness could be perceived as an illegitimate exercise of power, undermining trust.
