**Gregory, Ngo, and Miller develop and validate a dual-process model in which new donor brand choice intention is influenced by both brand salience and brand attitude.**

*Strength: moderate*

*Concepts: Brand Choice Intention, Brand Salience, Brand Attitude, Dual-Process Decision-Making, Donor Behaviour*

**Limitations:** The model is tested for new first-time donors in an unsolicited giving context involving international aid and disaster relief.

**Notes:** The finding is directly relevant to BEBA because it specifies heuristic and evaluative pathways through which nonprofit brands may become behaviourally effective.
