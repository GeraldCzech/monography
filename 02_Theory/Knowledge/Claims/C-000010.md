## Statement

The effectiveness and explanatory sufficiency of warm-glow models may vary across cultural and religious contexts.

## Evidence

The synthesis states that standard impure altruism models may be incomplete in contexts with strong moral or religious norms, where faith-based obligations or community duties may also shape giving.

Supported in the synthesis by: Fraser, 2018; Fraser, 2022.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should not universalize warm glow as the sole or dominant donation mechanism. In some contexts, obligation, religious duty, community identity, or moral norms may override or complement private emotional utility.

## Notes

The synthesis identifies cross-cultural variation as a major research gap.
