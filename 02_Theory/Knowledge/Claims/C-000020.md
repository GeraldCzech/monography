## Statement

In cause-related marketing, perceived skepticism can weaken or negate the warm glow effect of a campaign.

## Evidence

Chaabouni et al. (2020) are cited as showing that large publicized donation sizes can trigger skepticism, which may reduce warm glow and purchase intention.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should treat brand authenticity and trust as safeguards for behaviour activation. Nonprofit brands and partner campaigns may need transparency and credibility cues to prevent skepticism from undermining donor or consumer motivation.

## Notes

Emerging or context-dependent claim. The review identifies a need for further lab experiments on skepticism and cooling effects.
