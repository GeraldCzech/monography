## Statement

Most research on the intention-behavior gap uses between-person designs, which may not capture the dynamic, within-person fluctuations of intentions and volitional control.

## Implications for BEBA

This suggests that BEBA's focus on behavior under uncertainty should be investigated using dynamic, within-person methodologies.

## Notes

A major identified research gap is the need to move from static to dynamic models.
