## Statement

An individual’s donation to a nonprofit can be motivated by both concern for the public good or recipient and private utility derived from the act of giving itself.

## Evidence

Andreoni (1990) theorizes “impure altruism,” in which charitable giving reflects both pure altruism and warm glow utility.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should not model nonprofit giving as purely recipient-oriented. Nonprofit brands may become behaviourally effective by enabling both impact-related meaning and donor-experienced psychological reward.

Established claim in the reviewed literature.
