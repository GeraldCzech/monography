## Statement

Warm glow becomes theoretically relevant under conditions where donors cannot easily verify charitable impact and therefore derive utility from the act of giving rather than from confirmed outcomes.

## Evidence

Andreoni (1990); Crumpler & Grossman (2008); BEBA synthesis interpretation

## Implications for BEBA

Warm glow is relevant to BEBA’s core problem of charitable behaviour under uncertainty because it offers an affective substitute for verified impact knowledge.

## Notes

The claim is theoretically grounded but should be flagged as BEBA integration rather than direct literature consensus.
