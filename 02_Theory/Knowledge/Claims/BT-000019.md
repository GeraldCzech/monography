## Statement

The perceived typicality of a nonprofit brand is a stronger and more consistent predictor of the intention to donate time than of the intention to donate money.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim reinforces the need for distinct behavioural architectures for different types of supportive behaviour (time vs. money).

## Notes

The study found brand typicality to be a crucial predictor for time donations across both charities examined.
