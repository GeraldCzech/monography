## Statement

The effect of perceived brand differentiation on actual donation behaviour is mediated by the individual's perceived efficacy of the nonprofit.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim proposes a specific psychological mechanism (perceived efficacy) that explains *how* brand architecture translates into behaviour, adding explanatory depth to the BEBA model.

## Notes

This is a theoretically proposed mechanism based on analogy and related literature; it has not been empirically tested as a mediator for the brand differentiation-behaviour link.
