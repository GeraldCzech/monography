## Statement

Social-image concerns influence the amount donated more strongly than the initial decision to donate.

## Evidence

The synthesis states that social-image concerns are more influential on donation amount, while self-image concerns are more influential on participation.

Supported in the synthesis by: Grossman & Levy, 2024.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA can distinguish between acquisition mechanisms and amplification mechanisms. Public recognition, donor circles, badges, or social sharing may be more relevant for increasing gift size than for initiating first-time giving.

## Notes

Should be kept distinct from the broader claim that observability affects giving.
