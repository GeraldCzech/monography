## Statement

The influence of performance-expectation disconfirmation on satisfaction is stronger for individuals with low brand familiarity than for individuals with high brand familiarity.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim identifies low familiarity as a higher-uncertainty condition in which behavioural effectiveness depends more on expectation management and experiential learning.

## Notes

The finding may be most applicable to experience-dominant services.
