## Statement

Fundraising appeals that emphasize the donor’s positive emotional or self-benefit can increase donation outcomes compared with appeals focused solely on beneficiary benefit.

## Evidence

The synthesis reports that marketing frames emphasizing self-benefit significantly enhance warm glow and donation outcomes, and that self-benefit appeals can outperform recipient-benefit-only appeals.

Supported in the synthesis by: List et al., 2021.

## Implications for BEBA

Nonprofit brands may activate giving not only by increasing perceived impact but also by framing donation as emotionally meaningful for the donor. This supports a brand-as-experience-architecture interpretation.

## Notes

Should not be interpreted as implying that recipient-benefit appeals are ineffective in general.
