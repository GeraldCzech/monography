## Statement

Individuals may donate to a cause even when their contribution has no marginal effect on the total funds received by the cause.

## Evidence

Crumpler and Grossman (2008) provide experimental evidence using crowd-out designs showing that individuals continue to donate even when their donation does not increase the charity’s total funding.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should account for giving behaviour that is not fully explained by perceived instrumental impact. Brand-enabled behaviour may depend on the donor’s experience of acting generously, not only on outcome efficacy.

## Notes

Established empirical claim in the reviewed literature.
