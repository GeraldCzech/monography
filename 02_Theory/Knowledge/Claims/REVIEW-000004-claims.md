# Established Candidate Claims

## Claim WG-01

## Concept

Warm glow giving

## Statement

Prosocial behaviour can be partially motivated by private, non-instrumental utility derived from the act of giving itself, independent of the donation’s marginal impact on the public good.

## BEBA Layer

Behavioural mechanism / donor-side utility

## Relationship Type

motivates

## Evidence

Andreoni (1990); Crumpler & Grossman (2008); Bianchi et al. (2023)

## Confidence

High

## Status

candidate

## Implications for BEBA

Warm glow can function as a core behavioural mechanism through which nonprofit brands make giving behaviour personally rewarding, even when impact is uncertain, distant, or difficult for donors to verify.

## Notes

This is the central warm-glow claim derived from impure altruism theory and crowd-out evidence.

## Claim WG-02

## Concept

Impure altruism

## Statement

Donor behaviour is often better explained by impure altruism, which combines concern for beneficiary welfare with private satisfaction from giving, than by pure altruism alone.

## BEBA Layer

Motivational architecture

## Relationship Type

explains

## Evidence

Andreoni (1990); Wilhelm et al. (2014)

## Confidence

High

## Status

candidate

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should not model nonprofit support only as impact-oriented behaviour. It should include donor-side psychological rewards as part of the behaviour architecture.

## Notes

The claim is supported in the review by reference to incomplete crowd-out of private donations by public funding.

## Relationship Type

## Evidence

## Confidence

Medium

## Status
