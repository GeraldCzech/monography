## Statement

Perceived operational efficiency does not have a significant positive effect on giving intention or giving behavior.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim introduces the 'efficiency paradox', suggesting BEBA must not assume a simple positive relationship between efficiency perceptions and giving.

## Notes

The study reported a negative, though not statistically significant, coefficient, challenging the common assumption that donors always prefer higher efficiency.
