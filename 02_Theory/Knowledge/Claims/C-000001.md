## Statement

Donors derive private, non-pecuniary utility from the act of giving itself, independent of the marginal impact of their donation on the charitable outcome.

## Evidence

Andreoni’s theory of impure altruism argues that donor utility includes both concern for public-good provision and utility from one’s own contribution. Crowd-out experiments show that individuals continue to donate even when their contribution has no marginal effect on the charity’s total funds, indicating that the act of giving has intrinsic value for the donor.

Supported in the synthesis by: Andreoni, 1989, 1990; Crumpler & Grossman, 2008.

## Implications for BEBA

Warm glow can be treated as a core behavioural mechanism through which nonprofit brands become behaviourally effective. The brand does not only communicate impact; it can help make the emotional reward of giving salient and credible.
