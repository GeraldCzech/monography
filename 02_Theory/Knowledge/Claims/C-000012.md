## Statement

Warm glow can provide donors with a relatively certain private emotional benefit when the public impact of a donation is uncertain.

## Evidence

The synthesis argues that warm glow provides a certain private benefit in exchange for contributing to an uncertain public good. This is an interpretation derived from the reviewed warm glow literature and the BEBA framing.

Supported indirectly by the synthesis of: Andreoni, 1990; Crumpler & Grossman, 2008; List et al., 2021.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim is directly relevant to the current BEBA research question: “How do nonprofit brands become behaviourally effective under conditions of uncertainty?” It suggests that brands may become effective by stabilizing the donor’s expected emotional reward when impact outcomes remain ambiguous.

## Notes

This is a BEBA-theoretical integration claim, not yet an established empirical claim. It should be developed carefully and linked to uncertainty, trust, impact ambiguity, and donor experience literatures.
