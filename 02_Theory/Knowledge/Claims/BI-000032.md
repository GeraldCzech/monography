## Statement

In high-intangibility and high-perceived-risk contexts, brand image increases choice propensity by reducing consumer uncertainty.

## Implications for BEBA

This supports BEBA’s premise that nonprofit brand architecture can enable behaviour by reducing uncertainty about intangible value and impact.

## Notes

The claim is supported by the reviewed paper’s service-market interpretation and by cited conceptual arguments on service brands.
