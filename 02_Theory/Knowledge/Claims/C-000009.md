## Statement

In cause-related marketing, very large per-purchase donation amounts may reduce warm glow by triggering skepticism.

## Evidence

The synthesis reports emerging evidence that large per-purchase donation amounts can “cool” warm glow and reduce purchase intention, suggesting an inverted-U relationship.

Supported in the synthesis by: Chaabouni et al., 2020.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should include negative pathways in which prosocial brand claims become implausible, excessive, or credibility-damaging. More prosocial signalling is not always behaviourally beneficial.

## Notes

Evidence is context-specific to commercial cause-related marketing and should not be generalized automatically to nonprofit donation contexts.
