## Statement

Standard warm glow models may be insufficient to explain giving in religious or culturally specific contexts where duty, faith, or other-worldly rewards are salient motivations.

## Evidence

Fraser (2018, 2022) is cited as arguing that religious giving may involve motivations not fully captured by the standard warm glow or impure altruism model.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should avoid assuming that warm glow is a universal explanation for nonprofit giving. The architecture should allow multiple motivational systems, including identity, duty, moral obligation, religious commitment, and affective reward.

## Notes

Emerging or speculative claim relative to the reviewed material. This is not a rejection of warm glow but a limitation on its universal explanatory scope.
