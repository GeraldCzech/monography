## Claim ID

WG-C01

## Concept

Warm glow giving

## Statement

Donors can derive private emotional utility from the act of giving itself, independently of the donation’s consequential impact on recipients.

## BEBA Layer

Motivational layer

## Relationship Type

Mechanism

## Evidence

Reviewed material identifies warm glow as the motivational substrate of impure altruism and grounds it in Andreoni’s formal model of charitable giving. Key references named include Andreoni, 1989 and Andreoni, 1990.

## Confidence

High

## Status

candidate

## Implications for BEBA

Warm glow should be treated as a potential non-brand motivational pathway through which charitable behaviour becomes rewarding.

## Notes

This is the core definitional claim.

## Claim ID

WG-C02

## Concept

Impure altruism

## Statement

Incomplete crowd-out of private donations by government or third-party provision supports impure altruism over pure altruism.

## BEBA Layer

Motivational layer

## Relationship Type

Competing explanation

## Evidence

The reviewed material states that pure altruism predicts full crowd-out when charitable output is provided by others, whereas observed incomplete crowd-out indicates that donors value giving itself. Key references named include Andreoni, 1989; Andreoni, 1990; Crumpler & Grossman, 2008; and related crowd-out studies.

## Confidence

High

## Status

candidate

## Implications for BEBA

Behavioural effectiveness cannot be explained only by perceived beneficiary impact; donor-side utility must be considered.

## Notes

Central theoretical claim for distinguishing pure altruism from impure altruism.

## Summary

The extracted claims suggest that warm glow is a well-supported motivational mechanism in charitable giving, especially through impure altruism and incomplete crowd-out evidence. However, its integration into BEBA should remain **candidate-level** because brand-specific moderation, longitudinal dynamics, Austrian-context evidence, and SEM-compatible measurement are not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature.
