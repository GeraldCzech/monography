## Statement

Charitable giving is commonly motivated by a combination of altruistic concern for the beneficiary and private satisfaction from the act of giving.

## Evidence

The synthesis reports strong consensus that most donors are “impure altruists,” motivated by both concern for the cause and private satisfaction from giving.

Supported in the synthesis by: Andreoni, 1990; Wilhelm et al., 2014.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should avoid treating nonprofit brand equity as purely impact-belief driven. Donor behaviour may depend on both perceived social impact and the donor’s anticipated emotional or identity reward.

Closely related to C-WG-01 but broader because it specifies the mixed-motive structure.
