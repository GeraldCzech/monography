## Statement

Fundraising appeals that emphasize the donor’s self-benefit from giving can be more effective than appeals focused solely on the recipient’s benefit.

## Evidence

List et al. (2021) report that appeals targeting warm glow or “feel good” motivations can increase donation participation and amounts compared with purely recipient-benefit-focused appeals.

## Implications for BEBA

Nonprofit brand communications may enable behaviour by framing donation as both socially meaningful and personally rewarding. BEBA should distinguish impact appeals from donor-experience appeals.

## Notes

Established claim, but implementation likely depends on context and perceived authenticity.
