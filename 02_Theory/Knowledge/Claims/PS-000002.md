## Statement

Pro-social practices, including charitable giving and volunteering, are stratified by socioeconomic status and tend to reproduce existing social inequalities.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim qualifies a universally positive view of pro-social behaviour, forcing BEBA to consider the potentially stratifying effects of brand-enabled actions.

Based on the summary's report that the literature "consistently finds" this reproductive effect.
