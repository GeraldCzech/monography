## Statement

In some non-Western charitable giving contexts, income and religiosity are more salient predictors than traditional Theory of Planned Behavior factors.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim challenges BEBA to incorporate contextual and institutional factors that may override or reshape brand-enabled psychological pathways.

## Notes

The reviewed material presents this as evidence from non-Western contexts, but the extent and generality of the finding require further verification.
