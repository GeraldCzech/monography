## Statement

The long-term durability of warm glow from repeated fundraising appeals is not sufficiently established.

## Evidence

The synthesis identifies a research gap concerning “warmth decay” or desensitization from repeated exposure to warm-glow-based appeals.

## Implications for BEBA

BEBA should not assume that warm-glow-based brand appeals remain equally effective over time. Donor retention models may need to account for affective habituation or decay.

## Notes

Not a confirmed empirical claim. This should be treated as a research gap.
