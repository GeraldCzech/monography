## Statement

Self-image concerns influence whether an individual decides to donate.

## Evidence

The synthesis distinguishes self-image from social-image motives and states that self-image concerns are more influential for the decision to give.

Supported in the synthesis by: Grossman & Levy, 2024.

## Implications for BEBA

Nonprofit brand touchpoints aimed at donor acquisition may be more effective when they allow potential donors to see themselves as generous, responsible, compassionate, or efficacious actors.

## Notes

Evidence is presented as recent and specific. Further verification in the literature database would be needed before canonical use.
