## Statement

In nonprofit contexts with a strong member-service component, organizational identification is positively associated with donations, whereas peer identification is negatively associated with donations.

## Implications for BEBA

This claim introduces a critical competing mechanism that BEBA must model to explain behavioral variance, supporting the 'competing explanations' principle.

## Notes

This finding may be specific to contexts where peer interaction is a primary benefit (social consumption) and may not generalize to all NPOs.
