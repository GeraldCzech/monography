-   **SS-000005:** Situational Salience (social norm cues causally alter donation)
-   **SS-000006:** Situational Salience (cues can affect donation decision vs. amount differently)

# Core Theoretical Implication

The reviewed claims collectively reinforce the core BEBA premise that simple, linear models of intention-to-behaviour are insufficient. They highlight the necessity of modelling **competing and parallel pathways to behaviour**. Specifically, they establish a clear empirical and theoretical contest between:
1.  **Deliberative pathways** (moderated by culture and situation).
2.  **Habitual pathways** (driven by automaticity).
3.  **Brand-cued pathways** (driven by perceptions like uniqueness).
4.  **Simple behavioural persistence** (past behaviour predicting future behaviour).

The claims underscore that a key task for BEBA is to explain *when* and *for whom* each of these pathways becomes dominant, rather than assuming a single universal process.

# BEBA Layer Affected

-   **Market Layer:** SS-000004 qualifies the generalizability of findings from the Austrian context, defining the primary empirical setting as social and health services.
-   **Societal Layer:** SN-000008 and SN-000009 establish cultural context as a critical moderator, affecting the relevance of social-psychological constructs like Subjective Norm.
-   **Individual Layer:** All other claims (SS-000005, SS-000006, SN-000010, PB-000032, BU-000004, H-000003) directly impact the modelling of individual behaviour, introducing concepts of automaticity, situational cues, brand perception, and the multi-faceted nature of behavioural outcomes.

# Existing Claims Supported

-   The BEBA principle of "Behaviour before brands" is supported by PB-000032, which confirms that past behaviour is a powerful predictor that any brand-based model must account for or explain.
-   The "Architecture" component of BEBA is strongly supported by SS-000005, which provides causal evidence that situational cues (part of the choice architecture) directly influence behaviour.
-   The BEBA research question's focus on "conditions of uncertainty" is supported by the inconsistency of Subjective Norm effects (SN-000008, SN-000009), suggesting that brands might provide a more stable heuristic when social guidance is weak or contradictory.

# Existing Claims Challenged

-   Any simple, direct, and universal effect of `Subjective Norm -> Intention` in the BEBA model is challenged by SN-000008, SN-000009, and SN-000010. These claims demand that the role of social norms be modelled as highly conditional (on culture, on injunctive norms) or mediated (e.g., through a brand community).
-   A parsimonious model that only predicts the *occurrence* of a behaviour is challenged by SS-000006, which implies that the antecedents for the *decision to act* may differ from the antecedents for the *magnitude of the act*.

# New Candidate Claims

-   **Claim (Habit Pathway):** "Psychological habit, defined as automaticity, is a direct antecedent of supportive behaviour, operating partially or fully independent of behavioural intention." (Derived from H-000003)
-   **Claim (Brand Heuristic Pathway):** "Perceived Brand Uniqueness is a direct antecedent of supportive behaviour, serving as a heuristic that simplifies choice under uncertainty." (Derived from BU-000004)
-   **Claim (Mechanism Specification):** "The effect of Subjective Norm on behaviour is mediated by the strength of an individual's identification with a brand community." (Derived from SN-000008)
-   **Claim (Behavioural Outcome Distinction):** "Brand-related cognitions (e.g., uniqueness) primarily influence the extensive margin (decision to support), while situational and normative cues primarily influence the intensive margin (amount of support)." (Derived from SS-000006)

# Boundary Conditions

-   **Sectoral:** The findings confirming the dominance of the social/health service sector (SS-000004) establish a key boundary condition. BEBA models developed and tested on this core sector may not generalize to advocacy, environmental, or arts & culture nonprofits without further validation.
-   **Cultural:** The inconsistent effects of Subjective Norm (SN-000008, SN-000009) establish culture (e.g., individualism vs. collectivism) as a major boundary condition for the social-psychological components of the BEBA framework.

# Model Competition Implications

This set of claims establishes a critical area for model competition within BEBA:

1.  **Competition for Explaining Persistence:** Does `Past Behaviour` (PB-000032) predict future behaviour because it is a proxy for stable intentions (a classic TPB view), or because it reflects `Habit` automaticity (H-000003)? This requires an empirical test comparing a model with a `Past Behaviour -> Intention` path against one with a direct `Habit -> Behaviour` path that bypasses intention.
2.  **Competition for the Role of the Brand:** Does the brand act through deliberative cognitions (e.g., `Brand Uniqueness -> Attitude -> Intention -> Behaviour`) or does it act as a direct behavioural cue (`Brand Uniqueness -> Behaviour`)? Testing this is central to the BEBA thesis (BU-000004).
3.  **Competition for the Role of Social Influence:** A model where general `Social Norms -> Behaviour` is challenged. A competing model, where `Social Norms` are filtered or made salient by a `Brand Community -> Behaviour`, is implied by the inconsistency findings (SN-000008, SN-000010) and should be formally tested.

# Ontology Candidate Implications

1.  **`Habit` vs. `PastBehaviour`:** A critical distinction is required. `PastBehaviour` should be defined as an *observable event record*. `Habit` must be defined as a *latent psychological construct* representing response automaticity, consistent with H-000003. They cannot be used interchangeably.
2.  **`BehaviouralOutcome`:** The concept may need to be refined into sub-types to capture the distinction highlighted in SS-000006:
    -   `BehaviouralOutcome_Decision` (Extensive Margin: e.g., to donate or not)
    -   `BehaviouralOutcome_Magnitude` (Intensive Margin: e.g., how much to donate)
3.  **`SocialNorm`:** The ontology must continue to clearly distinguish between `DescriptiveNorm` (what others do) and `InjunctiveNorm` (what others approve of), as SN-000010 suggests their effects are not independent. The relationship to `SubjectiveNorm` (a TPB-specific construct) needs to be mapped explicitly.

# Recommendation

1.  **Prioritize Empirical Validation:** The claims on `Brand Uniqueness` (BU-000004) and `Habit` (H-000003) are identified as core to the BEBA framework but have the lowest confidence/evidence. They should be the focus of the next empirical study, specifically using a design that can test for causality and distinguish automaticity from simple behavioural frequency.
2.  **Formalize Model Competition:** Create formal model definitions (e.g., in `03_Models/Competing_Models/`) for the key competitions identified (Habit vs. TPB, Brand Heuristic vs. Deliberation). This will guide the empirical analysis plan.
3.  **Update Ontology Candidates:** Create a candidate file (`02_Theory/Ontology/Candidates/Habit_vs_PastBehaviour.md`) to formally propose the distinction between `Habit` and `PastBehaviour`. Do the same for `BehaviouralOutcome` sub-types.
4.  **Integrate Boundary Conditions:** Explicitly add "Cultural Context" and "Nonprofit Sub-Sector" as variables to consider in the `THEORETICAL_MAP.md` and any planned empirical designs. They should be treated as key moderators or sample selection criteria.
