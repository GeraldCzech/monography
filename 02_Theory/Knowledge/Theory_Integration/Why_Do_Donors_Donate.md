# Input Reviewed

This analysis integrates a set of nine related candidate claims (DS-000008, DL-000004, OI-000003, IS-000001, OI-000004, DL-000005, IS-000002, DL-000006, DB-000009) derived from `REVIEW-000092`. The claims collectively outline a dual-pathway model of donor loyalty, positing that loyalty is driven by both a traditional satisfaction-based path and a more complex identity-based path. Key findings include the causal sequence from satisfaction to organizational identification to identity salience, the empirical distinction between the two identity constructs, and the critical gap between attitudinal loyalty and actual donation behaviour.

# Core Theoretical Implication

The core implication is the validation of a dual-pathway architecture for building behavioural intentions (loyalty). The evidence suggests that a satisfaction-based pathway and an identity-based pathway operate in parallel. Crucially, the evidence also provides a specific, empirically-tested causal structure for the identity pathway (Satisfaction -> Organizational Identification -> Donor Identity Salience -> Loyalty). Finally, the demonstrated weakness of the loyalty-behaviour link (DB-000009) powerfully reinforces BEBA's central premise that overcoming the intention-action gap is the critical function of a brand architecture.

# BEBA Layer Affected

-   **Organisational Layer:** Claim DS-000008 links organisational strategy to individual-level outcomes, anchoring the model in the NPO's actions. Claim DL-000006 introduces organisational context (donation type) as a key moderator.
-   **Individual Layer:** The majority of the claims (DL-000004, OI-000003, IS-000001, OI-000004, DL-000005, IS-000002, DB-000009) detail the psychological mechanisms and relationships between satisfaction, identification, identity salience, loyalty, and behaviour at the individual donor level.

# Existing Claims Supported

-   **Claims about the Intention-Action Gap:** DB-000009 provides strong empirical support for BEBA's core focus on bridging the gap between positive attitudes/intentions (loyalty) and actual behaviour. It justifies the "Behaviour Architecture" part of the BEBA acronym.
-   **Claims about Identity's Role:** DL-000005 supports the general BEBA principle that identity is a significant driver of pro-organisational behaviour, operating alongside more transactional drivers like satisfaction.
-   **Claims about Organisational Antecedents:** DS-000008 supports the view that individual-level brand equity dimensions are rooted in the organisation's strategic orientation and actions.

# Existing Claims Challenged

This set of claims does not appear to directly challenge core BEBA tenets; rather, it provides significant refinement and specification. However, it might challenge any existing BEBA claims that:
-   Conflate organizational identification with individual identity salience.
-   Model loyalty as a direct and sufficient proxy for behaviour.
-   Propose that identity salience *moderates* the effect of organizational identification on loyalty (IS-000002 provides evidence for a direct effect model instead).
-   Assume a monolithic process for building loyalty that is insensitive to context (DL-000006 challenges this).

# New Candidate Claims

While the inputs are already candidate claims, their synthesis suggests a higher-order candidate claim for BEBA:

-   **New Candidate Claim:** "Nonprofit brand equity operates through at least two parallel psychological pathways: a transactional path mediated by satisfaction and a relational path mediated by a causal chain of organizational identification and identity salience. The relative strength of these pathways is contingent on the behavioural context."

# Boundary Conditions

-   **Donation Context:** Claim DL-000006 introduces a critical boundary condition. The BEBA model's parameterization must be context-sensitive. Identity pathways are stronger for high-involvement, identity-relevant behaviours (e.g., money donations), while satisfaction pathways may be more influential for lower-involvement, more routinized behaviours (e.g., blood donations). This suggests the "architecture" must be adaptable.

# Model Competition Implications

This evidence provides clear guidance for model specification within the BEBA framework:

1.  **Causal Direction:** The model `Satisfaction -> Organizational Identification` is empirically superior to the reverse (OI-000003).
2.  **Causal Direction:** The model `Organizational Identification -> Identity Salience` is empirically supported (IS-000001).
3.  **Direct vs. Moderated Effect:** A model where Identity Salience has a *direct* effect on loyalty is superior to one where it *moderates* the Identification-Loyalty relationship (IS-000002).

This allows BEBA to move from theoretical ambiguity to preferring a specific, evidence-based structural configuration for the identity pathway.

# Ontology Candidate Implications

The most significant implication is the need to formalize the distinction between two related but separate identity constructs:

-   **`Organizational Identification`**: Should be defined as a social identity construct representing a donor's sense of belongingness and connection to the NPO as a group. It is fundamentally relational and social.
-   **`Donor Identity Salience`**: Should be defined as a role identity construct representing the prominence and importance of the "donor" role within an individual's hierarchy of selves. It is fundamentally about self-concept.

Claim OI-000004 provides the empirical justification (discriminant validity) for maintaining this as a core ontological distinction within the BEBA knowledge base.

# Recommendation

1.  **Adopt the Dual-Pathway Structure:** Integrate the parallel satisfaction and identity pathways as a core component of the BEBA model for explaining the formation of behavioural intentions.
2.  **Formalize the Identity Chain:** Update the BEBA theoretical map (`01_THEORETICAL_MAP.md`) to reflect the specific causal sequence: `Satisfaction -> Organizational Identification -> Donor Identity Salience -> Loyalty`.
3.  **Refine Ontology:** Create or update concept files and the `glossary.md` to formally distinguish between `Organizational Identification` (social) and `Donor Identity Salience` (role/self-concept), citing claim OI-000004 as justification.
4.  **Incorporate Boundary Condition:** Add "Donation Context" (or a more general "Behavioural Context") as a key moderator variable in the BEBA model, specifically governing the relative influence of the satisfaction and identity pathways. This should be noted in the master outline and theoretical map.
5.  **Elevate Intention-Action Gap Evidence:** Use claim DB-000009 as a cornerstone piece of evidence in introductory chapters to justify the "Behaviour Architecture" focus of the BEBA project, as it clearly demonstrates the inadequacy of focusing solely on attitudinal loyalty.
