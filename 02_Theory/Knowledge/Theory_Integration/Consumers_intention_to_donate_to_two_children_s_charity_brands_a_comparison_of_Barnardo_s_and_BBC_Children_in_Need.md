# Input Reviewed

-   **BT-000019**: Brand Typicality is a stronger predictor for intention to donate time than money.
-   **BI-000042**: Brand Image effects are contingent on the nonprofit's market position.
-   **BT-000020**: Brand Typicality mediates the relationship between Brand Image and intention to donate time.
-   **PB-000017**: Past donation behaviour can be negatively associated with future donation intentions.

# Core Theoretical Implication

The reviewed claims collectively argue against a monolithic model of nonprofit brand equity. They establish that the cognitive pathway from brand perception to behavioural intention is highly specific, depending on both the **type of supportive behaviour** (time vs. money) and the **market context** of the nonprofit (e.g., established vs. event-driven). Brand Typicality emerges as a key cognitive mechanism, but its role is not universal. Furthermore, the claims introduce a significant challenge to linear, positive assumptions about habit formation, suggesting more complex psychological dynamics like moral licensing or donor fatigue could be at play.

# BEBA Layer Affected

-   **Individual Layer**: Primarily affected through the introduction of Brand Typicality as a cognitive mediator (`BT-000020`), the differentiation of behavioural intentions (`BT-000019`), and the challenging finding on past behaviour (`PB-000017`).
-   **Market Layer**: Directly affected by the claim that a brand's market position and public profile act as a boundary condition for how brand image dimensions translate into support (`BI-000042`).

# Existing Claims Supported

-   **Behavioural Specificity**: Claim `BT-000019` strongly supports the core BEBA principle that distinct behavioural architectures are required for different types of support (time vs. money).
-   **Context Dependency**: Claim `BI-000042` directly validates the BEBA principle that brand effects are not universal but are contingent on market-level factors.
-   **Cognitive Mediation**: Claim `BT-000020` supports the general BEBA architecture, which posits that cognitive and affective states mediate the relationship between brand exposure and behavioural outcomes.

# Existing Claims Challenged

-   **Habit Formation / Past Behaviour**: Claim `PB-000017` directly challenges any simple, positive, and linear claim within BEBA that `Past Behaviour -> Future Behaviour`. It forces a re-evaluation of this relationship, suggesting it is non-monotonic or moderated by other unobserved factors. This contradicts standard assumptions in models like the Theory of Planned Behaviour and habituation theory.

# New Candidate Claims

Based on the synthesis of the reviewed inputs, the following higher-order claims are proposed for integration into BEBA:

1.  **Claim**: The cognitive architecture linking brand perception to supportive behaviour is resource-dependent; the pathway for time-based support relies more heavily on category-level schemas (e.g., typicality) than the pathway for money-based support.
2.  **Claim**: The behavioural effect of past support is not a simple reinforcing loop. Under certain conditions, prior contributions can create a "moral licensing" effect that reduces the intention for subsequent support.
3.  **Claim**: The relevance of specific Brand Equity dimensions is determined by the interaction of the brand's market position and the specific behaviour being elicited.

# Boundary Conditions

-   **Behaviour Type**: The effect of Brand Typicality is bounded by the type of support. Its predictive power is strong for time donation but weaker for financial donation.
-   **NPO Market Position**: The influence of specific brand image dimensions is bounded by the nonprofit's market context (e.g., high-income, established incumbent vs. lower-income, event-driven challenger).

# Model Competition Implications

These claims have significant implications for BEBA's model competition principle:

1.  **Mandatory Model Differentiation**: BEBA must abandon any single model of "donation intention" and instead maintain at least two competing/parallel architectures: a **Time Support Model** and a **Financial Support Model**. Claim `BT-000020` suggests the Time Support Model must feature Brand Typicality as a key mediator between Brand Image and Intention.
2.  **Competing Explanations for Past Behaviour**: The anomalous finding in `PB-000017` necessitates a formal model competition around the effect of past behaviour. The simple `Habit -> Intention` model must be tested against a `Moral Licensing -> (-) Intention` or a `Goal Saturation -> (-) Intention` model.

# Ontology Candidate Implications

1.  **Elevate `Brand Typicality`**: Brand Typicality should be formally defined and integrated into the BEBA Ontology as a core `Construct` within the Individual Layer. Its definition should capture it as a cognitive schema representing how prototypical a brand is for its category.
2.  **Disambiguate `Supportive Behaviour`**: The ontology must enforce a clear distinction between `Intention to Donate Time` and `Intention to Donate Money` as separate `Constructs`. They cannot be used interchangeably or aggregated into a general "Donation Intention" construct without justification.
3.  **Introduce `Market Position`**: A new concept or a set of attributes may be needed at the Market Layer to capture `Market Position` (e.g., `revenue_tier`, `brand_age`, `primary_funding_model`). This would allow for formally modeling the contingency expressed in `BI-000042`.

# Recommendation

1.  **Update Core Architecture**: Formally split the BEBA behavioural architecture into (at minimum) two sub-architectures: one for financial support and one for time-based support.
2.  **Integrate Brand Typicality**: Add `Brand Typicality` as a canonical `Construct` in the `ConstructLedger` and `glossary.md`. Position it as a key mediator in the time-based support architecture.
3.  **Initiate Model Competition**: Create a research compass file (`ResearchCompass_PastBehaviourAnomalies.md`) to explicitly track the challenge from `PB-000017`. This file should outline competing theoretical models (Habituation vs. Moral Licensing) to be tested empirically.
4.  **Refine Contextual Factors**: Add `Market Position` as a key contextual variable in `01_THEORETICAL_MAP.md` that moderates the relationship between Market-level brand assets and Individual-level outcomes.
