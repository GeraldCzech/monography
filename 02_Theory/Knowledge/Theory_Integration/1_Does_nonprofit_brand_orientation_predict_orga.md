# Input Reviewed

This theory integration protocol reviews a set of seven candidate claims derived from `REVIEW-000094`. The claims cover the concepts of Brand Orientation, Brand Equity adaptation, Brand Differentiation, Internal Branding, and Attitude toward Charity within the nonprofit context. They collectively describe the link from an NPO's strategic brand posture to internal alignment, market perception, stakeholder attitudes, and ultimately, behavioural outcomes like donations and employee retention.

# Core Theoretical Implication

The core implication of this claim set is that a nonprofit's internal strategic choices regarding its brand (`Brand Orientation`, `Internal Branding`) are crucial antecedents to building external brand assets (`Brand Differentiation`, `Attitude toward Charity`) which, in turn, drive desired stakeholder behaviours (`Donation`, `Intent to Stay`). This provides strong, multi-level evidence for the foundational premise of BEBA: brand is a strategic architecture that enables behaviour. The claim set also validates the need for a context-specific theory (BE-000062).

# BEBA Layer Affected

The reviewed claims affect multiple layers of the BEBA architecture, demonstrating the theory's required multi-level nature:

-   **Organisational Layer**: Affected by claims on `Brand Orientation` (BO-000014, BO-000015, BO-000016) and `Internal Branding` (IB-000024). These concepts represent strategic inputs and internal processes.
-   **Market Layer**: Affected by the claim on `Brand Differentiation` (BD-000006), which describes a brand's position relative to competitors.
-   **Individual Layer**: Affected by the claim on `Attitude toward Charity` (AT-000004), which identifies a key psychological mediator in the stakeholder's mind.
-   **Core Theory / Metatheory**: Affected by the claim on `Brand Equity` adaptation (BE-000062), which provides the primary rationale for the BEBA project itself.

# Existing Claims Supported

This claim set provides broad support for the core causal logic of BEBA:

-   **The Organisational-Behaviour Link**: Claims BO-000014 and BO-000015 directly support the BEBA hypothesis that organisational-level brand strategy is positively associated with performance and key behaviours (donations).
-   **The Internal Alignment Mechanism**: Claim IB-000024 validates BEBA's emphasis on internal mechanisms, showing that `Internal Branding` leads to desired employee outcomes, a key component of a brand-enabled architecture.
-   **The Psychological Mediation Pathway**: Claim AT-000004 supports BEBA's modelling of psychological states as mediators between brand activities and behavioural outcomes. It specifies `Attitude toward Charity` as a critical mediator.
-   **The Context-Specificity of Nonprofit Branding**: Claim BE-000062 provides foundational support for BEBA's central argument: commercial models are insufficient, and a new theory adapted for the nonprofit context is necessary.

# Existing Claims Challenged

The reviewed claims do not challenge any existing core claims of BEBA. Instead, they provide empirical and theoretical substance to the hypothesised pathways. They move the theory from abstract principles to specified mechanisms and relationships.

# New Candidate Claims

Based on the synthesis of the reviewed claims, the following more integrated claims can be formulated for inclusion in the BEBA theory:

1.  **C-BEBA-NEW-01: Mediated Effect of Brand Orientation**: The positive effect of a nonprofit's Brand Orientation on stakeholder supportive behaviour (e.g., donation intention) is mediated by the stakeholder's Attitude toward the Charity. (Synthesizes BO-000015 and AT-000004).
2.  **C-BEBA-NEW-02: Internal-External Alignment Pathway**: A nonprofit's Brand Orientation, when implemented through Internal Branding strategies, enhances employee brand performance, which contributes to the overall strength of the Brand-Enabled Behaviour Architecture. (Synthesizes BO-000014 and IB-000024).
3.  **C-BEBA-NEW-03: Competitive Advantage through Differentiation**: In competitive markets, the effect of Brand Orientation on donor attraction is amplified by the degree of perceived Brand Differentiation the nonprofit achieves. (Synthesizes BO-000015 and BD-000006).

# Boundary Conditions

The reviewed claims introduce several important boundary conditions for BEBA:

1.  **Income Type**: The effect of Brand Orientation on fundraising is specified as being particularly strong for *voluntary income* and not for *statutory income* (from claim BO-000015).
2.  **Communication Strategy**: The long-term effectiveness of brand activities is moderated by the nonprofit's *strategic communication orientation* ("supportive" vs. "combative") (from claim BO-000016).
3.  **Stakeholder Type (Internal)**: The evidence for the positive effects of `Internal Branding` is currently more established for *paid staff* than for *volunteers* (from claim IB-000024).

# Model Competition Implications

This claim set significantly strengthens BEBA's position in the theoretical landscape:

-   It directly supports the argument against the simple transposition of commercial brand equity models (e.g., Aaker, Keller) to the nonprofit sector (BE-000062).
-   It provides evidence for a multi-level causal chain (Org Strategy -> Individual Psychology -> Behaviour) that is more comprehensive than models focusing only on consumer-based brand equity (CBBE) or organisational identity.
-   It highlights the necessity of including both internal (Internal Branding) and external (Brand Differentiation) components, supporting BEBA's holistic architectural approach.

# Ontology Candidate Implications

The reviewed claims necessitate the formal inclusion and definition of several key concepts in the BEBA ontology:

-   **`Brand Orientation`**: Must be defined as a core antecedent construct in the Organisational Layer, representing the organisation-wide strategic commitment to the brand.
-   **`Internal Branding`**: Must be defined as a core process construct in the Organisational Layer, describing the activities aimed at aligning employees and volunteers with the brand promise.
-   **`Brand Differentiation`**: Should be a key construct in the Market Layer, representing the degree to which a nonprofit brand is perceived as distinct from its competitors by stakeholders.
-   **`Attitude toward Charity`**: A critical mediating construct in the Individual Layer. It needs to be carefully distinguished from `Brand Attitude` or `Brand Trust` to ensure conceptual clarity.
-   **`Strategic Communication Orientation`**: A new candidate for a moderating variable that qualifies the relationship between brand activities and long-term outcomes.

3.  **Incorporate New Claims**: Add the newly formulated integrated claims (C-BEBA-NEW-01, -02, -03) to the central list of theoretical claims for empirical testing.
4.  **Update Theoretical Model**: Revise the visual and textual representation of the BEBA theoretical model (`01_THEORETICAL_MAP.md`) to explicitly show:
    -   `Brand Orientation` as an antecedent.

    -   The moderation path for `Strategic Communication Orientation`.
    -   The role of `Internal Branding` in the internal alignment pathway.
5.  **Document Boundaries**: Explicitly list the identified boundary conditions (Income Type, Communication Strategy, Internal Stakeholder Type) in the relevant theory and concept files. This is critical for future research design and for defining the scope of BEBA's explanatory power.
