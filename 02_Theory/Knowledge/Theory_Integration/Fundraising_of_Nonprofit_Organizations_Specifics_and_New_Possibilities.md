# Input Reviewed

This review integrates a set of nine candidate claims derived from `REVIEW-000067`, which covers sources such as Hommerová & Severová (2018), York (2015), and Novotný & Lukeš (2008). The claims address the relationship between internal nonprofit structures (strategic planning, professionalism), market-level perceptions (trust, reputation), and organizational outcomes (success, sustainability, funding mix).

# Core Theoretical Implication

The reviewed claims strongly support the BEBA thesis by linking internal organizational architecture to external market perceptions and, ultimately, to NPO success. The most significant implication is the introduction of nuance and contingency: the claims suggest that the effectiveness of an organizational architecture (specifically "professionalism") is not universal but is contingent on the target stakeholder group. This refines the BEBA model from a monolithic system to one that must account for stakeholder-specific pathways and potential trade-offs.

# BEBA Layer Affected

-   **Organisational Layer:** Heavily impacted. Claims `SP-000002`, `OP-000002`, `OP-000003`, `NS-000001`, `NS-000002`, `NS-000003`, and `F-000001` all pertain to the internal capabilities, planning, and behaviors that constitute the organizational architecture.
-   **Market Layer:** Directly impacted. Claims `T-000003` (Trustworthiness) and `BR-000014` (Brand Reputation) explicitly link market-level perceptions to NPO success, reinforcing this layer's role as a mediator between the organization and its stakeholders.
-   **Behavioural Layer:** Implicated through the connection of specific funding outcomes (public funding vs. individual donations) to organizational characteristics. This highlights that different stakeholder behaviours are driven by different architectural configurations.

# Existing Claims Supported

-   **Architecture-Outcome Link:** Claims `NS-000002` (financial plan -> success) and `NS-000003` (fundraising -> success) support the core BEBA premise that the internal "A" (Architecture) enables the external "B" (Behaviour) leading to positive outcomes.
-   **Internal Consistency of Architecture:** Claim `SP-000002` (strategic plan -> financial plan) provides evidence for the "architecture" concept itself, showing that its components are systematically related.
-   **Centrality of Brand Equity:** Claims `T-000003` (Trustworthiness -> success) and `BR-000014` (Brand Reputation -> success) provide direct, foundational support for the argument that brand equity is a key determinant of NPO success.

# Existing Claims Challenged

No core BEBA claims are directly contradicted. However, claim `OP-000003` (professionalization is *negatively* associated with reliance on individual donations) challenges a simplistic, universalist view of organizational capacity. It forces a refinement of the theory, suggesting that what constitutes an "effective" architecture is context- and stakeholder-dependent. A "one-size-fits-all" model of brand-enabling architecture is likely insufficient.

# New Candidate Claims

1.  **C-BEBA-ORG-011 (Candidate):** The optimal configuration of an NPO's internal architecture is contingent upon its primary target stakeholder group for resource acquisition.
2.  **C-BEBA-ORG-012 (Candidate):** There is a potential trade-off in architectural design; characteristics that attract institutional funders (e.g., high professionalization) may alienate individual grassroots donors.
3.  **C-BEBA-OUT-004 (Candidate):** NPO sustainability is a function of the dynamic interplay between leadership, environmental adaptability, and production capacity.

# Boundary Conditions

-   **Geographic/Cultural Context:** The findings regarding professionalism and funding sources (`OP-000002`, `OP-000003`) are from a study in the Czech Republic. This relationship may be bounded by national funding structures and the cultural perception of NPO professionalism.
-   **Stakeholder Type:** The effect of "Organisational Professionalism" is conditional on the stakeholder. It is a positive predictor for public funding but a negative predictor for individual donations. This makes stakeholder type a critical moderator in the BEBA model.

# Model Competition Implications

This review strongly supports introducing model competition into the BEBA framework around the concept of organizational capacity. Specifically:

-   **Model A (Universalist):** Posits that constructs like "Organisational Professionalism" have a uniformly positive main effect on Brand Equity and stakeholder support.
-   **Model B (Contingent):** Posits that the effect of "Organisational Professionalism" is moderated by stakeholder type.

The evidence from claims `OP-000002` and `OP-000003` provides strong preliminary support for Model B, making this a critical area for empirical testing.

# Ontology Candidate Implications

1.  **New Concept Candidate: `Stakeholder-Architectural Fit`**
    -   **Definition:** The degree to which an NPO's internal systems, processes, and professional posture align with the expectations, evaluation criteria, and cultural norms of a specific stakeholder segment.
    -   **Rationale:** This concept is needed to formally model the mechanism suggested by the contingent effects of professionalism.

2.  **Refinement of Outcome Concepts: `NPO Success` & `NPO Sustainability`**
    -   These are used as key dependent variables in the claims. They need to be formally defined within the BEBA ontology.
    -   `NS-000001` provides a candidate theoretical model for `NPO Sustainability` (f(leadership, adaptability, capacity)) that should be captured.

# Recommendation

1.  **Update Theory:** Integrate the reviewed claims to formalize the pathway from internal planning (`SP-000002`) through organizational behavior (`NS-000003`) and market perception (`T-000003`, `BR-000014`) to organizational outcomes.
2.  **Introduce Model Competition:** Create a new ADR or theoretical note (`02_Theory/Competing_Explanations/CE-001_Professionalism_Contingency.md`) to explicitly frame the "Universalist vs. Contingent" models of professionalism for empirical testing.
3.  **Propose New Ontology Candidate:** Create a candidate concept file (`03_Ontology/Candidates/Concept_StakeholderArchitecturalFit.md`) to define the proposed mechanism.
4.  **Update Construct Ledger:** Add `NPO Success` and `NPO Sustainability` to the `ConstructLedger.md` as key outcome constructs, noting their theoretical definitions provided by this review.
5.  **Tag for Context:** Ensure the evidence map for claims `OP-000002` and `OP-000003` is tagged with the "Czech Republic" context as a potential boundary condition.
