# Core Theoretical Implication

The reviewed claim set strengthens BEBA’s core assumption that nonprofit brand equity should not be treated as a generic attitudinal asset transferred from commercial branding theory. Instead, nonprofit brands become behaviourally effective through mechanisms that help stakeholders act under uncertainty, especially when outcomes, service quality, organizational integrity, and social impact cannot be directly observed.

The strongest theoretical contribution is the differentiation between generic nonprofit brand equity and NGO donor-based brand equity. The claims imply that donor behaviour toward NGOs is shaped less by simple awareness or generic brand associations and more by:

1. trust as uncertainty reduction;
2. moral value alignment;
3. cause identification;
4. perceived accountability and integrity signals;
5. inferential judgements of quality;
6. distinction between loyalty to the organization and commitment to the cause.

This supports the BEBA move from “brand equity as brand value” to “brand equity as behaviour-enabling architecture.”

# Existing Claims Challenged

## 1. Awareness as a simple positive antecedent

Challenged by:

- `BA-000026`
- `BF-000009`

The reviewed claims challenge any BEBA model in which brand awareness or familiarity is treated as linearly positive. Awareness can be behaviourally inert, and familiarity can become negative if associated with negative knowledge.

The implication is that BEBA should distinguish:

- awareness as recognition;
- familiarity as accumulated knowledge;
- valence of familiarity;
- cause-relevant meaning;
- trust-generating familiarity versus distrust-generating familiarity.

## 2. Loyalty as direct organizational attachment

Challenged by:

- `CC-000006`
- `BL-000005`

The reviewed claims suggest that nonprofit loyalty may not map cleanly onto commercial loyalty. Donors may be committed to a cause rather than to a specific organization. This challenges any model in which loyalty to the nonprofit brand is assumed to be the primary retention mechanism.

## 3. Perceived quality as direct service evaluation

Challenged by:

- `PQ-000005`
- `PQ-000006`

In nonprofit and NGO contexts, donors often do not consume or directly observe the service. Perceived quality should therefore be theorised as an inferential judgement rather than an experiential assessment.

## 4. One-size-fits-all nonprofit brand equity

Challenged by:

- `NO-000001`
- `NB-000054`
- `MG-000001`
- `SP-000001`
- `CC-000007`

The claim set challenges broad nonprofit generalization. BEBA should avoid presenting one universal nonprofit brand equity model without specifying organization type, stakeholder role, cultural context, and cause context.

# New Candidate Claims

The following candidate claims could be added to the BEBA claim architecture, preferably as candidate claims rather than canonical claims at this stage.

## Candidate Claim 1: Organization-Type Specificity

**Statement:**  
Nonprofit brand equity mechanisms vary by nonprofit organization type; foundations, charities, and NGOs should not be treated as theoretically interchangeable.

**Supported by:**  
`NO-000001`, `NB-000054`, `MG-000001`

**BEBA implication:**  
Organization type should become a modelling boundary condition.

## Candidate Claim 2: Donor-Based NGO Brand Equity Requires Moral-Cause Mechanisms

**Statement:**  
In NGO donor contexts, brand equity becomes behaviourally effective through moral value alignment, cause identification, trust, and perceived accountability rather than through awareness alone.

**Supported by:**  
`MV-000003`, `DS-000001`, `TR-000010`, `BT-000017`, `PA-000001`, `BA-000026`

**BEBA implication:**  
NGO donor behaviour should be modelled as a morally and uncertainty-mediated behavioural pathway.

## Candidate Claim 3: Familiarity Has Ambivalent Behavioural Effects

**Statement:**  
Brand familiarity can reduce uncertainty when associated with positive knowledge but amplify uncertainty when associated with negative knowledge.

**Supported by:**  
`BF-000009`

**BEBA implication:**  
Familiarity should be modelled with valence, not as a uniformly positive construct.

## Candidate Claim 4: Perceived Quality Is Inferential in Donor Contexts

**Statement:**  
Because donors often cannot directly observe nonprofit service delivery or impact, perceived quality functions as an inferential judgement under uncertainty.

**Supported by:**  
`PQ-000005`, `PQ-000006`

**BEBA implication:**  
Perceived quality should be repositioned from service-experience evaluation to uncertainty-based inference.

## Candidate Claim 5: Cause Commitment Can Bypass Organizational Brand Loyalty

**Statement:**  
Donor commitment may attach primarily to the cause rather than to the branded nonprofit organization, creating a behavioural pathway that can bypass organization-level brand loyalty.

**Supported by:**  
`CC-000006`, `BL-000005`

**BEBA implication:**  
BEBA should distinguish cause-directed behavioural commitment from brand-directed loyalty.

## Candidate Claim 6: Stakeholder Role Conditions Brand Equity Mechanisms

**Statement:**  
Brand equity mechanisms differ by stakeholder role; donor-based brand equity should not be generalized to volunteers, beneficiaries, staff, or institutional partners without further specification.

**Supported by:**  
`SP-000001`

**BEBA implication:**  
Stakeholder role should be an explicit parameter in the BEBA information model.

## 3. Do not collapse this into generic nonprofit brand equity

The most important architectural implication is that BEBA should not use “NPO” as a theoretically flat category. NGO donor-based brand equity should be modelled as a specific case.

## 4. Add candidate boundary conditions

Recommended candidate boundary conditions:

- organization type;
- stakeholder role;
- cause category;
- familiarity valence;
- cultural context;
- impact observability;
- cause versus organization attachment.

## 5. Treat cause commitment as a potential bypass route

This is especially important for BEBA model competition. A donor may donate because of the cause, even if organization-level brand equity is weak. This creates a rival or parallel behavioural route:

```text
Cause salience / moral concern
-> Cause commitment
-> Donation intention
```

This may bypass:

```text
Brand awareness
-> Brand associations
-> Brand trust
-> Brand loyalty
-> Donation intention
```

## 6. Prioritise trust, perceived accountability, and inferential quality for BEBA’s uncertainty architecture

The strongest fit with the current research question lies in the uncertainty mechanism:

```text
Low observability of NGO impact/service delivery
-> donor uncertainty
-> reliance on brand-enabled signals
-> trust / perceived accountability / inferred quality
-> behavioural intention
```

## 7. Evidence caution

Several claims have medium or low confidence, and some are based on review synthesis rather than direct empirical findings. They should not yet be written as settled canonical BEBA theory.
