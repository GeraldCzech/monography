# Input Reviewed

This review integrates a coherent set of six foundational methodological claims:
- **BE-000065:** Bayesian Evidence Aggregation
- **ET-000001:** Evidence-Theory-Ontology Workflow
- **JC-000001:** Jeffrey Conditionalization
- **LP-000001:** Likelihood Paradigm
- **KG-000001:** Knowledge Graph
- **BM-000008:** BEBA Methodological Framework (Synthesis)

These claims collectively define the epistemological, procedural, and technical foundations of the BEBA research project.

# Core Theoretical Implication

The core implication of this claim set is the formal establishment of the BEBA project as a work of **cumulative theory building** based on Bayesian epistemology. It moves the project's foundation away from traditional, discrete null-hypothesis significance testing (NHST) and narrative reviews towards a transparent, replicable, and formally structured process for integrating uncertain evidence into a coherent theoretical knowledge base. The synthesis claim (BM-000008) positions this methodological apparatus as a novel contribution in its own right, addressing a gap in how nonprofit brand research builds and validates theory.

# BEBA Layer Affected

Primarily, the **Organisational Layer** is affected. These claims define the "operating system" (BEBA-ROS) of the research itself. They dictate the rules for evidence-gathering, claim-validation, theory-integration, and knowledge-representation for the entire project. By extension, they govern how claims at the Individual, Organisational, and Societal layers are formulated and evaluated.

# Existing Claims Supported

This set of foundational claims does not directly support specific, substantive claims about nonprofit branding (e.g., the effect of brand awareness on donations). Instead, it provides the **justification for the entire process** by which all other BEBA claims are generated, reviewed, and assigned a degree of belief. It directly supports the core **Research Principles** of the BEBA project, such as "Theory before models," "Evidence before preference," and "Competing explanations over single models."

# Existing Claims Challenged

These claims do not challenge any existing *internal* BEBA claims. They are foundational. However, they implicitly and explicitly challenge the methodological status quo in the broader field of social science and marketing research by:
- Challenging the primacy of NHST as the sole arbiter of scientific knowledge (BE-000065).
- Challenging the common conflation of statistical significance, evidential strength, and theoretical truth (LP-000001).
- Challenging the adequacy of informal, narrative literature reviews for rigorous theory building (ET-000001).

# New Candidate Claims

The reviewed documents *are* a set of new candidate claims. The most significant is the meta-claim **BM-000008**, which posits that the synthesis of these individual components constitutes a novel and coherent methodological framework. This is the central justification for the project's unique approach.

# Boundary Conditions

This framework introduces a crucial **methodological boundary condition** for the entire BEBA theory. A claim can only be considered a well-supported part of the final BEBA theory if it can be derived and justified through the Evidence-Theory-Ontology workflow. Beliefs, hypotheses, or findings that cannot be processed through this evidence-aggregation pipeline are considered outside the formal boundaries of the developed theory.

# Model Competition Implications

This framework is central to BEBA's approach to model competition. The combination of the Likelihood Paradigm (LP-000001) and Bayesian Evidence Aggregation (BE-000065) provides the formal engine for comparing competing theoretical models. It shifts the goal from "finding the one correct model" to "quantifying the relative evidence for a set of competing explanations." Jeffrey Conditionalization (JC-000001) provides the specific mechanism for updating our relative belief in these models as new, uncertain evidence arrives.
