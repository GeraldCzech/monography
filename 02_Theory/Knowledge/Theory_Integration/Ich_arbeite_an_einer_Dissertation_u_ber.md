# Input Reviewed

-   **BA-000079**: An increase in a nonprofit's brand awareness (cognitive accessibility) causes a positive increase in an individual's perception of the descriptive social norm for supporting that nonprofit.
-   **BI-000052**: Nonprofit brand image and trust influence donation intention primarily by shaping donor attitudes.
-   **BD-000011**: Perceived brand differentiation has a positive and statistically significant direct effect on an individual's actual donation behaviour, controlling for brand awareness and brand image.
-   **BD-000012**: The effect of perceived brand differentiation on actual donation behaviour is mediated by the individual's perceived efficacy of the nonprofit.

# Core Theoretical Implication

This set of claims collectively proposes a multi-pathway model for how brand perceptions translate into pro-social behaviour. It moves beyond a monolithic "Brand Equity -> Intention" model by:
1.  Grounding foundational brand constructs (Awareness, Image) in specific cognitive and social-psychological mechanisms (fluency-as-prevalence, attitude formation).
2.  Proposing that a more strategic brand attribute (Differentiation) has the potential to directly influence *actual behaviour*, thereby addressing the intention-behaviour gap.
3.  Identifying a specific psychological mediator (Perceived Efficacy) to explain *how* brand differentiation might achieve this behavioural effect.

The synthesis suggests that different facets of brand equity operate through distinct causal pathways—some influencing social cognition, others shaping attitudes and intentions, and others potentially triggering behaviour directly.

# BEBA Layer Affected

-   **Individual Layer**: All four claims operate at the individual level of analysis, concerning an individual's perceptions, cognitions, intentions, and behaviours.

# Existing Claims Supported

-   The set supports the foundational BEBA assumption that brand is a critical antecedent to pro-social behaviour.
-   `BI-000052` specifically supports the classic, widely accepted pathway where brand perceptions (Image, Trust) influence attitudes, which in turn shape behavioural intentions. This validates a core component of many behavioural models that BEBA would likely build upon.

# Existing Claims Challenged

-   `BD-000011` challenges any model that assumes brand effects *only* operate through intentions. By proposing a direct link from a brand perception (Differentiation) to *actual behaviour*, it challenges the necessity and sufficiency of intention as the sole proximal determinant of brand-driven behaviour.
-   The set as a whole challenges a simplistic, aggregate view of "Brand Equity." It implies that components like Awareness, Image, and Differentiation are not interchangeable and may have unique, non-additive causal roles in the behavioural architecture.

# New Candidate Claims

Based on the synthesis, several new candidate claims can be formulated for testing:

1.  **C-BEBA-01 (Competing Pathways)**: The effect of nonprofit brand equity on pro-social behaviour is transmitted through at least three partially independent pathways: (a) an attitude-intention pathway, (b) a social-normative pathway, and (c) a direct efficacy-behaviour pathway.
2.  **C-BEBA-02 (Component Specificity)**: Foundational brand equity components (e.g., Awareness) primarily influence behaviour via social-cognitive heuristics (e.g., social norms), while strategic components (e.g., Differentiation) primarily influence behaviour via efficacy perceptions.
3.  **C-BEBA-03 (Interaction Effect)**: The direct effect of Brand Differentiation on behaviour (`BD-000011`) is moderated by Brand Awareness; the effect is stronger when awareness is high.

# Boundary Conditions

The reviewed claims do not explicitly introduce new boundary conditions. They are stated as general effects. Future research should investigate potential moderators such as:
-   Issue involvement / personal relevance
-   Pre-existing donor identity
-   Nonprofit category (e.g., humanitarian aid vs. environmental advocacy)
-   Information environment (e.g., high vs. low choice complexity)

# Model Competition Implications

This integration is highly consequential for model competition. It establishes a clear research agenda to test competing and complementary structural models:

-   **Model 1 (Standard TPB/TRA Adaptation)**: Brand Perceptions -> Attitude -> Intention -> Behaviour. (Based on `BI-000052`)
-   **Model 2 (Social Heuristic Path)**: Brand Awareness -> Perceived Social Norm -> Intention/Behaviour. (Based on `BA-000079`)
-   **Model 3 (Direct Behavioural Path)**: Brand Differentiation -> Actual Behaviour. (Based on `BD-000011`)
-   **Model 4 (Mediated Behavioural Path)**: Brand Differentiation -> Perceived Efficacy -> Actual Behaviour. (Based on `BD-000012`)

BEBA's empirical strategy should aim to build a comprehensive model that integrates these pathways and tests their relative explanatory power.

# Ontology Candidate Implications

This integration requires additions and refinements to the BEBA ontology:

1.  **New Concept Candidate**: `Perceived Efficacy`. This must be clearly defined as a distinct concept from `Brand Trust` or `Brand Image`. Definition candidate: "An individual's belief in a nonprofit's capability to solve the social problem it addresses."
2.  **New Mechanism Candidate**: `Fluency-as-Prevalence Heuristic`. This should be added as a specific cognitive mechanism linking `Brand Awareness` to `Perceived Social Norm`.
3.  **Refined Distinction**: The ontology must strongly enforce the distinction between `Donation Intention` and `Actual Donation Behaviour` as separate outcome constructs, as they are central to the competing pathways proposed.

# Recommendation

1.  **Create New Candidate Claims**: Formally create candidate claim files in the repository for C-BEBA-01, C-BEBA-02, and C-BEBA-03 outlined above.
2.  **Create Ontology Candidate File**: Initiate a new concept workspace or ontology candidate file for `Perceived Efficacy`, detailing its proposed definition, relationship to other constructs, and potential measurement approaches.
3.  **Update Theoretical Map**: Update `01_THEORETICAL_MAP.md` to visually represent the competing pathways identified. This map should clearly distinguish the "intention-mediated" route from the proposed "direct/efficacy-mediated" behavioural route.
4.  **Prioritize for Empirical Testing**: Flag the model competition aspect as a high-priority item for the empirical pipeline. The testing of a comprehensive structural model including all four claims should be a core objective of the dissertation's empirical chapters.
