# Core Theoretical Implication

The claim set strongly supports the central BEBA premise that nonprofit brands become behaviourally effective because they structure donor decision-making under uncertainty.

However, the material also refines BEBA in three important ways:

1. **Brand equity should not be reduced to awareness or salience.**  
   Awareness/familiarity may be necessary for behavioural influence, but it is insufficient and can even be non-significant or negative when not accompanied by positive associations, trust, or commitment.

2. **Trust and commitment appear more central in nonprofit contexts than classic commercial brand-equity dimensions such as perceived quality or differentiation.**  
   This supports a sector-specific BEBA architecture rather than direct importation of commercial brand-equity models.

3. **Measurement architecture must remain context-sensitive and competitive.**  
   The Boenigk & Becker three-dimensional structure — Awareness, Trust, Commitment — appears to be a plausible candidate for the Austrian nonprofit context, but should not yet be canonised without further validation.

# Existing Claims Supported

## BEBA Core Claim: Brands enable behaviour under uncertainty

Supported by:

- `UR-000002`: Nonprofit brands reduce donor decision complexity under information asymmetry and intangible outcomes.
- `BT-000009`: Brand trust increases willingness to give by increasing confidence that donations will be used effectively and ethically.
- `NB-000037`: Higher nonprofit customer-based brand equity is positively associated with donors’ intention to donate.

The strongest implication is that **brand equity operates as a behavioural enabling architecture**, not merely as a perceptual asset.

## BEBA Claim: Nonprofit brand equity is multidimensional

Supported by:

- `NB-000038`: Nonprofit brand equity includes awareness/familiarity and positive associations/image.
- `BT-000010`: Trust may be more central than perceived quality in nonprofit contexts.
- `DC-000004`: Commitment is a central dimension of nonprofit brand equity.

This supports BEBA’s construct-level position that nonprofit brand equity should be treated as a multidimensional architecture.

## BEBA Claim: Brand equity mediates between organisational action and donor behaviour

Supported by:

- `BV-000001`: Organisational marketing and communication investments build donor mindset, which in turn drives donation-related market performance.

This supports a value-chain-compatible interpretation of BEBA: organisational action does not directly produce donor behaviour; it works through intermediate brand-enabled cognitive, affective, and relational structures.

## BEBA Claim: Measurement must distinguish core constructs from overlapping facets

Supported by:

- `CR-000003`: Brand respect and brand differentiation lack discriminant validity in the Austrian nonprofit context.
- `CR-000004`: The Romero et al. model shows poor fit.
- `CR-000005`: The Romero et al. model contains construct redundancy.

These claims support BEBA’s need for disciplined construct architecture.

# Existing Claims Challenged

## Simple awareness-to-behaviour pathway

Challenged by:

- `BA-000014`: Awareness/familiarity alone is insufficient to generate positive nonprofit brand equity.
- `BA-000015`: Familiarity can have non-significant or negative effects when not accompanied by positive associations.

Implication: BEBA should avoid treating awareness as automatically behaviour-enabling. Awareness may function as an entry condition, exposure condition, or salience condition, but not necessarily as a positive behavioural mechanism.

## Direct adoption of commercial brand-equity models

Challenged by:

- `BT-000010`: Trust is more central in nonprofit brand equity than perceived quality.
- `MV-000002`: The Faircloth model shows marginal model fit in the Austrian nonprofit context.
- `CR-000003`: Some Faircloth dimensions lack discriminant validity.
- `CR-000004` and `CR-000005`: The Romero et al. model shows poor fit and construct redundancy.

Implication: BEBA should not inherit commercial brand-equity architectures without sector-specific theoretical and empirical justification.

## Universal measurement architecture

Challenged or qualified by:

- `CD-000006`: Psychometric validity differs across geographic and cultural contexts.
- `OS-000001`: Dynamics may differ between large, well-known nonprofits and smaller grassroots organisations.

Implication: BEBA should distinguish between **theoretical architecture** and **measurement architecture**. The former may be general; the latter may be context-sensitive.

# Recommendation

1. **Integrate this claim set as strong support for the individual-level BEBA mechanism of uncertainty reduction.**

2. **Do not canonise awareness as a sufficient driver of behaviour.**  
   Awareness should be represented as conditional or enabling, not inherently positive.

3. **Treat Brand Trust and Donor Commitment as central candidate constructs in the nonprofit-specific BEBA architecture.**

4. **Maintain model competition.**  
   The Boenigk & Becker model should be treated as a strong candidate measurement architecture for the Austrian nonprofit context, but Faircloth and Romero et al. should remain in the model competition space as challenged alternatives rather than being erased.

5. **Create or update ontology candidates rather than canonical ontology entries at this stage.**  
   Especially for:
   - Uncertainty Reduction
   - Trust-Based Confidence
   - Awareness as Conditional Salience
   - Social Signalling
   - Context-Sensitive Measurement
   - Organisational Visibility
   - Donor–Beneficiary–Organisation Triad

6. **Do not make empirical claims beyond the reviewed candidate statements.**  
   Any specific CFA/SEM numbers, model fit indices, coefficients, or sample details must be checked against verified pipeline outputs before being used in chapters or papers.

7. **Potential ADR needed if BEBA architecture is changed.**  
   If the core BEBA model is revised to make Trust and Commitment canonical dimensions, or if Awareness is formally downgraded from causal driver to conditional salience construct, this should trigger an ADR and likely updates to the ConstructLedger and glossary.
