abstract: It is often suggested that nonprofit organizations positively impact our
  local communities. Studies, however, have consistently shown that the distribution
  of these organizations varies considerably from one community to the next. These
  differences have led some scholars to begin raising serious concern about the degree
  of “charitable equity” across communities. Thus, the purpose of this study was to
  explore how the makeup of a community’s nonprofit sector affects the views of those
  who potentially depend on nonprofit services. Specifically, using data from a countywide
  survey of public attitudes toward nonprofits in southern California ( N = 1,002),
  we examined whether differences in the distribution of nonprofits affected individuals’
  confidence in nonprofit performance as well as their awareness of what nonprofit
  organizations even are. Findings indicated that nonprofit density was strongly related
  to awareness of the sector, while awareness was, in turn, strongly related to confidence
  in nonprofit performance.
