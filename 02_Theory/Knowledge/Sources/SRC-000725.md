abstract: In the past decade, nonprofits scholars have given increased attention to
  the topic of vulnerability and organizational demise. An early contribution to this
  literature was Tuckman and Chang’s elaboration of four financial ratios that they
  believe could be used to predict financial vulnerability. These measures have largely
  escaped empirical tests. This research note describes a test of the Tuckman-Chang
  financial vulnerability measures among a population of nonprofit arts organizations.
  The article concludes that although the Tuckman-Chang measures do not have utility
  for all types of arts nonprofits, the measures nonetheless show substantial promise
  for predicting the closure of some nonprofit organizations.
