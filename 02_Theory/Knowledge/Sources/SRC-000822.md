abstract: The authors propose a communication-based model of relationship marketing
  and discuss how communication (rather than persuasion) is the foundation of the
  “new” customer-focused marketing efforts. The authors trace recent parallel shifts
  in communication and marketing theory and show the intersections between communication
  and marketing. Although communication always has been a critical element in marketing,
  the authors show how the increase in interactivity makes communication an even more
  valuable element of marketing by identifying those many points that link the two
  disciplines. Using the three key points at which the two disciplines intersect—messages,
  stakeholders, and interactivity—the authors develop a communication-based model
  of marketing. They demonstrate how interactive communication at three levels—corporate,
  marketing, and marketing communication—leads to the brand relationships that drive
  brand value.
