abstract: Abstract Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) in sub-Saharan Africa (SSA)
  experience financial challenges that hinder efforts to promote social change and
  development. Revenue diversification is one adaptive response to these challenges,
  yet there is a lack of evidence concerning the relationship between revenue diversification
  and financial vulnerability among NGOs in SSA. Using data from an online survey
  of NGOs ( N = 170), we hypothesized that a greater number of revenue sources is
  associated with lower probability of financial vulnerability, while a greater level
  of dependence on international funding is associated with higher probability of
  financial vulnerability. Results from probit regression models controlling for organizational
  characteristics indicated partial support for hypotheses. Having four or more types
  of revenue was associated with 87% lower probability of financial vulnerability
  compared to having one type of revenue ( p &lt; 0.001). Also, NGOs with up to half
  of their budgets covered by international sources had 17% lower probability of financial
  vulnerability compared to NGOs with no international funding ( p &lt; 0.05). Implications
  for future research to further explore these relationships are discussed.
