  employ a wide variety of measures, with consumer-based surveys often playing a central
  role. The authors identify some core dimensions of survey-based measures of brand
  performance, explore how they link to one another, and examine how they vary across
  both countries and categories. Studies in the United States and China of soft drinks,
  toothpaste, and fast food suggest that survey-based brand metrics can be categorized
  into six main dimensions that reflect a fourstage, hierarchy-of-effects awareness-interest-desire-action-type
