These six questions break into three facets: model performance, norms and past behavior, and behavior translation
including volunteering and identity. The evidence is strongest for extended TPB rather than a distinct Integrated
Behavioral Model, and weaker for role identity moderation specifically.

Model Performance
Standard TPB predicts charitable intention well, explaining 44% of variance in a meta-analysis, but it predicts
actual giving less well, explaining 19% of behavior (White et al., 2023). Extended or revised TPB models usually
perform better because adding moral norm and past behavior increases explanatory power (Veludo-De-Oliveira et
al., 2016; Mittelman & Rojas Méndez, 2018).

‐

• A revised TPB outperformed TRA and standard TPB for future monetary donation behavior (Veludo-De-

Oliveira et al., 2016).
• In charitable giving, adding moral norm raised explained intention variance from 44% to 52% (White et al.,

2023).
• There is no direct evidence here that a distinct Integrated Behavioral Model outperforms TPB; the closest

evidence supports TPB integrated with ELM for ad response contexts, not general giving prediction (Van
Steenburg & Spears, 2021).

Norms and Past Behavior
Social norms do predict donation intention, but their effect depends on which norm is measured and in what
context. Subjective or injunctive norms are often significant, while descriptive norms are less consistent, and
moral norms usually emerge as stronger predictors than social norms alone (White et al., 2023; Smith &
McSweeney, 2007; Li et al., 2022).
• Injunctive norms predicted charitable intentions, sometimes about as strongly as attitude (Smith & McSweeney,

2007).
• Descriptive norms were non-significant in one revised TPB study, but significant in health giving and in some

experimental donation settings (Smith & McSweeney, 2007; Fehresti et al., 2021; Gugenishvili & Colliander,
2022).
• Past donation behavior repeatedly predicts future intention, and sometimes is among the strongest predictors

Overall, extended TPB models predict charitable giving better than standard TPB, social norms matter but are
usually weaker than moral norms and past behavior, and the intention-behavior gap remains a real limitation,
especially when moving from stated intention to actual donation.
