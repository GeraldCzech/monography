Impact of Perceived Service Quality on Repurchase Intention and Word of Mouth:
Mediating Role of Customer Satisfaction
Rohail Jibran 1 Madiha Rasul 2 Mubashir Hussain 3 Rimsha Shahid 4 Muhammad Yasin 5

Abstract: The current study provides and tests an integrated model that examines customer satisfaction as a
mediator among the relationship of perceived service quality, repurchase intention, and word of mouth. This
research was a quantitative study. A convenience sampling technique was used for this research. Data was
collected from those people who are availing hotel services in different cities of Pakistan. The sample size of this
study was 210. SPSS software was used for data analysis. Findings of this research show that if the service
provider of the hotel industry provides quality services to the customers, then customers will be happy and
satisfied. If the customer is satisfied with the service provider, he will reuse those particular services in the future
if needed and also generate positive word of mouth and voluntarily promote that particular service provider to
others as well. Managers should try to establish those kind of strategies which helps to provide a quality and
satisfying consumption experience to their customers, which can lead towards repurchase intention and
positive word of mouth.
Key Words: Perceived Service Quality (PSQ), Customer Satisfaction (CS), Repurchase Intentions (RPI),
Word of Mouth (WOM)

Introduction
Service quality is known as an important indicator for customer satisfaction. Previous studies suggest that
customer in this modern era wants to spend less time and effort on selecting a service provider for reuse
purposes (Yang et al., 2024). Customer wants to purchase the product or services with a minimum time
because the value of time is vital to customers. The customer considers high value of time and accepts it
as good for the customer (Singh et al., 2025). In the hotel industry, the quality of services remains vital for
customer satisfaction because customer judges service providers based on the quality of services they
provide to customers (Kalnaovakul et al., 2025). Service quality is also the key indicator for consumer postpurchase behaviour. If the quality of the service is good, then the customer will be satisfied with that
particular service provider, and it also generates positive post-purchase behaviour of the customer
(Nguyen et al., 2023). Quality service not only increases sales but also helps to maintain good relationships
with customers, which helps the service provider to retain customers (Wilistyorini & Sussanto, 2022). Sales
persons' training also plays a key role in providing a quality service and customer satisfaction, trained
sales persons can facilitate customers more effectively compared to untrained sales persons (Sann et al.,
2024). Service quality is the major tool to generate positive word of mouth and encourages customers for
repurchase intentions, hotel managers try to provide quality services to the customers to make customers

Theoretical Framework and Hypotheses Development
Perceived Services Quality and Customer Satisfaction
Previous studies and literatures confirms that customers' satisfaction is a very significant antecedent for
post-purchase behaviour intention and consistency (Ali et al., 2021). Service quality has been recognised
as a significant factor in both word-of-mouth communications and customer satisfaction (Andri et al.,
2022). The relationship among the perceptions of education can be summarised as follows: perceived
service quality is an antecedent of customer satisfaction. Service quality has a positive influence on
consumer satisfaction (Uzir et al., 2021). Previous study on customer fulfilment has also created the
connection between satisfaction and service quality (Fabian et al., 2024). Many studies have investigated
how perceptions of customer service quality stimulate the level of satisfaction (Perdomo et al., 2024).
Service quality has a big part in business marketing, it not only plays a significant role in customer
satisfaction but is also deliberated as a way to place a product in a dynamic atmosphere and is worthdriven for customers. In the hospitality context, the service quality has a direct relation with customers,
which is why satisfied customers repeat their purchases in hotels (Paulose & Shakeel, 2022).
H1: Perceived service quality has a positive impact on customer' satisfaction.

Perceived Service Quality and Repurchase Intention
Perceived service quality is characterised as the impression of potential customers about the level of quality
that a product or service would offer (Bakir et al., 2024). Salem & Kiss (2022) conclude that there is a direct
and significant connection between the service qualities, customer satisfaction and repurchase intentions.
Customers represented an effective image for creating competitive advantages for the hotel industry
(Şişman & Demirci, 2025). Teo et al. (2025) found a positive relationship between service qualities and
repeat purchase intentions and also readiness to recommend. In the Indian hoteling sectors, the service
quality dimensions have a positive impact and a positive relationship on consumers' loyalty and
satisfaction (Kumar et al., 2025). Previous studies have investigated that service quality goes to boost the
consumer's fulfilment and customer's loyalty (Izogo & Ogba, 2015). Nasir et al. (2021) argued that service
quality proportions (reliability, tangible, assurance and responsiveness) and consumer repurchase
intentions have a significant and positive relationship with each other. Good services qualities have a
substantial influence on customer satisfaction. Ali et al. (2021) conclude that the concept of consumers'
revisit intentions by examining the sequence of procedures in retention from consumer satisfaction and
the perspectives of consumer services. The finding shows that the advanced level of service quality impacts
the advanced level of consumer retention (Abdullah et al., 2023). Bujisic et al. (2014) indicate that good
service quality generates a quality and satisfying consumption experience for the customers, which leads
towards repurchase intentions.
H2: There is a positive relationship between perceived service quality and re-purchase intentions.

Perceived Service Quality and Word of Mouth
Customers always communicate with positive word of mouth when they have an optimistic perception of
the service's esteem. Companies can gain profit, decrease their advertising and acquisition costs by using
positive word of mouth (Tessema et al., 2024). The researched significance of word of mouth (WOM) for
the hospitality context has been very much recognised (Ali, 2022). Word of mouth has been proposed to
directly affect the customer to increase revisit intention. It is identified that when consumers are satisfied
with the services of their service experience, they would deliver and recommend to their friends and family
members to visit the same hotels again (Gontur et al., 2022). The perceived service quality has a direct
effect on behavioural intention, such as positive word of mouth (Gabbianelli & Pencarelli, 2023). Previous
studies explained that word-of-mouth is the primary cause of information in the decision of customer
repurchase intention. Words of mouth can be negative and positive. If consumers refer to other persons
about the products or services, it is called positive word of mouth; if consumers complain to further

H3: Perceived service quality has a positive impact on word of mouth.

Customer Satisfaction and Repurchase Intention
Attitude and satisfaction are the foremost antecedents of consumer loyalty and repurchase intention
(Nguyen et al., 2023). Customer satisfaction plays a very important role in the marketing context; if the
customer is satisfied with the service, it leads him towards repurchase in the future if the same service is
needed again (Tufahati et al., 2021). Miao et al. (2022) explained that satisfactions have a positive,
meaningful, important and direct effect on revisit objectives. Hui et al. (2025) investigate that there is
always a positive relationship between customer satisfaction and repurchase intention in the product and
service context and customer satisfaction is also linked with post-purchase behaviour of the customer
(Komara & Fathurahman, 2024). The service providers are always focusing on increasing the quality of
services to satisfy their customers, as a satisfied consumer goes towards loyalty (Lukman et al., 2024).
H4: There is a positive relationship between customer satisfaction and re-purchase intention.

Customer Satisfaction and Word of Mouth
Words of mouth impact the assumptions of customers; customers mostly deliver word of mouth, which he
has expectations of the services (Dangaiso et al., 2024). Words of mouth and repurchase intention can be
shown as a dimension of consumer loyalty (Sikombe, 2025). Kazungu & Kubenea (2023) presented that a
satisfaction platform is the subsequent of the distinction between expected performance and actual
performance. When the real levels of product or service are healthier than anticipated, then satisfaction
will be encouraging. Nasir et al. (2021) expressed that hotel industries can calculate customer satisfaction
by using guest remark cards. These cards are commonly used in welcome, accommodations and open areas
in the hotels for gathering customer comments. Some hotels give the customer a questionnaire to give
their comments in optimistic words of mouth or bad word of mouth and give them incentives for
completing the questionnaires. Through this method of getting the information from customers and
removing their areas of lacking service, it shows the accurate picture of expectations of consumer
fulfilment that are above or below (Karim & Rabiul, 2024). The hotel managers are always trying their best
to increase the service area to fulfil their consumers because greater customer satisfaction will lead to
customer loyalty and become the source of word of mouth (Marcos & Coelho 2021).
H5: Customer satisfaction has a positive impact on word of mouth.

Mediation Role of Customer Satisfaction between the Relationship of Perceived Service Quality
and Repurchase Intention
Fared et al. (2021) expressed that the key problem for hotel managers is to fascinate and retain customers.
Customers' emotions and revisit intention are playing the role of mediators of consumer satisfaction
(Soeharso, 2024). Satisfaction plays the role of a mediating variable between service quality and repurchase
intention (Mittal & Kaur, Mittal, 2024). In the Indian hoteling sectors, the service quality and customer
satisfaction suggest consumer loyalty (Iffan et al., 2024). In this way, Customer fulfilment and satisfaction
play the role of a mediating variable between service quality and consumer loyalty. Balakrishnan &
Christopher (2010) investigate that service quality has been observed as an antecedent of customer
fulfilment, which becomes the result of customer repurchase intention. Adi et al. (2010) exposed that
consumer satisfaction has a powerful mediating effect between service quality and consumer retention.
H6: Customer satisfaction mediates the relationship between perceived service quality and repurchase
intention.

Mediation Role of Customer Satisfaction between the Relationship of Perceived Service Quality
and Word of Mouth
Customer satisfaction plays a significant role in the relationship between service quality and word of
mouth. If a customer is satisfied with a particular service, it will work as a referral program for the service

Research Methodology
The research scrutinises the customer's perceived service quality regarding the output of repurchase
intentions and word-of-mouth. The population for this research consisted of customers of hotels in
Pakistan. All customers who visited the hotels were the population of this study. The investigation is taking
place in well-known 3-star, 4-star and 5-star hotels in Pakistan. The respondents are selected from the
different multination companies' employees, businessmen, guests and customers of the hotels. The
number of customers who visited these hotels was counted by carrying out an interview with the
representative of these hotels. The data was gathered from the hotels, like Pearl Continental Islamabad
hotel, Marriott hotel Islamabad, Pearl Continental Bhurban, Serena Faisalabad, Hotel One Lahore, Pearl
Continental Lahore, Pine Park hotel Naran, Green Valley hotel Rawalakot and Avari hotel Karachi. The
sample size was 204. The research instrument has been implemented from prior studies. Primary data was
collected through the self-administered questionnaire from the consumers of hotels in Pakistan. The
convenience sampling technique was used in this study. For analysing data, SPSS 21 was used. Perceived
service quality was measured by 29 items derived by Parasuraman et al. (1988). Customer satisfaction was
measured by three items derived by Maxham & Netemeyer (2002). Re-purchase intention was measured
by three items derived by Arnold & Reynolds (2003). Word of mouth was measured by four items derived
by Carroll & Ahuvia (2006). A Likert scale was used to analyse the respondents ' level of agreement or
disagreement with the subjective statement.

Practical Implications
Perceived service quality helps to promote the relationship with consumers and the service provider, by
encouraging the consumers' repurchase intentions and increasing the word of mouth. Particularly, the
hotel managers should work more to develop their business by using the techniques of providing good
service quality to their customers. In this way, their business becomes more developed. Hotel managers
should use the different kinds of techniques to improve their service quality dimensions. Both perceived
performance and overall service quality dimensions can become the cause of growth for the business. Hotel
managers should adopt the different positive consequences of consumers' company identification
techniques to reduce their acquisition cost and advertising cost. Service organisations are utilising the
advantages of the identification with their loyal customer, which becomes the source of reputation and
also becomes a positive output, like repurchasing intention and positive word of mouth. When the
relationship became strong with the service provider, then the customer became loyal, and loyal customers
become the key source of positive word of mouth and repurchase intention. Customers show their
attachment as loyal customers with the organisation by using the quality of the service. When consumers
got quality services from the hotel, the outcome was customer satisfaction, which enhances repurchase
behaviours, generates positive word-of-mouth and reduces the possibility of switching to competitors.

Limitations and Directions for Future Research
This study has some limitations, which are reflected as restrictions in the generalizability. Less financial
Support is also known as a limitation of this study. Since this study was conducted only in Pakistan, it is
also a major limitation of this study. In this research majority of the respondents were male and female
are less as combine with them. Mostly, females were hesitating and not ready to give the information or
response. The current research used the hypotheses with Pakistani hotels and used convenience sampling,
which can question the generalizability of the data. So, other sampling techniques can be used for future
research for more geographically and ethnically diverse populations. There are a limited number of
