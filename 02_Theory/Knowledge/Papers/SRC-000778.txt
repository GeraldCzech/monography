Yes, trust, commitment, and transparency are linked to donation support in
nonprofits, though mediation evidence is narrower.
The six questions cluster into trust and loyalty, integrity and transparency, and mediation and prosocial
outcomes.

Trust and Loyalty
Trust in a nonprofit brand generally predicts stronger donor commitment and support intentions, but direct
evidence on long-term behavioral loyalty is still thinner than evidence on attitudes and intentions (Millán et al.,
2023; Kutlu & Özcan, 2024).
• Trust predicts intention to donate in nonprofit samples (Kutlu & Özcan, 2024; Ha et al., 2022).
• Brand trust is treated as a core equity dimension alongside awareness and commitment in nonprofit brand

equity models (Romero & Abril, 2023; Hommerová et al., 2025).
• Commitment is usually the more relevant loyalty analogue in nonprofits because donors often support multiple

organizations, so attitudinal and emotional attachment matter more than exclusive repeat behavior (Romero et
al., 2023).

Integrity and Transparency
Perceived integrity appears to build trust because donors use moral and accountability cues to judge nonprofits
they cannot directly monitor (Venable et al., 2005; Romero & Abril, 2023). Transparency works similarly by reducing
uncertainty about how donations are used, although most evidence is cross-sectional.
• In nonprofit brand personality research, integrity and reliability emerged as central trust-related traits (Venable

et al., 2005).
• Perceived financial transparency is positively associated with donor trust and perceived nonprofit performance

(Ghoorah et al., 2025).
• Donors want clearer information on use of contributions, and communication about how donations help people

in need explains substantial variance in trust and commitment (Hommerová et al., 2025; O’Neil, 2009).

Mediation and Prosocial Outcomes
Brand trust does mediate some paths to donation intention, but the evidence is for specific upstream brand
constructs rather than a single unified “brand equity → trust → donation” pathway (Kutlu & Özcan, 2024; Ha et al.,
2022). Organizational commitment also aligns with broader prosocial support, including retention, advocacy, and
additional giving.
