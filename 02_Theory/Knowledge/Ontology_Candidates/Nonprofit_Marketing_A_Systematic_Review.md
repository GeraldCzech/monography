# Concept

ArchitecturalQuality

## Canonical Definition

Architectural Quality refers to the set of properties of a Brand-Enabled Behaviour Architecture (BEBA) that determine its effectiveness in converting stakeholder psychological states (e.g., trust, commitment) into desired behaviours (e.g., donations, volunteering). It is distinct from the mere existence or scale of the architecture, focusing instead on the qualitative aspects of its configuration and use.

## Alternative Definitions

Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature as a holistic concept. However, it is related to and builds upon more specific concepts such as:
*   **Dialogic Communication:** (cf. Kent & Taylor, 1998) The principles of dialogue in mediated communication, often applied to websites and social media.
*   **User Experience (UX):** A user's perceptions and responses resulting from the use or anticipated use of a product, system or service.
*   **Stakeholder Centricity:** The degree to which an organization's strategies and operations are designed around understanding and meeting the needs of its key stakeholders.

## BEBA Layer

Organisational. Architectural Quality is a property of the architecture that an organization designs and implements. Its effects are observed at the Individual layer (stakeholder behaviour), but its origin and control reside at the Organisational layer.

## Core Claims

*   **C-BEBA-011: The Architectural Quality Principle.** The behavioural effectiveness of a nonprofit brand is a function of not just the existence of its architecture (channels, platforms) but the *quality* of its configuration and use.
*   The conversion of stakeholder Trust into resource-providing Behaviour is positively moderated by Architectural Quality.

## Evidence Base

The concept is a theoretical synthesis derived from the analysis of REVIEW-000073. Specific supporting claims include:
*   **SM-000005:** The use of social media for dialogic communication (a component of high Architectural Quality) is associated with higher levels of stakeholder engagement.
*   **MO-000001:** Market Orientation (a driver of Architectural Quality) is a significant predictor of nonprofit performance.
*   **TR-000024:** The link between trust and donation is inconsistent, implying the existence of moderators, for which Architectural Quality is a primary candidate.

## Boundary Conditions

*   The initial evidence base is heavily weighted towards social media as the architectural channel. The concept's applicability to other channels (e.g., direct mail, events) needs further investigation.
*   The primary outcome behaviour discussed in the evidence base is donations. The moderating effect of Architectural Quality may differ for other behaviours (e.g., advocacy, volunteering, co-creation).

## Competing Explanations

*   **Brand Strength Primacy:** A competing view would be that a sufficiently strong brand can overcome a low-quality architecture, making architecture a secondary, rather than critical, factor.
*   **Channel Reach Primacy:** Another competing view is that the quantity (reach, frequency) of communication is more important than its quality (dialogic nature, centricity).

## Operationalisations

Architectural Quality is likely a second-order, formative construct. It could be operationalised by measuring its first-order dimensions:
1.  **Communication Mode:** Content analysis of organizational communications (e.g., social media posts, emails) coded for dialogic loops and interactive features.
2.  **Stakeholder Centricity:** Stakeholder surveys measuring perceptions of whether the organization listens, responds, and adapts to their needs.
3.  **Channel Integration:** Expert audit of the seamlessness of the stakeholder journey across different organizational touchpoints.

## Existing Scales

No known existing scale measures the holistic concept of `ArchitecturalQuality`. However, validated scales exist for related child concepts, such as:
*   Scales for Dialogic Communication on websites and social media (e.g., based on Kent & Taylor's principles).
*   Scales for Customer/Stakeholder Orientation.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Architectural Quality:** A property of an organization's Brand-Enabled Behaviour Architecture. It describes the effectiveness of the architecture's configuration and use in converting stakeholder psychological states, like trust, into desired behaviours. High-quality architectures are typically more dialogic, stakeholder-centric, and integrated.

## Confidence

Medium-Theoretical. The concept provides a strong theoretical explanation for inconsistent findings in the literature (e.g., the trust-behaviour link). However, it is a new synthesis and has not yet been subjected to direct empirical operationalisation and testing within the BEBA framework.

## Review Recommendation

Accept for formal development. The concept of `ArchitecturalQuality` is a cornerstone implication from the literature review. It directly addresses the "how" in the core research question. Prioritize the development of a measurement model for this construct and include it in the next wave of empirical model competition, specifically as a moderator of the Trust -> Behaviour relationship.
