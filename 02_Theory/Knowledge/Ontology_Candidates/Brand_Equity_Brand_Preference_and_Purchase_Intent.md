# Concept

Brand Equity, Brand Preference, and Purchase Intent

## Canonical Definition

Brand equity, in this candidate sense, refers to a cognitive and evaluative brand structure that can influence behavioural precursors such as preference and purchase or support intention, especially when alternatives are functionally similar or difficult to evaluate.

For BEBA, this concept should be treated as a candidate mechanism by which brand-related structures become behaviourally effective under uncertainty, rather than as a universal or context-independent driver of behaviour.

## Alternative Definitions

- Consumer-based brand equity as a consumer-side structure that increases preference for one brand over otherwise similar alternatives.
- Brand equity as a set of favourable, strong, and relevant brand associations that shape intention.
- Brand equity as a simplifying decision architecture that helps individuals form preference or intention under uncertainty.
- Brand name as a heuristic cue that may influence choice without requiring full attribute evaluation.

## BEBA Layer

Primary layer: Individual Layer

Relevant mechanisms:


## Core Claims

1. Long-term communication or advertising investment is positively associated with consumer-based brand equity among objectively similar brands.

2. Higher consumer-based brand equity can generate greater consumer preference than functionally similar lower-equity brands.

3. Higher consumer-based brand equity can generate stronger purchase intentions than functionally similar lower-equity brands.

4. Brand name can influence consumer preference as a distinct attribute in multi-attribute choice contexts.

5. Brand-name effects may be stronger in low-involvement contexts and weaker in higher-involvement or higher-risk contexts.

6. Advertising investment is associated with more favourable brand association structures among objectively similar brands.

7. For BEBA, brand equity should be theorised as a behaviour-enabling architecture whose effects vary by decision context, uncertainty, perceived risk, involvement, and availability of alternative evaluative cues.

Evidence supports the theoretical plausibility that brand-related cognitive structures influence preference and intention.

Evidence limitations:

- The reviewed claims concern consumer-based brand equity, brand names, advertising expenditure, and brand associations.
- The evidence does not directly establish nonprofit donation, volunteering, advocacy, or support behaviour.
- Advertising expenditure is a proxy for communication investment and should not be treated as equivalent to communication quality or causal communication effectiveness.
- Causal claims should be avoided unless supported by stronger evidence.

Nonprofit transfer status:

Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature.

## Boundary Conditions

- Involvement: Brand-name and brand-equity effects may be stronger in low-involvement contexts.
- Perceived decision risk: Higher perceived risk may reduce reliance on simple brand-name cues and increase reliance on substantive evidence.
- Category type: Product-category findings may not transfer directly to services or nonprofit organisations.
- Functional similarity: Brand equity may matter more when alternatives are difficult to distinguish through objective attributes.
- Communication quality: Expenditure alone does not capture credibility, consistency, message content, or channel effectiveness.
- Availability of alternative cues: Impact evidence, accountability information, social proof, trust signals, or prior experience may compete with brand-based cues.
- Nonprofit uncertainty: Donation use, organisational effectiveness, mission legitimacy, and moral allocation risk may alter the role of brand equity.

4. Substantive evidence pathway

```text
Impact evidence / accountability / trust information
→ perceived credibility
→ support intention
```

5. Involvement- or risk-moderated pathway

```text
Brand equity → support intention
is moderated by involvement, perceived risk, or uncertainty
```

6. Familiarity or salience bypass pathway

```text
Name recognition / salience
→ heuristic preference
→ behavioural intention
```

This pathway may bypass richer brand-equity structures such as trust, identification, perceived quality, or mission association.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Brand equity as behavioural precursor architecture**

Brand equity as behavioural precursor architecture refers to the role of brand-related cognitive and evaluative structures in shaping preference, intention, or behavioural readiness. In BEBA, this concept is treated as context-sensitive: its influence depends on uncertainty, involvement, perceived risk, functional similarity among alternatives, and the availability of alternative decision cues.

**Brand name as heuristic**

Brand name as heuristic refers to the use of a brand or organisation name as a simplifying decision cue that can influence preference or intention without detailed attribute evaluation.

**Communication investment**

Communication investment refers to sustained resource allocation to brand-related communication activities that may be associated with the development of brand associations and consumer-based brand equity. Advertising expenditure may be one proxy but should not be treated as equivalent to the construct.
