# Concept

Nonprofit Brand Image

## Canonical Definition

**Candidate definition:** Nonprofit brand image is a multidimensional stakeholder perception of a nonprofit organization that includes evaluations of its usefulness, efficiency, affective appeal, dynamism, reliability, and ethicality, and that may shape supportive behavioural intentions under conditions of uncertainty.

## Alternative Definitions

- Nonprofit brand image as a general favourable or unfavourable evaluation of a nonprofit organization.
- Nonprofit brand image as a set of stakeholder associations attached to a nonprofit brand.
- Nonprofit brand image as a perceptual antecedent of donation and volunteering intentions.
- Nonprofit brand image as a trust-relevant perceptual structure composed partly of reliability and ethicality perceptions.

## Boundary Conditions

- Evidence is limited primarily to the UK children’s charity context.
- Cross-national validity is not sufficiently covered.
- Transferability to other nonprofit subsectors is not established.
- Outcomes are self-reported intentions, not observed behaviours.
- The uncertainty-reduction mechanism is theoretically plausible but not directly tested.
- The 18-item six-factor scale is a candidate instrument, not a canonical BEBA scale.
- Discriminant validity against trust, reputation, legitimacy, authenticity, attitude, and credibility requires further review or testing.

## Competing Explanations

1. **Global Image Model**  
   Nonprofit brand image operates as a single overall favourable evaluation.

2. **Four-Factor Image Model**  
   Nonprofit brand image can be captured by the earlier Michel and Rieunier four-factor structure.

3. **Six-Factor Image Model**  
   Nonprofit brand image consists of usefulness, efficiency, affect, dynamism, reliability, and ethicality.

4. **Affect-Centred Model**  
   Supportive intentions are mainly driven by emotional liking or affective appeal.

5. **Trust-Relevant Image Model**  
   Supportive intentions are shaped by reliability, ethicality, and other trust-relevant perceptions under uncertainty.

6. **Direct Image Effect Model**  
   Brand image directly predicts donation and volunteering intention.

7. **Mediated Uncertainty-Reduction Model**  
   Reliability and ethicality reduce stakeholder uncertainty, which then enables supportive behavioural intention or behaviour.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Nonprofit Brand Image**  
A stakeholder’s multidimensional perception of a nonprofit organization, potentially including usefulness, efficiency, affect, dynamism, reliability, and ethicality. In BEBA, nonprofit brand image is treated as a candidate behaviour-enabling perceptual architecture rather than merely a general favourable evaluation. Current evidence links it to donation and volunteering intentions, while causal effects on observed behaviour and mediation through uncertainty reduction require further validation.
