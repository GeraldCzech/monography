# Concept

Brand Orientation

## Canonical Definition

A canonical definition must be extracted from the source literature (referenced in `REVIEW-000082`). Based on the provided synthesis, a working definition is:

> The internal strategic posture and set of organizational activities that prioritize the creation and maintenance of brand value, acting as an antecedent to external brand equity and organizational performance.

## Boundary Conditions

- The relationship between Brand Orientation and performance may be moderated by the NPO's specific sub-sector (e.g., advocacy vs. service delivery).
- The effectiveness of a brand orientation may depend on sufficient organizational resources to execute a brand strategy.

## Competing Explanations

- A pure **Market Orientation** framework, which may not explicitly prioritize the brand as the central integrating mechanism.
- A pure **Mission Orientation** framework, where organizational behaviour is driven solely by mission fulfillment, potentially neglecting strategic brand management. BEBA's premise is that these are not mutually exclusive and that a Brand Orientation can be the vehicle for a Mission Orientation.

## Operationalisations

The construct is operationalized as a latent variable measured via psychometric scales, typically capturing managerial and employee attitudes, priorities, and brand-centric behaviours.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Brand Orientation**: An organizational-level strategic posture where the brand is the central principle guiding the organization's actions, culture, and strategy. It represents a commitment to building and leveraging brand equity as a primary driver of achieving the organization's mission and performance goals.
