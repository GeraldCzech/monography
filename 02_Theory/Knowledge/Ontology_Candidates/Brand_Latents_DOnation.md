# Concept

**Brand Latents in Donation**  
Alternative label: **Brand-enabled donation mechanisms**

## Canonical Definition

Brand latents in donation are individual-level brand-related perceptions and relationship states that make nonprofit donation behaviour more likely by increasing cognitive availability, positive evaluation, uncertainty reduction, identity congruence, and relational durability.

## Alternative Definitions

- A set of nonprofit brand equity mechanisms that influence donation intention, propensity to donate, sustained support, and bequest intention.
- The latent perceptual and relational dimensions through which nonprofit brands become behaviourally effective.
- A multi-route architecture of brand effects on giving behaviour.

## Core Claims

1. Nonprofit brands enable donation behaviour through multiple latent mechanisms rather than through a single general attitude.
2. Cognitive availability mechanisms, including awareness, salience, and familiarity, increase the likelihood that a nonprofit enters the donor’s consideration set.
3. Evaluative mechanisms, including brand image and brand personality, improve donor evaluation and contribution likelihood.
4. Trust-related mechanisms reduce perceived uncertainty in giving, but their effects are conditional on familiarity, measurement context, donor stage, and behavioural outcome.
5. Self-congruence enables donation by making giving behaviour identity-expressive.
6. Brand commitment supports more durable support behaviours, including sustained support and possibly bequest-related intentions.
7. Organisational brand signals such as digital presence, social media marketing, and heritage become behaviourally effective mainly through individual perceptions such as image, trust, and transparency.
8. Extrinsic monetary incentives may weaken some brand-enabled motivational pathways.

## Boundary Conditions

- **Donor lifecycle stage:** Salience may matter more for new donors; commitment and transparency may matter more for existing supporters, volunteers, or bequest prospects.
- **Behavioural outcome type:** Donation intention, time donation, sustained support, and bequest intention may depend on different mechanisms.
- **Familiarity:** Familiarity may compete with or precede trust-related constructs.
- **Trust versus trustworthiness:** These should not yet be collapsed.
- **Incentive context:** Extrinsic monetary incentives may disrupt intrinsic, affective, or identity-based brand effects.
- **Measurement specification:** Effects may vary depending on whether constructs are measured as trust, trustworthiness, awareness, familiarity, salience, image, or commitment.

## Competing Explanations

1. **Direct-effects brand equity model:** Brand perceptions directly predict donation intention.
2. **Trust-mediated model:** Brand-building activities affect donation through brand trust.
3. **Cognitive availability model:** Giving depends on awareness, familiarity, and salience.
4. **Identity-congruence model:** Giving is enabled by self-brand fit.
5. **Relationship-commitment model:** Durable support depends on commitment and relationship depth.
6. **Organisational signal-processing model:** Organisational brand inputs affect behaviour through individual perception formation.
7. **Incentive-disruption model:** Extrinsic incentives weaken brand-enabled motivation.

**Medium-high** for the general multi-mechanism architecture.  
**Medium** for specific mechanism dominance by donor stage or behavioural outcome.  
**Medium-low** for any claim that one mechanism, especially trust, is universally primary.
