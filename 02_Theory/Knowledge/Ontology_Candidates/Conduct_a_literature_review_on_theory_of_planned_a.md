# Concept

Intention-Behavior Gap

## Canonical Definition

The observable discrepancy between an individual's stated intention to perform a specific behavior and their subsequent actual performance or non-performance of that behavior. In the BEBA context, this refers specifically to the failure of positive donation intentions to translate into actual donation behavior.

## Alternative Definitions

- Intention-Action Gap
- Value-Action Gap (a broader, related concept)

## BEBA Layer

- **Primary:** Individual Layer. The gap is a cognitive and behavioral phenomenon occurring within an individual.
- **Secondary:** Organisational Layer. The *causes* of and *solutions* for the gap are often organisational (e.g., a complex donation process creating an environmental constraint). BEBA posits that the nonprofit brand is a key organisational mechanism for bridging this gap.

## Parent Concepts

- BehavioralInconsistency
- ModelPredictionError

## Child Concepts

- DonationIntentionBehaviorGap (proposed specific instance)

## Related Concepts

- `BehavioralIntention` (The antecedent)
- `Behavior` (The outcome)
- `Theory of Planned Behavior` (A model where the gap is a known limitation)
- `Integrated Behavioral Model` (A model that explicitly includes post-intentional factors to help explain the gap)
- `EnvironmentalConstraints` (A key post-intentional factor contributing to the gap)
- `Skills` (A key post-intentional factor contributing to the gap)
- `BEBA` (The theoretical framework proposed to bridge the gap)

## Core Claims

- `IB-000026`: The intention-behavior gap is significant in donation contexts.
- `C-BEBA-001` (Candidate Claim): Nonprofit Brand Equity acts as a behavioral architecture that primarily bridges the intention-behavior gap by shaping post-intentional factors (e.g., skills, environmental constraints) as specified in the Integrated Behavioral Model.

## Evidence Base

The existence and significance of the gap in prosocial and donation contexts are supported by high-confidence evidence from `REVIEW-000103`. It is described as a "well-documented" phenomenon.

## Boundary Conditions

The *existence* of the gap is a general phenomenon. However, the *size* of the gap and the effectiveness of mechanisms to bridge it are subject to boundary conditions:
- **Behavioral Complexity:** The gap is likely larger for high-barrier or complex behaviors where Perceived Behavioral Control is lower (derived from `PB-000030`).
- **Cultural Context:** The effectiveness of social-norm-based interventions to close the gap may be moderated by cultural factors like individualism vs. collectivism (derived from `SN-000008`).

## Competing Explanations

While the BEBA framework focuses on post-intentional factors like `EnvironmentalConstraints` and `Skills`, other explanations for the gap exist:
- **Unstable Intentions:** Individuals simply change their minds between the measurement of intention and the opportunity for behavior.
- **Measurement Error:** The measures of intention or behavior are not valid or reliable, creating an artificial gap.
- **Methodological Artifacts:** The time lag between measuring intention and behavior is too long.

## Operationalisations

The gap is operationalised as the quantified difference between a measure of behavioral intention and a measure of actual behavior. This can be:
- A simple percentage of "intenders" who fail to act.
- The amount of variance in `Behavior` that is not explained by `BehavioralIntention` in a regression model.

## Existing Scales

Not applicable. The Intention-Behavior Gap is not a latent construct measured with a scale. It is an observed phenomenon resulting from the relationship (or lack thereof) between two other measured constructs (Intention and Behavior).

## Recommended ConstructLedger Updates

It is recommended to add `IntentionBehaviorGap` to the `ConstructLedger` not as a measurable construct, but as a core "Phenomenon" that the BEBA research programme aims to explain. This clarifies its central role in the overall theoretical model.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Intention-Behavior Gap:** The common failure of individuals' stated intentions to translate into corresponding actions. In the nonprofit context, it represents the gap between someone intending to donate or support a cause and them actually doing so.

## Confidence

High. The source document identifies claim `IB-000026` as being supported by "high-confidence evidence" and describes the phenomenon as "well-documented".

## Review Recommendation

**Accept and integrate.** The Intention-Behavior Gap is identified as the central problem the BEBA framework seeks to explain and solve. Its formal inclusion in the ontology is essential for grounding the entire theory.
