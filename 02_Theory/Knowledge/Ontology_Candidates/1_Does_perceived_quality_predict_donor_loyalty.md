# Concept

Behavioural Amplification

## Canonical Definition

The process by which brand-related cognitive structures (e.g., Brand Credibility, Trust) increase the probability of converting positive stakeholder experiences and psychological states (e.g., Perceived Quality, Satisfaction) into desired supportive behaviours (e.g., repeat support, positive word-of-mouth).

## Alternative Definitions

Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature. The concept is a BEBA-specific synthesis. It is analogous to "catalysis" in chemistry or "moderation" in statistics.

## BEBA Layer

**Individual Layer:** The effect occurs within the individual stakeholder's decision process.
**Organisational Layer (Enabler):** The effect is enabled by the organisational brand architecture which builds the cognitive structures (e.g., credibility) that facilitate amplification.

## Core Claims

-   **C-BEBA-011 (Candidate):** The primary mechanism through which nonprofit brands enable supportive behaviour is by amplifying the conversion of positive service experiences (quality, value, satisfaction) into behavioural intentions and actions.
-   **C-BEBA-012 (Candidate):** Brand Credibility acts as a positive moderator on the relationship between stakeholder satisfaction and subsequent supportive behaviours (e.g., repeat support, positive WOM).

## Evidence Base

The primary evidence is inferential, derived from `REVIEW-000095`. Specifically, the combination of claim `BC-000042` (Brand Credibility moderates the Service Quality -> Behaviour link) and `BE-000063` (lack of evidence for a direct Brand Equity -> Perceived Quality link) suggests that the brand's role is not as a direct antecedent but as a moderator of an existing causal chain. This review challenges a simple signaling model and supports an amplification model.

## Boundary Conditions

-   The effect may be contingent on **stakeholder type**, potentially being stronger for stakeholders who are also direct service consumers (as per `PV-000003`).
-   The strength of the underlying `Satisfaction -> Behaviour` link that is being amplified is **context-dependent** and can be weak in some sectors (as noted in review of `S-000011`, `S-000012`).

## Competing Explanations

1.  **Signaling Model:** Brand acts as a direct signal of quality (`Brand Equity -> Perceived Quality`). This is challenged by claim `BE-000063`.
2.  **Service-Logic Model (Baseline):** Stakeholder behaviour is explained entirely by the non-brand chain: `Perceived Quality -> Perceived Value -> Satisfaction -> Behaviour`.
3.  **Relational Bypass Model:** Loyalty is driven by interpersonal `Relationship Quality` independent of brand or service quality perceptions.

## Operationalisations

The amplification mechanism is operationalized as a statistical **interaction effect** in a structural equation model or moderated regression analysis. For example:
`Supportive Behaviour = β1(Satisfaction) + β2(Brand Credibility) + β3(Satisfaction * Brand Credibility) + ε`.
Evidence for amplification would be a statistically significant and positive coefficient for the interaction term (`β3`).

## Existing Scales

There are no direct scales for "Behavioural Amplification" as it is a process, not a perceptual construct. It is tested using established scales for its constituent components, such as those for Satisfaction, Brand Credibility, and measures of Supportive Behaviour.

## Recommended ConstructLedger Updates

No new construct entry for "Behavioural Amplification" is required. Instead, the `ConstructLedger` should be reviewed to ensure that constructs identified as potential **amplifiers** (e.g., Brand Credibility, Brand Trust) have their proposed role updated to include moderation.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Behavioural Amplification:** In the BEBA framework, the theoretical mechanism by which brand architecture strengthens the link between positive stakeholder experiences (e.g., satisfaction) and subsequent supportive behaviours (e.g., loyalty, advocacy). It represents the brand's role as a catalyst for behaviour rather than a direct antecedent of quality perception.

## Confidence

High. This concept emerges directly from a systematic synthesis of evidence (`REVIEW-000095`) and provides a clear, testable, and theoretically valuable direction for the BEBA research programme.

## Review Recommendation

High Priority. This concept represents a core theoretical hypothesis for BEBA, distinguishing it from simpler signaling or service-logic models. It requires manual review and formal adoption into the core theory.
