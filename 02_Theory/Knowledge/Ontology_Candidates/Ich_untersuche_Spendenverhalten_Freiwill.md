# Concept

Habitus

## Canonical Definition

A system of durable, transposable dispositions acquired through an individual's life experiences and position within the social structure. Habitus functions as a "structured structure, predisposed to function as a structuring structure," generating practices, perceptions, and appreciations (taste) that are pre-reflexive and feel 'natural' to the agent. Within BEBA, it represents the embodied, sociological foundation that precedes and shapes the psychological drivers of supporter behaviour.

## Alternative Definitions

-   **Cognitive Psychology Analogue:** A complex system of interconnected schemas, implicit attitudes, and cognitive scripts that guide perception and behaviour automatically. This view often misses the sociological dimension of class and social reproduction.
-   **Behavioural Economics Analogue:** A set of deeply ingrained heuristics and biases that constitute an individual's 'System 1' or 'fast' thinking, shaped by their socio-economic environment.
-   **Lay Definition:** The collection of habits, instincts, and ways of seeing the world that a person picks up from their upbringing, education, and social environment, guiding their choices without them having to think about it.

## BEBA Layer

**Individual Layer.** Habitus is an attribute of the individual agent. However, it is the primary theoretical bridge connecting the **Societal Layer** (social structures, class, capital distribution) to the individual's behaviour.

## Related Concepts

-   `Capital` (Economic, Social, Cultural, Symbolic): Habitus is the embodiment of an individual's history and capital portfolio.
-   `Field`: Habitus is activated and has meaning within a specific social field (e.g., the 'philanthropic field').
-   `Attitude` (from TPB/TRA): Claim `H-000005` posits that Habitus shapes an individual's attitudes towards a behaviour.
-   `Subjective Norms` (from TPB/TRA): Habitus shapes an individual's perception of social norms.
-   `Perceived Behavioural Control` (from TPB/TRA): Habitus, through embodied experience, shapes an individual's sense of what is possible or 'for them'.

## Core Claims

-   `H-000004`: Supporter behaviour is generated by the supporter's habitus, which reflects their position in social space, making behaviour a pre-reflexive practice rather than a purely conscious choice.
-   `H-000005`: Habitus provides the generative foundation for the antecedents of intention (Attitudes, Norms, Perceived Control) in theories like the Theory of Planned Behaviour.
-   `BC-000043`: Nonprofit brand choice is a manifestation of 'taste', which is an expression of habitus.
-   `BEBA-Social-Antecedents`: The psychological antecedents of brand equity are themselves shaped and constrained by an individual supporter's habitus.

## Evidence Base

The primary evidence base is sociological, rooted in the work of Pierre Bourdieu (e.g., *Distinction*, *Outline of a Theory of Practice*). Its application to consumer behaviour is well-established in the field of Consumer Culture Theory (CCT). Its application to nonprofit supporter behaviour is a theoretical extension proposed by `REVIEW-000109`. The specific claim linking Habitus to TPB antecedents (`H-000005`) is noted as a synthesis with lower direct evidence and requires further investigation.

## Boundary Conditions

-   **Novelty vs. Routine:** The explanatory power of Habitus is strongest for recurring, established practices (e.g., sustained giving) and weaker for novel, high-deliberation behaviours (e.g., a first-time donation to an emergency appeal).
-   **Social Position:** The specific manifestations of Habitus (i.e., the practices and tastes it generates) are conditional on an individual's volume and composition of capital.

## Competing Explanations

Habitus is the core construct in a practice-based explanation of behaviour, which competes with and can be integrated into cognitive-intentional models.
-   **Rational Choice / Cognitive Models:** These models omit Habitus, positing that behaviour is primarily driven by conscious deliberation, intentions, and utility calculations.
-   **Pure Behaviourism:** These models focus only on stimulus-response and reinforcement, ignoring both the cognitive and the embodied social history captured by Habitus.
-   **BEBA's Integrated Model:** `REVIEW-000109` proposes a dual-process model where Habitus acts as a foundational antecedent to cognitive constructs, providing a more comprehensive explanation than either a purely sociological or purely psychological model alone.

## Operationalisations

Direct measurement of Habitus is notoriously difficult and a subject of methodological debate. It is typically operationalised indirectly through proxies that capture an individual's social trajectory and position:
-   **Capital Portfolio:** Measuring an individual's economic (income, wealth), cultural (education level, parental education, cultural consumption), and social (network ties) capital.
-   **Social Trajectory:** Capturing inter-generational mobility (e.g., comparing parental and individual occupation/education).
-   **Latent Class Analysis (LCA):** Using indicators of practices, tastes, and capital to statistically identify clusters representing different habitus formations.

## Existing Scales

There are no widely validated, direct psychometric scales for Habitus as a holistic construct. Measurement relies on the proxy-based approaches described under Operationalisations.

## Recommended ConstructLedger Updates

-   **Create New Entry: `Habitus`**. Classify as a core theoretical construct. Note that measurement is typically indirect via proxies for `Capital` and `Social Position`.
-   **Create New Entries: `EconomicCapital`, `CulturalCapital`, `SocialCapital`**. These are necessary to operationalise `Habitus` and model the social space.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Habitus:** A system of lasting, transposable dispositions through which individuals perceive, judge, and act in the world. Acquired through personal history and social position, it functions as an embodied, pre-conscious generator of practices and tastes.

## Confidence

-   **Theoretical Coherence:** High. The concept is deeply rooted in a major sociological paradigm.
-   **Empirical Tractability:** Low to Medium. Operationalisation is complex and indirect.
-   **Integration into BEBA:** Medium. The proposed synthesis (`H-000005`) that links Habitus to existing cognitive constructs is a key theoretical step but requires significant further evidence and testing.

## Review Recommendation

Accept as a foundational candidate concept for the BEBA ontology. Prioritise the development of related candidates (`Capital`, `Field`, `Practice`) to enable the formal specification of competing explanatory models as outlined in `REVIEW-000109`. This concept is essential for advancing BEBA towards a dual-process architecture that incorporates sociological explanations of behaviour.
