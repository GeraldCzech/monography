# Concept

Perceived Efficacy

## Canonical Definition

An individual's belief in a nonprofit organisation's capability to effectively address and solve the social or environmental problem it purports to tackle. It is a cognitive judgment about the organisation's competence and impact.

## Alternative Definitions

-   **Response Efficacy (Health Psychology)**: An individual's belief that a recommended action or behaviour will be effective in averting a threat (Witte, 1992). In the nonprofit context, this would be the belief that donating to the NPO is an effective action to address the social problem. BEBA's Perceived Efficacy focuses on the *organisation's* capability, which is a direct antecedent to this belief.
-   **Perceived Organisational Effectiveness**: An umbrella term for an organisation's ability to achieve its goals. Perceived Efficacy is the specific perception of this effectiveness held by an external stakeholder (e.g., a donor).

## BEBA Layer

Individual Layer. It is a perception held by an individual about an organisation.

## Parent Concepts

-   Cognitive Judgment
-   Belief
-   Brand-Related Perception

## Child Concepts

-   Perceived Programmatic Efficacy (Belief in the effectiveness of the NPO's specific interventions)
-   Perceived Financial Efficacy (Belief in the NPO's efficient use of donated funds to create impact)

## Related Concepts

-   **Brand Differentiation**: Proposed as a key antecedent. A well-differentiated brand may signal a unique, and therefore more effective, approach to problem-solving.
-   **Brand Trust**: Perceived Efficacy is a component of, or a key antecedent to, Brand Trust. Trust is broader, encompassing integrity and benevolence, while efficacy is specifically about competence and impact. BEBA must clarify this relationship.
-   **Actual Donation Behaviour**: Proposed as a key consequent. Perceived Efficacy is hypothesised to be a direct driver of behaviour.
-   **Self-Efficacy**: Distinguished from Perceived Efficacy. Self-Efficacy is the individual's belief in their *own* ability to make a difference through their donation. The two are likely related (e.g., high organisational efficacy may boost an individual's self-efficacy).
-   **Brand Image**: A broader set of associations about the brand. Perceived Efficacy is a specific, performance-related association within the brand image.

## Core Claims

-   **BD-000012**: The effect of perceived brand differentiation on actual donation behaviour is mediated by the individual's perceived efficacy of the nonprofit.
-   Perceived Efficacy is conceptually distinct from the benevolence and integrity dimensions of Brand Trust.
-   An individual's Perceived Efficacy for a nonprofit has a stronger direct effect on *actual behaviour* than on *behavioural intention*, as it can act as a justification for action that bypasses deliberative intention formation.

## Evidence Base

The concept is well-established in nonprofit marketing and donor behaviour literature.

-   Bendapudi, Singh, & Bendapudi (1996) identify perceived effectiveness as a key factor in enhancing helping behaviour.
-   Shang & Sargeant (2018) found that perceived effectiveness was a significant driver of individual donation behaviour, operating alongside moral satisfaction and social influence.
-   The specific link from *Brand Differentiation* to Perceived Efficacy, as proposed in claim `BD-000012`, is a core BEBA hypothesis requiring direct empirical testing within the project's pipeline.

## Boundary Conditions

The causal impact of Perceived Efficacy on donation behaviour may be moderated by:

-   **Problem Scope**: The effect may be stronger for large-scale, complex problems (e.g., climate change, systemic poverty) where organisational competence is more critical than for smaller, local issues.
-   **Donor Mindset**: The effect may be stronger for donors with a more analytical or consequentialist mindset ("what works") versus those with a more deontological or emotional mindset ("the right thing to do").
-   **Information Environment**: The effect is contingent on the availability of credible information about an NPO's impact. In its absence, donors may rely on other heuristics (e.g., brand awareness, trust).

## Competing Explanations

The effect attributed to Perceived Efficacy could be explained by other mechanisms:

-   **Trust-as-Mediator**: The link between Brand Differentiation and Behaviour is fully mediated by general Brand Trust, with Perceived Efficacy being merely a sub-component of trust with no independent explanatory power.
-   **Affective Identity**: A differentiated brand creates a stronger emotional bond and sense of identity for the donor. The subsequent behaviour is driven by this affective connection and identity expression, not a cognitive judgment of efficacy.
-   **Cognitive Fluency**: A differentiated brand is simply easier to process and remember, and this fluency is misattributed as a signal of quality or effectiveness, without a deep cognitive judgment taking place.

## Operationalisations

Measurement via multi-item scales using Likert-type responses. Example items:

-   "[NPO Name] is highly effective at achieving its mission."
-   "I believe that [NPO Name] makes a significant positive impact in its field."
-   "Donations to [NPO Name] are put to good use to solve social problems."
-   "Compared to other organisations, [NPO Name] has a superior approach to tackling the issue."

## Existing Scales

Scales for perceived organisational effectiveness and related concepts exist and should be reviewed for adaptation.

-   See scales used in studies like Shang & Sargeant (2018) or Webb, Green, & Brashear (2000).
-   A specific scale for *brand-driven* perceived efficacy may need to be developed and validated as part of the BEBA project.
