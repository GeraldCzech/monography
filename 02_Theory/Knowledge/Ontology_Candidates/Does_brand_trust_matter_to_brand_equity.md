# Concept
Supporter Trust

## Canonical Definition
A supporter's confident belief in a nonprofit's reliability and intentions. This reflects the supporter's willingness to be vulnerable to the organization's actions based on the expectation that it will perform a particular action important to the supporter, irrespective of the supporter's ability to monitor or control the organization. It is comprised of two core dimensions:
1.  **Reliability**: The belief that the organization is competent, effective, and dependable in fulfilling its promises and executing its mission.
2.  **Intentions**: The belief that the organization is acting with integrity and benevolence, demonstrating genuine concern for its beneficiaries and supporters.

## Alternative Definitions
-   **Unidimensional Trust**: Some models treat trust as a single, holistic construct without distinct dimensions, often termed "overall trust" or "credibility".
-   **Affective vs. Cognitive Trust**: A distinction is sometimes made between trust based on rational calculation (cognitive) and trust based on feelings and emotional connection (affective). The proposed canonical definition primarily focuses on the cognitive-evaluative dimension, though affect may influence it.
-   **Ability, Benevolence, Integrity (ABI) Model**: A widely used model defining trust through three facets: Ability (similar to reliability), Benevolence, and Integrity (both captured under Intentions).

## Related Concepts
-   **Supporter Satisfaction / Perceived Supporter Value**: A primary antecedent. Positive experiences with the NPO build trust.
-   **Supporter Loyalty**: A primary consequent. Trust fosters commitment and sustained supportive behaviour.
-   **Perceived Risk / Uncertainty**: A key moderator. Trust becomes more salient and valuable in situations where outcomes are uncertain or the NPO's actions are not fully transparent.
-   **Nonprofit Brand Equity (BEBA)**: A distal consequent. Trust contributes to BEBA through its influence on loyalty.

## Core Claims
-   `C-BEBA-SUP-001`: Supporter Trust is a bi-dimensional construct comprising a reliability (competence) dimension and an intentions (integrity/benevolence) dimension. (Based on BT-000021)
-   `C-BEBA-SUP-002`: Positive supporter experiences and perceived value are positive antecedents of Supporter Trust. (Based on BT-000022)
-   `C-BEBA-SUP-003`: Supporter Trust is a positive antecedent of supporter loyalty (attitudinal and behavioural). (Based on BL-000010)
-   `C-BEBA-SUP-005`: Supporter Trust partially mediates the relationship between positive supporter experiences and supporter loyalty. (Based on BT-000023)
-   `C-BEBA-SUP-006`: Models of BEBA that include Supporter Trust as a mediator between experiences and loyalty have superior explanatory power to those that omit it. (Based on BE-000055)

## Boundary Conditions
-   **Sector**: The primary evidence base is from the for-profit sector. While the psychological mechanisms are likely to be similar, their specific salience and manifestation in the high-involvement, value-driven nonprofit context require empirical validation.
-   **Stakeholder Group**: The concept is framed for `Supporters` (donors, volunteers), but may apply differently to other groups like beneficiaries or institutional funders.
-   **Involvement Level**: The original evidence stems from low-involvement consumer goods. The role of trust is hypothesized to be even stronger in high-involvement nonprofit decisions.

## Competing Explanations
-   **Direct Reinforcement Model**: A more parsimonious model where supporter loyalty is primarily a direct result of satisfaction (positive reinforcement), without the necessary cognitive mediation of trust. Claim `BT-000023` (partial mediation) explicitly supports that this direct path co-exists with the trust-mediated path.
-   **Reputation-as-Proxy**: Models where a broader `Reputation` construct serves the primary function of reducing uncertainty, with `Trust` being a redundant or fully subsumed concept.

## Operationalisations
The construct is operationalised as a latent variable measured via a multi-item survey scale, capturing its two proposed dimensions (Reliability and Intentions).

## Existing Scales
-   Scales from Delgado-Ballester, Munuera-Alemán, & Yagüe-Guillén (2003) for reliability and intentions are a direct source for the claims reviewed.
-   Other relevant scales include those from Morgan & Hunt (1994) and Doney & Cannon (1997).
-   *Note*: All existing scales require critical review and adaptation for the nonprofit context, focusing on mission-related competence and benevolent intentions towards beneficiaries and society.

## Confidence
Moderate. The theoretical model is well-established in other domains, and its components are highly plausible for the nonprofit sector. Confidence is not "High" because the claims are based on evidence from a different context (for-profit, low-involvement) that requires specific empirical validation for BEBA.

## Review Recommendation
Accept as Candidate for Empirical Testing. The concept and its associated causal model should be formally incorporated into the BEBA theoretical framework as a candidate theory. This justifies a specific model competition in the empirical plan to test a trust-mediated model against a more parsimonious direct-effects model, and to confirm the proposed partial mediation structure.
