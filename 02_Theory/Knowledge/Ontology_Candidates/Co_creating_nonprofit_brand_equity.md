# Concept
Nonprofit Brand Co-Creation

## Canonical Definition
Nonprofit Brand Co-Creation is the dynamic, interactive process through which multiple stakeholder groups, both internal and external, collectively shape the meaning, value, and behavioural potential of a nonprofit brand. This process is driven by the differential experiences and perceptions of stakeholders, and is particularly facilitated by mechanisms such as the transition of individuals between stakeholder roles, which creates reinforcing feedback loops.

## Alternative Definitions
`Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature.` The reviewed claims focus on a specific mechanism (stakeholder transition) rather than offering broad alternative definitions. Commercial literature often defines co-creation more narrowly as customer participation in value creation (e.g., product design), a conceptualisation challenged by the nonprofit context.

## BEBA Layer
-   **Individual:** Involves stakeholder-specific cognitions (awareness, image) and experiences.
-   **Market:** Describes the interaction and feedback loops between different stakeholder groups.
-   **Organisational:** Implies a need for management strategies that facilitate, rather than simply direct, brand meaning.

## Core Claims
1.  **BEBA-C-NEW-01:** The structural components of a BEBA (e.g., Awareness, Image) can form a common cognitive interface shared by diverse stakeholder groups.
2.  **BEBA-C-NEW-02:** The behavioural influence of BEBA components is contingent on the stakeholder's relationship to the organization and their primary mode of experience (direct vs. indirect).
3.  **BEBA-C-NEW-02a (Sub-claim):** For internal stakeholders with direct, experiential knowledge, brand awareness has a greater relative influence on their brand-related cognitions and behaviour.
4.  **BEBA-C-NEW-02b (Sub-claim):** For external stakeholders with indirect, observational knowledge, brand image serves as a primary quality heuristic and has a greater relative influence on their brand-related cognitions and behaviour.
5.  **BEBA-C-NEW-03:** The transition of stakeholders from one role to another (e.g., internal to external) is a key mechanism for brand co-creation, strengthening the BEBA's feedback loops.

## Evidence Base
The primary evidence is derived from a synthesis of claims from a single empirical study in a nonprofit context (military training). The source claims are `NB-000069`, `BC-000033`, `BA-000066`, `BI-000041`, and `NB-000070`.

## Boundary Conditions
-   **Organisational Context:** The evidence originates from a highly structured, mandatory-participation nonprofit. Generalisability to voluntary, member-driven, or donor-funded nonprofits is not yet established.
-   **Stakeholder Definition:** The findings are based on a narrow definition of "internal" (conscripts) and "external" (potential employers), which may not apply to all stakeholder taxonomies (e.g., donors, volunteers, beneficiaries).
-   **Measurement Specificity:** The claims are conditional on the specific modified B2B measurement model used in the source study.

## Competing Explanations
1.  **Pooled Model:** A simpler model assuming the effects of brand equity components (Awareness, Image) are invariant across all stakeholder groups. The evidence suggests this model would have a poorer fit.
2.  **Direct Model Transfer:** The direct application of commercial B2B or B2C brand equity models without modification for the nonprofit context. Claim `NB-000070` provides evidence against this approach.

## Operationalisations
The concept can be operationalised by testing for moderating effects. A recommended approach is a multi-group structural equation model (SEM) where `StakeholderType` (Internal vs. External) is the grouping variable, testing for different path coefficients from `BrandAwareness` and `BrandImage` to a higher-order `BrandEquity` construct.

## Existing Scales
No dedicated scale for "Nonprofit Brand Co-Creation" exists. The source study adapted commercial B2B scales (e.g., Davis et al., 2008) for brand equity components but found them inadequate without modification, highlighting a measurement gap.

## Recommended ConstructLedger Updates
-   Propose adding `StakeholderType` as a key moderating variable for models of `Nonprofit Brand Equity`.
-   Add a note to the `Nonprofit Brand Equity` entry recommending multi-group analysis as a standard validation procedure to test for stakeholder-specific effects.

## Recommended Glossary Entry
**Brand Co-Creation (Nonprofit):** An interactive process where the meaning and value of a nonprofit brand are collectively shaped by the distinct experiences and transitioning roles of both internal and external stakeholders, rather than being solely dictated by the organization.

## Confidence
**Low-to-Medium.** The theoretical mechanism is compelling and integrates several empirical findings. However, the evidence base is currently limited to a single study in a unique nonprofit context. The claims require replication across different types of nonprofit organisations to increase confidence.

## Review Recommendation
1.  **Formalise Claims:** Accept the five `BEBA-C-NEW` claims as official candidate claims within the BEBA theory.
2.  **Inform Empirical Pipeline:** Prioritise a replication study in a different nonprofit context (e.g., a voluntary or donor-based organisation) that explicitly models stakeholder type as a moderator using multi-group SEM.
3.  **Update Ontology:** Accept this concept into the candidate ontology. Refine the definitions of `InternalStakeholder` and `ExternalStakeholder` and consider adding `ExperienceType` as a formal property.
