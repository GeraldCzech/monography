# Concept
Internal BEBA Pathway

## Canonical Definition
The psychological and organizational mechanism by which a nonprofit's strategic brand orientation is translated into performance-relevant staff behaviour. This pathway is a moderated serial mediation process where brand orientation fosters staff emotional brand attachment, which in turn drives staff service involvement. The effectiveness of this pathway is contingent upon contextual factors, notably leadership style.

## Alternative Definitions
-   **Direct Effects Definition**: A simplified view where internal branding is the direct application of brand strategy to influence staff behaviour, without necessary psychological mediators.
-   **Cognitive Definition**: A process focused on knowledge transfer, where internal branding's primary role is to ensure staff understand the brand promise (`Brand Understanding`) rather than forming an emotional connection to it.
-   **Social Identity Definition**: A process where internal branding works by strengthening staff's identification with the organization as a social group, with brand being a key symbol of that group identity.

## BEBA Layer
This concept bridges the **Organisational Layer** and the **Individual Layer**.
-   **Organisational Layer**: `Brand Orientation Behaviour`, `Charismatic Leadership`, `Organizational Performance`.
-   **Individual Layer**: `Staff Emotional Brand Attachment`, `Staff Service Involvement`, `Motivation Internalization`.

## Core Claims
1.  **C-BEBA-Internal-01 (Mechanism):** The internal BEBA pathway operates through a sequence of staff motivation internalization, progressing from affective connection (emotional attachment) to behavioural enactment (service involvement).
2.  **C-BEBA-Internal-02 (Theory):** Self-Determination Theory explains the psychological process by which organisational brand orientation is transformed into self-determined staff behaviour.
3.  **C-BEBA-Internal-03 (Moderation):** The efficiency of the internal BEBA pathway (from brand orientation to staff behaviour) is positively moderated by leadership behaviours, specifically charismatic leadership.
4.  **Full Serial Mediation (IB-000022):** The effect of NPO brand orientation on performance is *fully* mediated by the sequence of staff emotional brand attachment and staff service involvement.

## Boundary Conditions
-   **Leadership Style:** The mechanism's effectiveness is highly contingent on leadership. The tested claims show it is strengthened by `Charismatic Leadership`. In the absence of supportive leadership styles, the pathway from brand orientation to staff behaviour is likely to be weaker or broken.

## Competing Explanations
This moderated serial mediation model competes with several alternatives:
1.  **A Direct Effects Model:** Assumes Brand Orientation directly causes performance outcomes, bypassing psychological states.
2.  **A Simple Mediation Model:** Assumes only one mediator (e.g., emotional attachment) is sufficient to explain the effect.
3.  **An Alternative Mediator Model:** Proposes cognitive mediators (e.g., `Brand Understanding`) are more critical than the affective mediator (`Emotional Attachment`).
4.  **An Alternative Theory Model:** Frames the process using Social Identity Theory rather than Self-Determination Theory.
