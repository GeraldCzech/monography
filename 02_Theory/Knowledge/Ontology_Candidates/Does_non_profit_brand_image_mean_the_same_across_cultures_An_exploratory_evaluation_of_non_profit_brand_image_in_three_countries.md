# Concept

Non-profit Brand Image

## Canonical Definition

A second-order cognitive structure representing a stakeholder's overall perception of a non-profit organisation. It is formed from six first-order dimensions of brand associations: Usefulness, Efficiency, Affect, Dynamism, Reliability, and Ethicality.

## Alternative Definitions

-   **Unidimensional Models:** Simplified models treating brand image as a single, holistic latent variable without specifying its component parts.
-   **Ad-Hoc Models:** Project-specific or context-specific models of brand image that lack a stable, generalisable dimensional structure.
-   **For-Profit Analogues:** Direct applications of corporate brand image models (e.g., Aaker's) that may not fully capture the unique dimensions relevant to the non-profit sector, such as perceived ethicality and efficiency in resource use.

## BEBA Layer

-   **Individual Layer:** The six-dimensional structure itself represents a cognitive architecture within an individual's mind.
-   **Societal Layer:** The measurement properties and expression of this structure are moderated by cultural context, making it a key concept for cross-cultural analysis at the societal level.

## Parent Concepts

-   `Brand Image`
-   `Stakeholder Perception`

-   `NBI: Usefulness` (Perceived utility and effectiveness of the NPO's services)
-   `NBI: Efficiency` (Perception of the NPO as well-managed, non-wasteful, and professional)
-   `NBI: Affect` (Feelings and emotional responses evoked by the NPO brand)
-   `NBI: Dynamism` (Perception of the NPO as modern, innovative, and forward-looking)
-   `NBI: Reliability` (Perception of the NPO as dependable, consistent, and trustworthy)
-   `NBI: Ethicality` (Perception of the NPO as adhering to high moral and ethical standards)

1.  Non-profit Brand Image is a multidimensional, second-order construct composed of six distinct first-order factors. (Based on NP-000002)
2.  The six-dimensional structure of Non-profit Brand Image is conceptually stable across diverse national cultures. (Based on NP-000003)

## Evidence Base

The primary evidence is a multi-group confirmatory factor analysis (MGCFA) across samples from the UK, India, and Bosnia-Herzegovina. The analysis established a stable second-order factor structure and demonstrated configural and metric invariance, but failed to establish scalar invariance.

-   **Source Literature:** SRC-000303, SRC-000211.
-   **Claims:** NP-000002, NP-000003, NP-000004, NP-000005, NP-000006.

## Boundary Conditions

The applicability of this model for comparing *levels* (i.e., mean scores) of brand image is bounded by culture. While the model's structure and the relationships between brand image and other constructs can be compared across cultures where metric invariance holds, direct comparisons of which country has a "better" brand image are methodologically invalid without establishing scalar invariance or using models that account for response bias.

## Competing Explanations

-   The primary competing explanation is that brand image is either a unidimensional construct or that its structure is culture-specific (i.e., it would fail tests of configural invariance). The evidence reviewed refutes these simpler explanations.

## Operationalisations

The concept is operationalised as a second-order latent variable in structural equation models, with six first-order latent variables (the dimensions) measured by specific scale items.

## Existing Scales

A multi-item scale developed and validated cross-culturally in the source literature (SRC-000303, SRC-000211). The scale's properties are established as configurally and metrically invariant across UK, India, and Bosnia-Herzegovina.
