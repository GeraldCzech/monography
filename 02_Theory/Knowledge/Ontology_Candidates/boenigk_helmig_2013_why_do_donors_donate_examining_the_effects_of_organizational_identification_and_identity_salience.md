# Concept

Behavioural Realisation Gap

## Canonical Definition

The **behavioural realisation gap** is the discrepancy between a donor’s proximal behavioural disposition, such as loyalty or intention, and the donor’s realised behaviour, caused by contextual constraints, opportunities, resources, timing, regulation, physical limits, or situational factors.

## Alternative Definitions

- The gap between psychological readiness to act and observable donation behaviour.
- The incomplete translation of donor loyalty into actual donation frequency, volume, or persistence.
- A constraint-sensitive interval between behavioural enablement and behavioural execution.

## Core Claims

1. Donor loyalty should not be treated as equivalent to actual donation behaviour.

2. Satisfaction, donor-nonprofit identification, and donor identity salience may strengthen loyalty, but loyalty may only weakly translate into realised donation behaviour under some conditions.

3. Behavioural realisation is context-dependent and may be constrained by regulatory, physical, logistical, demographic, temporal, or situational factors.

4. BEBA should distinguish between **psychological enablement** and **behavioural realisation**.

5. The behavioural realisation gap provides a candidate explanation for why nonprofit brands may create favourable dispositions without producing proportional behavioural outcomes.

## Boundary Conditions

- Donation type: money donation and blood donation may involve different behavioural structures.
- Physical constraints: blood donation may be limited by biological eligibility or recovery requirements.
- Regulatory constraints: some donation behaviours may have formal limits.
- Resource constraints: money donation may depend on disposable income.
- Opportunity constraints: behaviour may require access, timing, prompts, or organisational solicitation.
- Behavioural outcome type: loyalty may predict stated commitment better than actual frequency, amount, or persistence.
- Demographics: age and income may condition mechanism strength, but evidence remains limited.

## Competing Explanations

1. **Simple loyalty model**

```text
Loyalty → actual donation behaviour
```

Assessment: insufficient where loyalty explains little variance in actual behaviour.

2. **Satisfaction-driven model**

```text
Donor orientation → satisfaction → loyalty → behaviour
```

Assessment: plausible but incomplete; does not fully explain identity mechanisms or behavioural constraints.

3. **Identity-driven model**

```text
Donor-nonprofit identification → donor identity salience → loyalty → behaviour
```

Assessment: supported as a candidate pathway, but still requires a behavioural realisation layer.

4. **Context-first model**

```text
Resources / regulation / opportunity / physical ability → actual behaviour
```

Assessment: explains behaviour independently of loyalty or brand equity in some contexts.

5. **Brand equity mediation model**

```text
Brand equity → loyalty → behaviour
```

Assessment: should not be assumed as universal; behaviour may bypass brand equity through satisfaction, resources, opportunity, or constraints.

## Operationalisations

Possible operational indicators:

- Difference between donor loyalty score and observed donation frequency.
- Difference between stated intention and realised donation behaviour.
- Difference between donor commitment and donation amount.
- Frequency of cases where high-loyalty donors do not donate.
- Constraint-adjusted behavioural conversion rate.
- Self-reported inability to donate despite willingness.
- Regulatory or physical eligibility status.
- Opportunity exposure, such as solicitation frequency or access to donation channels.
- Time since last donation compared with stated willingness to donate again.

## Existing Scales

Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature.

The reviewed claim set distinguishes loyalty from actual behaviour, but it does not provide sufficient evidence for a dedicated behavioural realisation gap scale.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Behavioural Realisation Gap**  
The gap between a person’s readiness or disposition to perform a behaviour and the behaviour that is actually realised. In BEBA, the concept captures the idea that nonprofit brand effectiveness depends not only on creating loyalty, identification, or intention, but also on whether contextual conditions allow those dispositions to become observable behaviour.

## Confidence

**Medium.**

The concept is theoretically important for BEBA and is clearly suggested by the reviewed claim set. However, the evidence appears to rely primarily on one reviewed empirical source across two nonprofit contexts. Additional literature is needed before canonical adoption.

## Review Recommendation

**High-priority ontology candidate.**

Recommended action:

1. Keep as candidate, not canonical.
2. Review additional literature on:
   - intention-behaviour gaps,
   - loyalty-behaviour gaps,
   - donation constraints,
   - blood donation eligibility and regulation,
   - opportunity and solicitation effects,
   - donor income and resource constraints.
3. Use this concept to strengthen BEBA’s distinction between:
   - psychological enablement,
   - behavioural disposition,
   - realised behaviour.
4. Do not collapse donor loyalty into actual donation behaviour in the ontology.
