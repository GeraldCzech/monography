# Concept
Multidimensional Consumer-Based Brand Equity (MBE)

## Canonical Definition
A three-dimensional conceptualization and operationalization of consumer-based brand equity comprising Brand Loyalty, Perceived Quality, and a single, combined factor of Brand Awareness and Brand Associations. This model represents a parsimonious, empirically validated structure for measuring brand equity.

## Alternative Definitions
-   **Aaker's Five-Dimension Model:** A more comprehensive conceptual model including Perceived Quality, Brand Loyalty, Brand Awareness, Brand Associations, and Proprietary Brand Assets.
-   **Keller's Knowledge-Structure Model:** A cognitive model defining CBBE as the differential effect that brand knowledge has on consumer response to the marketing of that brand, focusing on the dimensions of brand awareness and brand image (composed of associations).

## BEBA Layer
-   **Individual Layer:** Defines the structure and measurement of brand equity as a psychological construct residing in the mind of the individual stakeholder.
-   **Societal Layer:** The model's internal structure (i.e., the relative importance of its dimensions) is moderated by societal-level factors like culture.

## Core Claims
-   **BE-000049:** CBBE can be parsimoniously and validly measured as a three-dimensional construct: Brand Loyalty, Perceived Quality, and a combined Brand Awareness/Associations factor.
-   **BE-000050:** Brand Awareness and Brand Associations lack sufficient empirical discriminant validity in survey-based measurement and are better modelled as a single, combined latent factor.
-   **BE-000051:** The 10-item MBE scale demonstrates at least metric invariance across different cultural contexts (e.g., US and Korean samples).
-   **BE-000052:** MBE is a significant positive correlate of key behavioural precursors, including brand attitude and purchase intention.
-   **BE-000053:** The relative importance and contribution of the three MBE dimensions to overall brand equity vary by culture.

## Evidence Base
The primary evidence is the scale development and validation study by Yoo, B., Donthu, N., & Lee, S. (2000), as summarised in `REVIEW-000061`. The study provides multi-sample, cross-cultural evidence for the scale's psychometric properties and predictive validity.

## Boundary Conditions
-   **Culture:** The framework's internal weighting is not universal. The relative importance of dimensions like Perceived Quality vs. Brand Loyalty is moderated by cultural context (e.g., individualistic vs. collectivist cultures).
-   **Measurement Context:** The fusion of Awareness and Associations is an empirical finding from survey-based research; other methods (e.g., experimental, neurological) might establish greater separation.

## Competing Explanations
The MBE model competes with more conceptually comprehensive but less parsimonious measurement models (e.g., Aaker's 5-factor model). The central tension is between conceptual granularity and psychometric robustness/parsimony. MBE prioritizes the latter, challenging the assumption that all theoretically distinct concepts can or should be operationalized as separate latent variables in a single instrument.

## Operationalisations
The concept is operationalized via the 10-item Multidimensional Brand Equity (MBE) scale developed by Yoo, Donthu, & Lee (2000).
