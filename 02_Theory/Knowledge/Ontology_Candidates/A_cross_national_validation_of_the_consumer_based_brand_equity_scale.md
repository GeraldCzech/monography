# Concept

Consumer-Based Brand Equity

## Canonical Definition

Consumer-Based Brand Equity is a multidimensional perception architecture through which individuals hold brand-related cognitive, evaluative, associative, and relational representations that may influence behavioural readiness.

For BEBA, Consumer-Based Brand Equity should be treated as a **source architecture** for nonprofit brand equity, not as a canonical nonprofit construct.

## Alternative Definitions

- Brand equity as a multidimensional construct including awareness, perceived quality, associations, and loyalty.
- Brand equity as the consumer-side value of a brand derived from brand knowledge and brand-related responses.
- Brand equity as a latent perceptual structure that may vary in dimensional stability across contexts.

## BEBA Layer

Primary layer: **Individual layer**

Secondary layer: **Market layer**

Relevant BEBA functions:

- cognitive availability,
- evaluative confidence,
- association formation,
- loyalty or relational predisposition,
- cross-context comparability.

## Core Claims

1. Consumer-Based Brand Equity should be modelled as multidimensional rather than unidimensional.  
   Supported by: CB-000009, CB-000010.

2. Brand associations may require internal differentiation into theoretically meaningful subdimensions.  
   Supported by: BA-000029, OA-000002.

3. Organisational associations can be empirically distinguished from perceived value and brand personality in the reviewed consumer-brand context.  
   Supported by: OA-000002.

4. Measurement invariance is required before Consumer-Based Brand Equity scores are compared across national or cultural samples.  
   Supported by: MI-000005, CB-000010.

5. Commercial Consumer-Based Brand Equity scales should not be transferred directly into nonprofit BEBA without nonprofit-specific validation.  
   Supported by: CB-000011.

## Evidence Base

Reviewed claim set:

- CB-000009: Multidimensional CBBE model demonstrates configural invariance between UK and Spanish consumer samples.
- CB-000010: Factor loadings of multidimensional CBBE scale are equivalent between UK and Spanish samples.
- BA-000029: Brand associations are better represented as a three-factor structure: perceived value, brand personality, organisational associations.
- OA-000002: Organisational associations can be empirically distinguished from perceived value and brand personality.
- MI-000005: Measurement invariance testing is required before CBBE scores are compared across national samples.
- CB-000011: The validated CBBE scale has not been shown to be invariant in nonprofit, service, B2B, or non-Western contexts.

Evidence status: candidate support for measurement architecture, not direct evidence of nonprofit behavioural effectiveness.

## Boundary Conditions

- Evidence comes from consumer product contexts, not nonprofit contexts.
- Evidence is limited to UK and Spanish consumer samples.
- Findings should not be generalised to nonprofit, service, B2B, non-Western, or global South contexts without further validation.
- Configural and metric invariance do not automatically establish scalar invariance or behavioural equivalence.
- The evidence concerns measurement structure, not the behavioural mechanism by which brands enable action under uncertainty.

## Competing Explanations

Consumer-Based Brand Equity does not yet establish superiority over alternative explanations of nonprofit behaviour, including:

- trust-only models,
- legitimacy models,
- commitment models,
- donor identity models,
- perceived impact models,
- social norm models,
- habit or convenience models,
- general attitude or satisfaction models.

BEBA should therefore treat CBBE as a source architecture to be tested against competing nonprofit behaviour explanations.

## Operationalisations

Possible operational dimensions derived from the reviewed CBBE architecture:

- brand awareness,
- perceived quality,
- brand associations,
- brand loyalty,
- perceived value,
- brand personality,
- organisational associations.

For nonprofit BEBA, these dimensions require theoretical adaptation and empirical validation before canonical use.

Potential nonprofit-specific association candidates, requiring separate evidence:

- organisational trust,
- credibility,
- mission fit,
- moral legitimacy,
- perceived impact,
- transparency,
- value congruence.

## Existing Scales

The reviewed claim set concerns a multidimensional Consumer-Based Brand Equity scale validated in UK and Spanish consumer samples.

Current status for BEBA:

- usable as a source measurement architecture,
- not validated as a nonprofit BEBA scale,
- not established as invariant in nonprofit, service, B2B, or non-Western contexts.

## Recommended ConstructLedger Updates

Recommended candidate entries or updates:

1. Add or retain **Consumer-Based Brand Equity** as a source construct, not as the canonical BEBA construct.
2. Mark its measurement status as: commercial-origin, multidimensional, nonprofit validation required.
3. Add measurement-invariance warning for cross-context comparisons.
4. Link CBBE to Nonprofit Brand Equity through an “informs/adapts” relation, not an equivalence relation.
5. Add Organisational Associations as a candidate subdimension of Brand Associations if not already represented.

Suggested relation entries:

```text
Nonprofit Brand Equity adapts Consumer-Based Brand Equity

```

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Consumer-Based Brand Equity:**  
A multidimensional individual-level brand perception architecture comprising cognitive, evaluative, associative, and relational components such as awareness, perceived quality, brand associations, and loyalty. In BEBA, Consumer-Based Brand Equity is treated as a source architecture for nonprofit brand equity, not as a directly transferable nonprofit construct.

## Confidence

**Moderate.**

The evidence supports multidimensional structure and cross-national measurement considerations in the reviewed commercial consumer contexts. Confidence is lower for nonprofit BEBA transfer because nonprofit-specific validation and behavioural-effectiveness evidence are not yet established.

## Review Recommendation

Retain as an **ontology candidate / source concept**.

Do not promote to canonical nonprofit BEBA construct until:

1. nonprofit-specific dimensionality is theoretically justified,
2. nonprofit empirical validation is available,
3. measurement invariance is tested across relevant stakeholder, cultural, national, or organisational contexts,
4. behavioural relevance under uncertainty is evaluated against competing explanations.
