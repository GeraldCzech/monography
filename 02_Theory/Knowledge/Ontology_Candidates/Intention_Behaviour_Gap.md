# Concept

Intention–Behavior Gap

## Canonical Definition

The discrepancy between an individual’s stated intention to perform a charitable behaviour and their subsequent actual performance of that behaviour.

## Alternative Definitions

- The difference between donation intention and actual donation behaviour.
- A post-intentional discontinuity in which favourable motivation or intention does not translate into charitable action.
- In BEBA terms: a behavioural conversion problem showing that brand-related attitudes, trust, satisfaction, commitment, or intentions are insufficient indicators of nonprofit brand effectiveness unless they result in actual behaviour.

## BEBA Layer

Primary layer: **Individual**

Secondary relevance:

- **Brand–Behaviour Interface**: because nonprofit brands may influence whether intentions become behaviour.
- **Societal Layer**: where social norms moderate intention-to-behaviour conversion.

## Evidence Base

Evidence base reviewed: claim set on the **Intention–Behavior Gap** in charitable giving.

The reviewed claims support the concept as a foundational behavioural problem for BEBA. They suggest that nonprofit brand equity should not be inferred from favourable evaluations, trust, satisfaction, commitment, or donation intentions alone. Behavioural effectiveness depends on whether brand-enabled mechanisms help convert motivation, intention, or cue exposure into actual charitable action.

## Boundary Conditions

- **Self-regulatory capacity**: intentions are less likely to become behaviour when individuals fail to initiate, maintain, or complete intended action.
- **Practical friction**: time, effort, resource constraints, payment difficulty, or unclear action paths may reduce conversion.
- **Emotional activation**: acute affective responses may bypass prior intention and trigger immediate giving.
- **Cognitive salience**: donation is more likely when a cause becomes salient at a moment of behavioural opportunity.
- **Normative alignment**: conversion is stronger when both injunctive and descriptive norms support donating.
- **Action opportunity**: cue-driven behaviour requires a feasible behavioural opportunity.

## Competing Explanations

1. **Intention-formation models**  
   These explain why people form donation intentions but are incomplete if they do not explain enactment.

2. **Relational brand equity models**  
   These explain favourable nonprofit relationships through trust, satisfaction, and commitment, but may overstate behavioural effectiveness if actual behaviour is not measured.

3. **Cue-driven behaviour models**  
   These explain donation behaviour through emotion, salience, and situational triggers without requiring prior explicit intention.

4. **Friction-based explanations**  
   These explain the gap through practical, cognitive, motivational, or situational barriers between intention and action.

5. **Normative-context explanations**  
   These explain conversion as conditional on whether social expectations and observed social behaviour support giving.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Intention–Behavior Gap**  
The discrepancy between an individual’s stated intention to perform a charitable behaviour and their subsequent actual performance of that behaviour. In BEBA, the concept marks the difference between motivational readiness and behavioural effectiveness. It supports the view that nonprofit brand equity must be assessed not only by favourable attitudes, trust, satisfaction, commitment, or intentions, but by whether brand-enabled mechanisms help produce actual charitable action.
