# Concept

Brand Orientation

## Canonical Definition

The degree to which an organisation's culture, strategy, and internal processes are systematically aligned and committed to the creation, maintenance, and protection of brand value as a central organising principle. It represents an internal, organisational-level antecedent to external brand performance.

## Alternative Definitions

Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature (`REVIEW-000075`). Further review of foundational brand orientation literature (e.g., Urde, 1999) is required to document alternative conceptualisations.

## Core Claims

1.  **Claim BO-000004:** The internal state of the organisation (i.e., its Brand Orientation) is a critical and necessary condition for external brand success and the effectiveness of its brand architecture.
2.  **Claim (Synthesis) Antecedent Alignment:** The behavioural effectiveness of a BEBA is contingent upon the alignment between its Organisational Layer antecedents (e.g., Brand Orientation, co-creative processes) and its expression at the Individual Layer (e.g., perceived Credibility and Image).

## Evidence Base

-   **Primary:** `REVIEW-000075`

The concept is proposed based on the synthesis of claims from the specified review, which argues that a purely external or perceptual view of brand equity is insufficient.

## Boundary Conditions

Brand Orientation functions as an internal boundary condition for the BEBA model. A low degree of brand orientation within a nonprofit is expected to severely limit or undermine the effectiveness of any externally-focused brand architecture, regardless of its design or communication budget.

## Competing Explanations

This concept challenges purely external, market-based views of brand equity which treat the brand primarily as a perceptual asset in the stakeholder's mind, without explicit reference to the internal organisational culture that creates it. A competing explanation would posit that brand equity can be effectively built through marketing communications alone, irrespective of the organisation's internal cultural state.

## Operationalisations

Not specified in the provided review synthesis. Operationalisation would likely involve assessing the extent to which brand values are embedded in internal training, performance metrics, strategic decision-making, and resource allocation.

## Existing Scales

Not specified in the provided review synthesis. A dedicated literature search is required to identify and vet existing, validated scales for measuring Brand Orientation, particularly within a nonprofit context.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Brand Orientation:** An organisational culture that prioritises the creation and maintenance of brand value. It reflects the degree to which an organisation's strategy, operations, and employee behaviours are aligned with its brand promise, serving as a critical internal foundation for an effective Brand-Enabled Behaviour Architecture (BEBA).
