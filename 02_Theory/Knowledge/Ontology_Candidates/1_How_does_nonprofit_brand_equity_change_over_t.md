# Concept

BEBA System Dynamics

## Canonical Definition

The conceptualisation of BEBA as a dynamic system wherein the behavioural effectiveness of a nonprofit brand is an emergent property. This system is defined by three core characteristics: (a) feedback loops between stakeholder behaviour and brand perceptions, (b) a hierarchical and non-linear integration of brand signals, and (c) model parameters that are contingent on both time and the perceived authenticity of the brand's actions.

## Alternative Definitions

-   **Static Brand Equity Model:** A contrasting view where brand equity is a stable asset, formed by a linear, additive combination of antecedents (e.g., awareness, image), which then unidirectionally influences behaviour.
-   **Recursive Path Model:** A model where brand equity influences behaviour, but which lacks a feedback mechanism where behaviour reciprocally shapes brand equity over time.

## BEBA Layer

-   **Individual Layer:** The concept describes the core psychological mechanisms of signal integration, perception formation, and behavioural reinforcement.
-   **Market Layer:** The concept accounts for macro-level changes over time (temporal dynamics) and the contextual factors (e.g., perceived authenticity of advocacy) that shape the environment for individual behaviour.

## Core Claims

The central claim is SYS-000001, which is substantiated by the following five claims:

-   **SYS-000001 (BEBA System Dynamics):** The behavioural effectiveness of a nonprofit brand is an emergent property of a dynamic system characterized by (a) feedback loops between stakeholder behaviour and brand perceptions, (b) a hierarchical and non-linear integration of brand signals, and (c) parameters that are contingent on both time and the perceived authenticity of brand actions.
-   **NB-000100 (Nonprofit Brand Equity Dynamics):** The influence of brand equity drivers on donor behaviour changes over multi-year periods.
-   **BF-000015 (Behavioural Feedback Loop):** Donating positively reinforces brand equity perceptions, mediated by commitment and brand-self congruence.
-   **ST-000001 (Signalling Theory):** Brand equity evaluation is a nonlinear function of signals, with asymmetric and substitution effects.
-   **BA-000075 (Brand Associations):** Higher-order associations (e.g., values, mission congruence) are stronger predictors of sustained support than lower-order ones (e.g., awareness).
-   **BA-000076 (Brand Activism):** The effect of sociopolitical advocacy on brand equity is moderated by stakeholders' perception of its authenticity.

## Boundary Conditions

-   **Temporal Frame:** The structure and parameter weights of the BEBA system are not static. Explanations of brand effectiveness must specify a temporal frame (e.g., short-term acquisition vs. long-term loyalty).
-   **Perceived Authenticity:** The effects of deliberative brand actions (e.g., brand activism, strategic communication) on brand equity are bounded by stakeholder perceptions of their authenticity.
-   **Context:** Non-linear signalling effects may be more pronounced in specific contexts, such as online environments with high signal volume and velocity.

## Competing Explanations

-   **Simple CBBE Models:** These are challenged by the inclusion of feedback loops and a hierarchical, non-additive structure.
-   **Static Structural Equation Models (SEM):** Cross-sectional SEMs are insufficient as they cannot capture the temporal dynamics or feedback loops inherent to the system.
-   **Composite Index Models:** Models that create a single brand equity score via simple summation are challenged by the evidence for non-linear, asymmetric, and substitution effects among signals.

## Operationalisations

This is a meta-theoretical concept that guides empirical strategy rather than being a single measurable variable. It implies the need for specific methodologies:

-   **Longitudinal Designs:** Panel studies to capture change over time.
-   **Dynamic Models:** Dynamic panel models (e.g., Arellano-Bond), latent growth curve models, or time-series analysis.
-   **Advanced Measurement:** Conjoint analysis or choice experiments to uncover non-linear trade-offs.
-   **Causal Complexity Methods:** Fuzzy-set Qualitative Comparative Analysis (fsQCA) to model signal substitution.
-   **Computational Models:** Agent-based modeling to simulate emergent properties from individual-level rules.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**BEBA System Dynamics:** A theoretical perspective viewing Nonprofit Brand Equity as an emergent property of a complex, adaptive system. It moves beyond static, linear models by emphasizing feedback loops from behaviour to perception, non-linear integration of brand signals, and the time- and context-dependency of relationships.
