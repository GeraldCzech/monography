# Concept

Conditional Dual-Route Nonprofit Brand-Enabled Behaviour

## Canonical Definition

Conditional dual-route nonprofit brand-enabled behaviour refers to the proposition that nonprofit brands influence donor choice intentions through two partially distinct pathways: an evaluative pathway in which brand salience shapes brand attitude and brand attitude shapes intention, and a heuristic accessibility pathway in which brand salience directly supports choice intention by making the nonprofit mentally available and easy to select under uncertainty.

## Alternative Definitions

- Nonprofit brand effectiveness through parallel evaluative and heuristic mechanisms.
- Dual-route donor brand choice architecture.
- Conditional salience–attitude–choice pathway.
- Brand-enabled donor choice under varying involvement and resource conditions.

## Core Claims

1. Brand salience can positively predict donor attitude.
2. Brand attitude can positively predict donation or brand choice intention.
3. Brand salience can directly predict brand choice intention independently of brand attitude.
4. Brand attitude may partially mediate the relationship between brand salience and choice intention.
5. Donor decision involvement may strengthen the attitude-based choice pathway.
6. Low donor decision involvement may strengthen relative reliance on the salience-based pathway.
7. Income may moderate pathway reliance, with higher-income donors relying more on attitude and lower-income donors relying more on salience.
8. Market position may shape salience formation, with prominence and distinctiveness contributing differently for larger versus smaller nonprofit brands.

## Boundary Conditions

- Best supported for new donor acquisition.
- Sector evidence is limited to international aid charities.
- Empirical context is an Australian disaster-relief scenario.
- Outcome evidence concerns intention or choice intention, not observed donation behaviour.
- Route dominance appears conditional on donor decision involvement.
- Income is a plausible but theoretically under-specified moderator.
- Market position may affect whether salience is built through prominence or distinctiveness.
- Generalisability to other nonprofit sectors, cultures, and giving contexts remains uncertain.

## Competing Explanations

### Attitude-Mediated Brand Equity Model

```text
Brand Salience → Brand Attitude → Donation / Choice Intention
```

This explanation treats nonprofit brand equity primarily as an evaluative mechanism. It is supported by salience–attitude and attitude–intention links but is incomplete because brand salience may also have a direct effect on choice intention.

### Heuristic Salience Model

```text
Brand Salience → Choice Intention
```

This explanation treats brand salience as a direct behavioural enabler through mental availability and ease of retrieval. It is especially relevant under low involvement or lower resource conditions but does not explain situations where attitude-based processing dominates.

### Conditional Dual-Route Model

```text
                 → Brand Attitude → Choice Intention
Brand Salience
                 → Choice Intention

Moderators:
- Donor decision involvement
- Income / resource capacity
- Market position / salience route
```

This explanation is most compatible with BEBA because it represents nonprofit brand effectiveness as a conditional architecture of parallel mechanisms rather than a single linear pathway.

## Operationalisations

Potential operationalisations:

- Brand salience as prominence, distinctiveness, recognition, recall, or mental availability.
- Brand attitude as favourable or unfavourable evaluation of the nonprofit brand.
- Donation intention as stated likelihood of donating.
- Brand choice intention as stated likelihood of selecting one nonprofit over alternatives.
- Donor decision involvement as perceived importance, attention, or cognitive engagement in the donation decision.
- Income as donor resource capacity or socioeconomic indicator.
- Market position as relative organizational size, familiarity, category leadership, or competitive position.

Caution: Income should not be interpreted as a psychological mechanism without further theoretical grounding.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Conditional Dual-Route Nonprofit Brand-Enabled Behaviour**  
A proposed BEBA concept describing how nonprofit brands influence donor choice intentions through both an evaluative route, where brand salience affects brand attitude and brand attitude affects intention, and a heuristic route, where brand salience directly supports choice by making the nonprofit mentally available and easy to select. The relative dominance of these routes may depend on donor involvement, income or resource capacity, and nonprofit market position.

## Confidence

Medium.

Rationale:

- The claim set is theoretically coherent and strongly relevant to BEBA.
- Evidence supports multiple behavioural pathways rather than a purely attitudinal model.
- However, the evidence is bounded to intention-level outcomes, new donors, international aid charities, an Australian disaster-relief scenario, and scenario-based decision-making.
- Income moderation requires further theoretical interpretation.
- Observed behavioural validation remains needed.
