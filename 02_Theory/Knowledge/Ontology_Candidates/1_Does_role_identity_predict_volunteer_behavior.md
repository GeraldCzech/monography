# Concept

Volunteer Role Identity

## Canonical Definition

A self-perception derived from an individual's identification with the social role of 'volunteer'. Within the BEBA framework, it is a core psychological construct that becomes more salient and central to a person's overall self-concept through the act of volunteering. It functions as the primary mediating hub through which organisational factors (like brand reputation and support) are translated into sustained pro-organisational behaviour.

## Core Claims

-   Volunteer Role Identity positively predicts volunteering intentions and actual volunteer performance (Claim `VR-000001`, `VR-000002`).
-   Past volunteering behaviour strengthens Volunteer Role Identity, forming a positive reinforcement loop (Claim `VR-000003`).
-   Volunteer Role Identity fully mediates the relationship between key antecedents (e.g., Perceived Organisational Support, Role Clarity) and behavioural outcomes like volunteering intentions (Claim `VR-000004`).
-   The translation of nonprofit brand equity into sustainable volunteer behaviour is primarily mediated by the formation and reinforcement of this identity (Candidate Claim `BEBA-C-001`).

## Evidence Base

This ontology candidate is derived from the synthesis of claims `VR-000001` through `PB-000027` as documented in literature review `REVIEW-000096`.

## Boundary Conditions

The causal link from experiential antecedents like `Perceived Organisational Support` and `Role Clarity` to Volunteer Role Identity may be moderated by the nature of participation. The primary evidence for these links comes from studies on **mandatory volunteers**, and the effect size may differ for intrinsically motivated volunteers.

## Competing Explanations

This identity-centric mechanism competes with traditional, pre-behavioral brand equity models that posit direct causal links from cognitive brand assets (e.g., awareness, image) to behavioural outcomes, without accounting for the powerful role of post-behavioral identity formation and reinforcement.

## Operationalisations

The construct is typically operationalised as a latent variable measured via self-report scales assessing the salience and prominence of the "volunteer" role in an individual's self-concept.
