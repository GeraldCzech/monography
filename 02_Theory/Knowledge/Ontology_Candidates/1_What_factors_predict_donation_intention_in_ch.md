# Concept

Dual-Pathway Architecture

## Canonical Definition

The Dual-Pathway Architecture is the core explanatory framework of BEBA, positing that a nonprofit brand enables pro-social behaviour through at least two distinct psychological pathways operating in parallel. These pathways are:
1.  **Deliberative-Cognitive Pathway**: A rational, belief-based route, consistent with the Theory of Planned Behavior, where brand assets (e.g., Reputation) reduce uncertainty and improve stakeholder assessments (e.g., Trust), which in turn mediate the formation of attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioural control.
2.  **Affective-Identity Pathway**: A more direct, heuristic route where the brand acts as a resource for self-concept needs. It is mediated by mechanisms like Brand Identification and the potential for affective rewards (e.g., "warm glow"), which can drive behaviour independent of, or in addition to, extensive cognitive deliberation.

## Alternative Definitions

Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature.

## BEBA Layer

-   **Individual Layer**: The primary layer where the dual psychological pathways operate.
-   **Relational Layer**: Mediates the pathways through concepts like `Trust` and `Brand Identification`.
-   **Organisational Layer**: The source of antecedents (e.g., `Reputation`) and activators (e.g., `Communication Appeals`) for both pathways.

## Core Claims

The architecture is defined by the following higher-order claims derived from the synthesis in `REVIEW-000101`:
-   **C-BEBA-01 (Dual Pathway)**: The BEBA framework enables pro-social behaviour through at least two distinct psychological pathways: a deliberative-cognitive path (mediated by attitudes, norms, and perceived control) and an affective-identity path (mediated by brand identification).
-   **C-BEBA-02 (Contingent Architecture)**: The specific structure and relative influence of BEBA's pathways are contingent on the target behaviour (e.g., donating money vs. time).
-   **C-BEBA-03 (Mechanism Activation)**: Organisational brand communications are a primary mechanism for activating specific motivational states (e.g., warm glow) within the BEBA framework, thereby influencing the choice and outcome of the behavioural pathway.

## Evidence Base

## Boundary Conditions

-   **Target Behaviour Type**: The architecture's specific configuration and the dominance of one pathway over the other are contingent on the type of pro-social behaviour being explained (e.g., donating time vs. donating money).
-   **Communication Framing**: The activation of the affective-identity pathway may be contingent on the framing of organisational communications (e.g., donor-centric appeals designed to elicit a "warm glow").

## Competing Explanations

The Dual-Pathway Architecture is positioned as a superior explanation against simpler, single-pathway models:
-   **Pure Deliberation Model**: A model where behaviour is explained solely through the deliberative-cognitive pathway (e.g., an extended TPB model).
-   **Pure Identity Model**: A model where behaviour is explained solely through the affective-identity pathway (e.g., direct effect of Brand Identification).

The BEBA empirical strategy should explicitly test the integrated dual-pathway model against these more parsimonious, competing explanations.

## Operationalisations

This is a theoretical framework, not a directly measured construct. It is operationalised as a structural equation model (or similar statistical path model) that specifies and estimates the causal links for both the Deliberative-Cognitive and Affective-Identity pathways simultaneously. This allows for a formal statistical comparison of the pathways' relative influence and the overall model fit against competing single-pathway models.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Dual-Pathway Architecture**: The core framework of BEBA theory, which proposes that a brand enables pro-social behaviour through two parallel psychological routes: a rational, belief-based *Deliberative-Cognitive Pathway* and a heuristic, self-concept-based *Affective-Identity Pathway*.
