# Concept

Social Competence and Trust

## Canonical Definition

A primary dimension of nonprofit brand personality representing a stakeholder's perception of an organisation as being trustworthy, reliable, responsible, and capable in its social mission. It combines the cognitive evaluation of an NPO's effectiveness with an affective evaluation of its integrity and good faith.

## Alternative Definitions

Not sufficiently covered in the reviewed literature. The concept is presented as a novel factor structure. It can be seen as a sector-specific synthesis of more general constructs like `Brand Trust`, `Organizational Credibility`, and the 'Sincerity' and 'Competence' dimensions from for-profit models (Aaker, 1997).

## BEBA Layer

Individual Layer. This is a perceptual construct held by an individual stakeholder that influences their behavioural intentions and actions towards the NPO.

## Core Claims

1.  **Primacy Claim:** 'Social Competence and Trust' is the primary and most influential dimension of nonprofit brand personality. (Derived from `C-NEW-BP-02`)
2.  **Behavioural Enablement Claim:** A stakeholder's perception of an NPO's 'Social Competence and Trust' is a significant positive predictor of their willingness to provide support, such as donating. (Derived from `C-NEW-BP-03` and `BP-000027`)

## Evidence Base

The primary evidence comes from the empirical study reviewed in `REVIEW-000081` ("The_Concept_of_Brand_Personality_as_an_Instrument_for_Advanced_Non_Profit_Branding_An_Empirical_Analysis"). The claims `BP-000026` and `BP-000027` specifically identify this factor and link it to donation intention. The evidence is currently limited to this single study.

## Boundary Conditions

The empirical evidence for this construct is derived exclusively from the German market. Its structure and predictive validity require cross-cultural validation before it can be considered a universal construct for NPOs.

## Competing Explanations

The primary competing explanation is the direct application of for-profit brand personality dimensions (e.g., Aaker, 1997) to the nonprofit sector. In this alternative view, general dimensions like 'Sincerity' or 'Competence' would be the key drivers, rather than this synthesized, sector-specific construct.

## Operationalisations

This construct has been operationalised as a latent factor derived from a factor analysis of brand personality trait ratings for various NPOs.

## Existing Scales

The scale used is the one developed and validated within the source study (`The_Concept_of_Brand_Personality...`). The specific items and psychometric properties must be extracted from the original paper.

## Recommended Glossary Entry

**Social Competence and Trust:** A key dimension of nonprofit brand personality reflecting stakeholder perceptions of an NPO's reliability, integrity, and capability in fulfilling its social mission. It is a primary driver of supportive behaviours like donation.

## Confidence

Provisional. The construct shows strong theoretical coherence and has initial empirical support from a single study. However, confidence is provisional pending replication, cross-cultural validation, and testing against observed behaviour (not just intentions).

## Review Recommendation

Accept as a high-priority candidate for the BEBA ontology. Prioritize this construct for replication in the main empirical pipeline. It represents a core mechanism in the BEBA framework, linking brand perception directly to the enablement of supportive behaviour.
