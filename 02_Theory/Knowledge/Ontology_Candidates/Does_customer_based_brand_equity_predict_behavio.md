# Concept

Brand Trust

## Canonical Definition

A stakeholder's confident belief in a nonprofit brand's reliability, integrity, and benevolence, enabling the stakeholder to accept vulnerability and commit to supportive action, especially under uncertainty.

## Alternative Definitions

- "The willingness of a stakeholder to rely on a nonprofit's abilities to perform its stated function." (Adapted from Moorman, Zaltman, & Deshpande, 1992)
- "A psychological state comprising the intention to accept vulnerability based upon positive expectations of the intentions or behavior of another." (Rousseau et al., 1998)

## Core Claims

- `BT-000033`: Brand trust is a key driver of commitment, and its importance is amplified under conditions of high perceived uncertainty regarding a nonprofit's effectiveness.
- `BT-000034`: Brand trust acts as a central mediator between other brand perceptions (e.g., personality, anthropomorphism) and supportive intentions.
- `BEBA-C-012 (Candidate)`: Brand trust acts as a primary mediator, channeling the effects of other brand perceptions (e.g., awareness, personality) onto supportive intentions.

## Evidence Base

- The core claims are derived from the synthesis of claims in `REVIEW-000102`, which integrates findings related to claims `NB-000106`, `BT-000033`, `BP-000029`, `BA-000077`, and `BT-000034`.
- The central mediating role is a key theoretical implication from this synthesis, proposed for formal empirical testing.

## Boundary Conditions

- **Perceived Uncertainty**: The causal influence of Brand Trust on supportive intentions is strengthened as a stakeholder's perceived uncertainty about a nonprofit's operations or impact increases.

## Competing Explanations

- **Direct Effects Model**: A theoretical model where Brand Trust is conceptualized as one of several brand assets (alongside Brand Awareness, Brand Personality, etc.) that have independent, parallel effects on supportive intentions. This contrasts with the BEBA proposition of a Trust-Mediated model where trust is a central, serial mediator.

## Operationalisations

- Measurement is typically conducted via multi-item psychometric scales administered in stakeholder surveys.
- Items are designed to capture stakeholder perceptions of the brand's reliability (ability), integrity, and benevolence.

## Existing Scales

- Scales from the commercial marketing literature, such as those by Chaudhuri and Holbrook (2001) or Delgado-Ballester, Munuera-Alemán, & Yagüe-Guillén (2003), could be adapted.
- Nonprofit-specific scales, such as those related to donor trust (e.g., Sargeant & Lee, 2004), should be reviewed for direct applicability or adaptation.

## Recommended ConstructLedger Updates

- Update the `Role` of the `BrandTrust` construct from `Predictor` to `Primary Mediator`.
- Add a note to `BrandTrust` explicitly stating its effect is moderated by `PerceivedUncertainty`.
- In the model mapping section, specify `BrandAwareness` and `BrandPersonality` as key upstream antecedents to `BrandTrust`.
