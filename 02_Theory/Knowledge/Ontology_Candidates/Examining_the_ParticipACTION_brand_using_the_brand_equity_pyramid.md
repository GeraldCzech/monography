# Concept
Nonprofit Brand Equity Pyramid

## Canonical Definition
A sequential, hierarchical model that posits the development of nonprofit brand equity as a structured psychological progression through four distinct stages: (1) establishing Brand Identity (Salience), (2) creating Brand Meaning (Performance & Imagery), (3) eliciting positive Brand Responses (Judgements & Feelings), and (4) forging Brand Resonance (an intense, active loyalty relationship). The model's core principle is that achieving a higher stage is contingent upon successfully completing the preceding one, with Brand Resonance being the most proximate psychological driver of brand-enabled behaviours.

## Alternative Definitions
This is a direct application of Keller's (2001) Customer-Based Brand Equity (CBBE) model, or "Brand Equity Pyramid," to the nonprofit context. The core logic and structure are identical, but the interpretation of constructs (e.g., "performance" or "behaviour") is adapted for the nonprofit mission.

## BEBA Layer
-   **Organisational Layer:** Provides a strategic framework for brand building and resource allocation.
-   **Individual Layer:** Describes the cognitive and affective sequence a stakeholder progresses through to form a strong brand relationship. This represents the core psychological architecture.
-   **Behavioural Layer:** The final stage, Brand Resonance, directly links the individual-level psychological state to stakeholder behavioural intentions (e.g., donating, volunteering, advocacy).

## Core Claims
1.  **C-BEBA-SEQ-01:** The influence of a nonprofit brand on stakeholder behavioural intention is mediated by a sequential psychological progression from (1) Identity Salience, to (2) Brand Meaning, to (3) Positive Brand Responses, to (4) Brand Resonance.
2.  **C-BEBA-MED-01:** The effect of Brand Identity on Brand Resonance is fully mediated by the intervening stages of Brand Meaning and Brand Responses, making the complete architectural sequence a necessary condition for building a behaviourally effective brand relationship.
3.  **C-BEBA-LINK-01:** In the nonprofit context, Brand Resonance is a direct and positive predictor of stakeholder intentions to adopt brand-advocated behaviours.

## Evidence Base
The claims are derived from an empirical study of the Canadian health-promotion nonprofit, ParticipACTION. The findings are documented in claims BE-000056, BI-000043, BM-000005, BR-000012, BR-000013, and BE-000057, which collectively validate the sequential and mediating structure of the pyramid in that context.

## Boundary Conditions
-   **Context:** The evidence originates from a single, well-established national brand in the **health-promotion** sector. Generalisability to other sectors (e.g., humanitarian aid, advocacy, arts) requires further evidence.
-   **Brand Maturity:** The model was tested on a mature brand. Its applicability to new or low-awareness nonprofits may differ.
-   **Data Structure:** The evidence is based on **cross-sectional survey data**, which supports the hypothesised sequence but does not definitively prove causation over time.
-   **Outcome Measure:** The model predicts **self-reported behavioural intention**, not observed behaviour. The intention-behaviour gap is a potential moderator.

## Competing Explanations
-   **Direct Effect Models:** These models posit a direct link from lower-level constructs (e.g., Brand Awareness) to behavioural outcomes, bypassing the intermediate stages. The evidence of full mediation directly challenges this view.
-   **Independent Contributor Models:** These models treat brand equity dimensions (e.g., imagery, perceived quality) as parallel, independent predictors of outcomes, rather than as components in a structured, necessary sequence.

## Operationalisations
The concept is operationalised as a structural equation model (SEM) where the four stages are specified as latent variables in a sequential, causal chain. Each stage is measured by survey items corresponding to its constituent blocks (e.g., Salience, Performance, Imagery, Judgements, Feelings, Resonance).

## Existing Scales
Validated scales exist for the constituent components of the CBBE pyramid, developed and refined by Keller and subsequent researchers. The ParticipACTION study adapted these scales for the specific nonprofit context.

## Recommended Glossary Entry
**Nonprofit Brand Equity Pyramid:** An application of Keller's CBBE model to nonprofits, describing brand building as a four-stage sequential process (Identity -> Meaning -> Responses -> Resonance). It functions as a psychological architecture where stakeholder progression through the stages builds brand equity, culminating in a resonant relationship that drives mission-supporting behaviours.

## Confidence
Moderate. The evidence from the single source study is strong and internally consistent, providing a robust validation in one specific context. However, confidence in its universal applicability across the entire nonprofit sector is moderated by the noted boundary conditions.

## Review Recommendation
Recommend for formal review and integration into the core BEBA theory as a primary, empirically-supported pathway. The review should explicitly consider the documented boundary conditions and position this model as a benchmark for comparison against competing explanations.
