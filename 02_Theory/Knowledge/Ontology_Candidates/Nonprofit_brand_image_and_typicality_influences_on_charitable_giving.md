# Concept
Nonprofit Brand Image

## Canonical Definition
Based on the evidence reviewed (REVIEW-000074), Nonprofit Brand Image is a multidimensional construct representing an individual's mental representation of a nonprofit organization, composed of four distinct dimensions: Usefulness, Efficiency, Affect, and Dynamism.

## Core Claims
- **NB-000077:** Nonprofit Brand Image is a multidimensional construct composed of Usefulness, Efficiency, Affect, and Dynamism.
- **NB-000078:** A more positive Nonprofit Brand Image positively influences an individual's intention to donate money.
- **NB-000079:** A more positive Nonprofit Brand Image positively influences an individual's intention to donate time.
- **NB-000080:** The 'Efficiency' dimension of Nonprofit Brand Image has a stronger positive effect on the intention to donate money than other dimensions.
- **NB-000081:** The 'Affect' dimension of Nonprofit Brand Image has a stronger positive effect on the intention to donate time than other dimensions.

## Evidence Base
The claims are derived from `REVIEW-000074`, which summarises empirical findings from a study of large, well-known humanitarian aid charities. The evidence supports a nuanced model where different components of brand image differentially predict different types of supportive behaviour.

## Boundary Conditions
- **Contextual:** The evidence originates from a study in a French context involving large, well-known humanitarian aid organizations. Generalizability to smaller, niche, or non-humanitarian NPOs, or to different cultural contexts, requires further investigation.
- **Methodological:** The evidence is based on self-reported *behavioural intentions* rather than observed behaviours. The intention-behaviour gap is a known limitation.

## Competing Explanations
- **Monolithic Brand Equity Models:** This multidimensional view with behaviour-specific pathways challenges simpler models that treat brand equity or brand image as a single, unidimensional construct with a uniform effect on all supportive behaviours.
- **Purely Affect-driven Models:** The finding that 'Efficiency' (a more rational/cognitive dimension) is the strongest predictor of financial donation intention challenges models that posit that nonprofit support is driven primarily by affect or "warm glow".

## Operationalisations
The concept is operationalised as a multidimensional, reflective latent construct measured via a multi-item scale capturing its four constituent dimensions.

## Recommended Glossary Entry
**Nonprofit Brand Image:** An individual's overall perception of a nonprofit brand, comprising a network of associations across four key dimensions: its perceived usefulness to society (Usefulness), its operational and financial effectiveness (Efficiency), the feelings it evokes (Affect), and its perceived modernity and innovativeness (Dynamism).

## Confidence
**Medium-High.** The evidence for the construct's structure and its differential effects on intentions is strong within the context of the original study. Confidence is moderated by the need to test generalizability across different types of nonprofits and cultural contexts.

## Review Recommendation
**Accept and Integrate.** This concept is critical for advancing the BEBA theory. It provides the empirical and conceptual basis for the "behaviour-specific pathways" principle, moving the BEBA from a general "Brand -> Behaviour" model to a more precise "Specific Brand Component -> Specific Behaviour" architecture. Formal adoption is recommended. An ADR should be considered to formally integrate the "behaviour-specific pathways" principle into the core BEBA theory.
