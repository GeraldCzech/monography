# Concept
Perceptual Mediation of Brand Orientation

## Canonical Definition
A causal mechanism wherein the effect of a stakeholder's perception of a nonprofit's brand orientation (PBO) on the personal benefits they perceive is not direct, but is fully transmitted through their perception of the organization's market orientation (PMO). In this model, the brand shapes how stakeholders interpret organizational behaviour, and it is this interpreted behaviour that directly generates value.

## Alternative Definitions
-   The indirect effect of perceived brand strategy on stakeholder value.
-   The process by which market-oriented behaviours realize the value promised by the brand in the stakeholder's mind.
-   A specific "Brand-to-Behaviour-to-Benefit" causal chain.

## BEBA Layer
Primarily operates at the **Individual Layer**, as it describes a cognitive and perceptual process within a stakeholder. It has direct implications for the **Organizational Layer**, as it models the perceptual consequences of strategic choices (Brand and Market Orientation).

## Core Claims
-   A stakeholder's perception of an organization's brand orientation is a positive and significant predictor of their perception of its market orientation. (PM-000003)
-   A stakeholder's perception of an organization's market orientation is a positive and significant predictor of the personal benefits they perceive. (PM-000004)
-   There is no significant direct effect of perceived brand orientation on perceived stakeholder benefits when perceived market orientation is included in the model. (PB-000024)
-   The relationship between perceived brand orientation and perceived stakeholder benefits is fully mediated by perceived market orientation. (PB-000025)

## Evidence Base
The claims are derived from a single empirical study on Australian nonprofit church organizations, as synthesized in `REVIEW-000088`. The evidence is based on claims PM-000003, PM-000004, PB-000024, PB-000025, and PB-000026.

## Boundary Conditions
-   **Context:** Findings are from Australian church organizations and may not generalize to other nonprofit sub-sectors (e.g., humanitarian, healthcare, advocacy).
-   **Stakeholder Group:** The study focused exclusively on members. The mechanism may differ for donors, volunteers, or the general public.
-   **Value Type:** Perceived benefits were operationalized as spiritual and social benefits. The model may not hold for organizations delivering primarily utilitarian benefits.
-   **Sampling:** The use of convenience sampling limits statistical generalizability.

## Competing Explanations
This mediation model is proposed as superior to two competing explanations:
1.  **Direct Effects Model:** A model where `Perceived Brand Orientation` has a direct, unmediated effect on `Perceived Stakeholder Benefits`. This is challenged by evidence of a non-significant direct path (PB-000024).
2.  **Independent Predictors Model:** A model where `Perceived Brand Orientation` and `Perceived Market Orientation` are uncorrelated, independent predictors of benefits. This is challenged by the significant causal path from PBO to PMO (PM-000003).

## Operationalisations
This causal mechanism is not operationalized as a single variable. It is tested as a structural model using techniques like Structural Equation Modeling (SEM), where the constituent constructs (`Perceived Brand Orientation`, `Perceived Market Orientation`, `Perceived Stakeholder Benefits`) are measured via scales and the causal paths between them are estimated.

## Existing Scales
The constituent constructs have validated scales. The source literature reviewed in `REVIEW-000088` validates perceptual scales for `Perceived Brand Orientation` and `Perceived Market Orientation` from the stakeholder's viewpoint (PB-000026). The specific items should be sourced from the original study.

## Recommended ConstructLedger Updates
1.  **Add Construct:** `Perceived Brand Orientation`.
    -   **Definition:** An individual-level construct representing a stakeholder's belief about the extent to which an organization prioritizes and strategically manages its brand.
    -   **Note:** Distinguish from the organizational-level `Brand Orientation`.
2.  **Add Construct:** `Perceived Market Orientation`.
    -   **Definition:** An individual-level construct representing a stakeholder's belief about the extent to which an organization understands and responds to their needs.
    -   **Note:** Distinguish from the organizational-level `Market Orientation`.

## Recommended Glossary Entry
**Perceptual Mediation of Brand Orientation:** A causal process where a nonprofit's brand strategy influences stakeholder value indirectly, by first shaping how stakeholders perceive the organization's behaviours (such as its market orientation).

## Confidence
**Medium.** The proposed mechanism is theoretically coherent with the BEBA framework and is supported by a single, targeted empirical study. Confidence is not high because the evidence base is currently limited to one study in a specific nonprofit context.

## Review Recommendation
Recommend for inclusion in the BEBA theoretical map as a primary candidate mechanism for explaining how brand equity is generated. Acceptance should be conditional on:
1.  Acknowledging the strong boundary conditions.
2.  Triggering the creation of ontology candidate files for `Perceived Market Orientation` and `Perceived Brand Orientation` as distinct, individual-level constructs.
3.  Prioritizing this mediation model for future empirical testing and replication in different nonprofit contexts and with different stakeholder groups.
