# Concept

Brand Factors and Donation Intention in Nonprofits and Charities

## Canonical Definition

A candidate BEBA concept describing how nonprofit brand-related perceptions and cues influence donation intention by reducing donor uncertainty and enabling supportive behavioural readiness through mechanisms such as trust, attitude formation, perceived authenticity, identity congruence, salience, and credibility.

## Alternative Definitions

- Nonprofit brand-enabled donation intention mechanism.
- Brand-based uncertainty reduction for charitable giving intention.
- Individual-level nonprofit brand perception pathway to supportive behavioural readiness.

## BEBA Layer

Primary: Individual Layer  
Secondary: Organisational Layer

Cross-layer logic:

```text
Organisational signals / brand architecture
→ individual perception and uncertainty reduction
→ trust, attitude, salience, identification
→ supportive behavioural intention
```

## Core Claims

1. Nonprofit brand perceptions can increase donation intention, but the relationship is unlikely to be a simple direct-effect pathway.

2. Trust is a central mechanism through which nonprofit brand perceptions become behaviourally relevant under uncertainty.

3. Positive attitudes toward the charity mediate the relationship between brand perceptions and donation intention.

4. Brand personality and donor self-concept congruence may increase donation intention by making support personally meaningful.

5. Brand familiarity increases salience and consideration but is insufficient for donation intention without trust, credibility, or favourable associations.

6. Perceived brand authenticity may strengthen supportive behavioural readiness by signalling credibility, moral consistency, or value alignment.

7. Negative organisational reputation can weaken or suppress the positive behavioural effects of nonprofit brand factors.

8. Different supportive behaviours, such as donating, volunteering, and referral, may depend on different brand dimensions.

9. Donor involvement moderates brand cue processing, with low-involvement donors more likely to rely on heuristic cues and high-involvement donors more likely to rely on evaluative attitudes.

10. The reviewed evidence mainly concerns donation intention, not verified longitudinal donation behaviour.

## Boundary Conditions

- Behavioural outcome type: donation intention, actual donation, volunteering, referral, advocacy, and repeat support should not be treated as equivalent without justification.
- Donor involvement: cue-processing routes may differ between low- and high-involvement donors.
- Organisational reputation: negative reputation or scandal information may override positive brand associations.
- Trust availability: familiarity and image may be insufficient where trust is absent.
- Evidence scope: current support is stronger for intention than for verified behaviour.

## Competing Explanations

1. Direct Brand Effect Model

```text
Brand Image → Donation Intention
```

Partially supported but likely underspecified.

2. Trust-Mediated BEBA Model

```text
Brand Perceptions → Trust → Donation Intention
```

Highly consistent with BEBA’s uncertainty-reduction premise.

3. Attitude-Mediated Model

```text
Brand Perceptions → Attitude Toward the Charity → Donation Intention
```

Supported as a plausible cognition-affect-intention pathway.

4. Dual-Mediation Model

```text
Brand Perceptions → Trust and/or Attitude → Donation Intention
```

Plausible, but the reviewed material does not settle the ordering between trust and attitude.

5. Identity-Congruence Model

```text
Brand Personality / Symbolic Meaning
→ Donor-Brand Congruence
→ Donation Intention
```

Relevant where support behaviour is value-expressive.

6. Familiarity-Heuristic Model

```text
Brand Familiarity
→ Salience / Consideration
→ Perceived Credibility
→ Donation Intention
```

Supported only with qualification; familiarity should not be treated as sufficient.

7. Reputation-Moderated Model

```text
Brand Perceptions → Trust / Attitude → Donation Intention
                 moderated by Organisational Reputation
```

Relevant where scandal, low effectiveness, or integrity concerns are salient.

8. Behaviour-Specific Model

```text
Brand Dimensions → Behaviour-Specific Support Intention
```

Required if donation, volunteering, referral, and advocacy are modelled separately.
