# Concept

ICNPO Group

## Canonical Definition

An ICNPO Group is a category within the International Classification of Nonprofit Organizations (ICNPO) that classifies a nonprofit organisation based on its primary economic activity. The system is designed to facilitate international comparative analysis by providing a standardized, activity-based framework that transcends national legal and administrative differences (Salamon & Anheier, 1996).

## Alternative Definitions

Not applicable. The concept refers to a specific, defined classification system. However, there are alternative *systems* for classifying nonprofit organisations:

-   **Standard Industrial Classifications (SIC/ISIC/NACE):** These systems are designed for the business sector and often fail to capture the specific activities and structures of nonprofit organisations.
-   **National Taxonomies (e.g., NTEE in the US):** These systems are often highly detailed and useful within a single country but lack international comparability.
-   **Legal Status Classifications:** Systems based on legal definitions (e.g., "charity," "association," "foundation") vary significantly across countries and are poor proxies for organisational function.

## BEBA Layer

-   **Societal Layer:** Provides the framework for situating and comparing the nonprofit sector across different national and cultural contexts.
-   **Organisational Layer:** Serves as a primary characteristic of a nonprofit organisation, defining its operational environment, competitive set, and functional purpose.

## Child Concepts

The ICNPO system is hierarchical. The 12 major groups are parent concepts to more specific subgroups. For example:
-   `ICNPO Group 01: Culture and Recreation` is the parent to `ICNPO Subgroup 1100: Media and communications`, `ICNPO Subgroup 1200: Visual arts, architecture, ceramic arts`, etc.

## Related Concepts

-   **Organisation:** An `Organisation` is classified by one primary `ICNPO Group`.
-   **Operational Environment:** The `ICNPO Group` is a key determinant of an organisation's operational environment.
-   **Behavioural Arena:** Different `ICNPO Groups` can be conceptualised as distinct "behavioural arenas" with unique stakeholder expectations and behavioural norms.

## Core Claims

-   A classification system based on primary economic activity (like ICNPO) provides a more robust basis for international comparative research than systems based on legal status, funding sources, or standard industrial codes.
-   The nonprofit sector is highly heterogeneous; treating it as a monolithic entity leads to invalid theoretical generalizations and flawed empirical analysis.
-   The functional relationship between brand equity and stakeholder behaviour is moderated by the nonprofit's `ICNPO Group`.
-   The variance in brand-enabled behaviour across the nonprofit sector can be partially explained by an organisation's `ICNPO Group`.

## Evidence Base

The primary evidence base is the extensive theoretical and empirical work from the Johns Hopkins Comparative Nonprofit Sector Project, which developed and validated the ICNPO system across dozens of countries (e.g., Salamon & Anheier, 1992, 1996, 1997). The claims reviewed (SC-000011 to SC-000016) synthesize this literature.

## Boundary Conditions

-   The classification's validity depends on the accurate identification of an organisation's *primary* activity, which can be ambiguous for large, multi-purpose organisations.
-   The ICNPO system, while international, may not capture unique institutional forms present only in specific national contexts.
-   The ICNPO groups themselves act as boundary conditions for the BEBA theory; propositions and models may not apply uniformly across all 12 groups.

## Competing Explanations

-   **Monolithic Sector View:** The assumption that the nonprofit sector is homogenous enough that findings can be generalized without segmentation.
-   **Industrial Classification Sufficiency:** The argument that standard industrial classifications (ISIC/NACE) are adequate for classifying NPOs.
-   **National System Primacy:** The view that nationally-specific systems (like NTEE) are superior due to their detail, at the cost of international comparability.

## Operationalisations

The `ICNPO Group` is operationalised as a categorical variable with 12 levels corresponding to the major groups of the ICNPO manual. Assignment is based on a qualitative coding of an organisation's mission statement, program descriptions, and financial reports to determine its primary economic activity.

## Confidence

High. The ICNPO is the de facto standard for comparative nonprofit research, and its theoretical underpinnings are well-established. Its utility for structuring analysis within the BEBA framework is strongly supported by the reviewed claims.
